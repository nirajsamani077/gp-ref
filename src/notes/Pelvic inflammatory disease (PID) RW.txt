Pelvic inflammatory disease (PID) RW

Painful condition associated with acute systemic ascending infection of womb tubes ovaries risking TO abscess, hydro/pyosalpinx, peritonitis & longer term comps (chronic pelvic pain, infertility, risk of ectopic)
• Caused almost always by STI (chlamydia, gonorrhoea, TV & mycoplasma genitalium rising cause, other pathogens eg gardnerella & anaerobes can contribute too)
• Non-STI PID can occur (IUD or no cause found)

Consider if STI RFs (<25, multiple partners/recent change, no use of barrier, previous STI)

Px = Abdo pain (deep dysparenuria, dysmenorrhoea), purulent vaginal discharge abnormal bleeding (IMB PCB) & fever/systemic
• On spec swab ECS/HVS & bimanual should show cervical excitation (motion tenderness) 
• RULE OUT PREGNANCY & UTI with urine (first catch urine or self-taken LVS NAAT also alt ECS)
• If systemically unwell or severe = admit Gynae 

Treatment  (if not admitting)
• Don’t delay Abx if suspecting or wait for swabs 
• Treat triple broad spectrum ABx 
   - IM Cef 1g STAT + Doxy 100mg BD & Metro 400mg BD 14d for both (alt = Oflox 400mg BD + Metro 400mg BD 14d , or Moxiflox 400mg BD 14d which is best treatment for mycoplasma related PID but quinolone risk tendon rupture/hepatotoxic)
   - Review all after 3d (swabs should be back) & safety net, refer GUM in meanwhile for work up contact tracing (6m partners), partner notification & abstain during treatment 
   - Leave any IUD in situ unless lack of response after 3d, balance risk of pregnancy and need for emergency contraception, probably admit/advice 
   - Further review after 1mo to ensure compliance & would need TOC if gonorrhoea or mycoplasma (although GUM should sort) .
