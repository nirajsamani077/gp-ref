Hernias 

Inguinal - superomedial to pubic tubercle, in infants congenital indirect hernia due to patent processus vaginalis, needs urgent repair as high risk of incarceration.

Femoral - inferolateral, women, higher risk of strangulation so need urgent repair

Umbilical - symmetrical bulge below umbillicus, often in infant, usually resolve by 4yo, rare comps 

Paraumbillical - asymmetrical bulge above/below 

Epigastric - midline between umbillicus & sternum, obese, raised pressures 

Spigellian - lateral ventral, rare in older 

Other - incisional, obturator, richter

Inguinal hernias should generally be referred for routine mesh repair even if asymptomatic, unilateral generally open, bilateral/recurrent generally laparoscopic (hernia truss if not fit for surgery) - usually 2 weeks off manual work (longer if open)
