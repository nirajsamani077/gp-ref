Dysuria 

Top causes
• Lower UTI (cystitis) more than upper 
• STI (urethritis)
• Vulvovaginitis/balanitis (irritant/Candida) - “peri-urethral” 
• Urethral stricture or stone 
• Chronic pain (interstitial cystitis/endometriosis in women, prostatitis in men)

5 ready reckoners 
• Systemically unwell?
• Recurrent problem?
• Discharge (PU/PV?)?
• Visible genital problem?
• Positive MSU?

History 
• Single episode or recurrent, trigger
• Haematuria, LUTS 
• STI symptoms (discharge/lumps/bumps), LMP 
• Itch or genital irritation, washing products?
• Systemic (fever, loin pains)

Examine (+ Ix)
• Genital & urine dip/MSU, STI screen/ Umbrella GUM clinic if suspecting STI

Tips 
• In F>65 can Dx cystitis without urinalysis, in M with any >1 UTI should investigate as rare 
• In kids recurrent unexplained dysuria can be NAI, in elderly same can be bladder tumour
• If sterile Pyuria think peri-urethral, STI, malignancy, TB  & if haematuria persists after treatment of UTI refer 
• Prostatitis sudden obstructive LUTS, systemic upset needs abx 2 weeks
