Peripheral vascular disease (PVD) RW

Reduced peripheral (length dependent LL) circulation causing 
• Intermittent claudication - predictable pain (leg buttock hip) with walking distance, DDx spinal stenosis improved by leaning forward (eg walking uphill) = examine & refer vascular 
• Critical limb ischaemia -Night/rest pain, hanging foot over edge of bed, tissue loss (arterial ulcers, gangrene, necrosis), insidious onset = examine & refer vascular same day (high risk of amputation)
• Acute limb ischaemia - 6 Ps - Pain, Pallor, Pulseless, Paralysis, Paraesthesia, Perishingly cold = admit vascular time critical 

Biggest RFs - smoking & DM

Examining vascular 
• Absent or weak DP or PT pulses (Doppler if got)
• Cold discoloured skin (not if sitting usually pink)
• Buergers test - Lie flat, elevate leg foot will turn white & lower foot below bed level will turn red due to reactive hyperaemia (sunset foot) = critical ischaemia 
• Arterial ulcers - small punched out pressure pts 
• Examination generally poor pick up of PVD 

ABPI 
• Lying flat, check BP manual both arms & PT/DP
• Highest ankle SBP/highest arm SBP = ABPI 
   - >1 = PVD unlikely but DM/age can calcify!
   - <0.8 = Moderate PVD, can’t use stockings 
   - <0.5 = Critical PVD 
 
Management 
• Refer vascular - Duplex USS, angio/stent/bypass
• Secondary prevention - Stop smoking & manage CV RFs, atorva 80mg, clopidogrel 75mg 
• Supervised exercise programme 3mo to extend walking distance
• If declines surgery can offer peripheral artery dilators (naftidrofuryl oxalate) 100-200mg TDS & stop after 6mo if no symptomatic benefit 

Main risk = CV event (MI or stroke) so secondary prevention as above
