6-8 week check GP notebook

History 
• Any initial concerns 
• Birth / pregnancy hx 
• Feeding - hv involvement
• Development - smile, follow, jump to loud noise
• Parent expectations 

Examination screening questions - FHx 
• Hips, heart, eyes, testes

Eyes 
• Congenital cataracts /retinoblastoma - light reflection symmetrical following, red reflex (no shadows) in dark, if abnormal urgent 2WW ophthalmology ref as immediate surgery can prevent irreversible blindness ( parents colour same), RFs FHx, TORCH, VZV, HSV, SNHL, FAS, prem and FHx BL CC refer even if red reflex 
• Obvious lid iris sclera pupil issues 
• Birthmarks - haemangioma, extensive port wine stain may suggest glaucoma 

Head 
• Plot HC
• Ant font feel 
• Ears normal 
• Palate visual or finger 

Tone and reflex (hold baby)
• Head control 
• Stepping reflex - hold with feet touching couch 
• Startle reflex - as laying down on couch 

Heart - 50% only detected in pregnancy 
• RFs consider SSRI, T21, TORCH, mat disease, or baby cyanosis, apnea, FTT
• Inspect - iWOB
• Palpate - femoral pulses (if can’t COA), CRT, apex (Dextro), heaves/thrills 
• Ausc - 5 areas usuals + midscap for COA, if any murmur refer speed depends on clinical, benign usually short soft systolic sternal border no associated 

Hips 
• RFs - FHx DDH, breech at 36/40 refer any even if normal exam 
• Ask any issues with hips when changing nappy 
• Check leg length and knees same level when flexed and smooth movement when flexing hip 
• Ppv of AS creases is low 
• Barlow to dislocate hip - stabilise pelvis with right hand, left hand thumb on thigh and fingertip on trochanter, apply backward pressure 
• Ortolani to relocate dislocated hip
