Diabetes - additional points RW

Other types of DM /secondary 
• Polygenic (95%) - T1/T2DM or LADA = latent autoimmune diabetes in adults (ie T1DM in adults or type 1.5 diabetes) in thin person with quick insulin deficiency needing insulin & GAD (or other islet cell) antibodies present
• Monogenic (5%) - MODY = maturity onset diabetes of young (ie T2DM in kids), AD usually with strong FHx, may not be overweight. Mitochondrial disease also mimics with maternal FHx and SNHL 
• Steroid-induced diabetes 
• Pancreatic endocrine dysfunction (CF, chronic pancreatitis)

C-peptide 
• Can distinguish T1 & T2DM (low in T1 due to lack of insulin production & cofactor) but in early stages (honeymoon) might be high 
• Would be high also in insulinoma or Cushings, and low in severe liver disease or Addisons 

T2DM subtypes  
• Mild obesity related - early onset, obese
• Mild old age related - late onset, less comps, not warrant intensive Rx due to lifetime risk of comps 
• Severe insulin resistant - obese with fatty liver
• Severe insulin deficient - T1DM phenotype with negative GAD, but early need for insulin & early retinopathy & nephropathy 

Screening for T2DM 
• Finding high risk groups based on conditions, BMI, BAME, FHx and checking HbA1C, & even if not in prediabetic range = rescreen every 3-5y and offer brief advice about risk & lifestyle 
• Diabetes Risk Score specific tool for this 

Diabetes diagnosis 
• T1DM = Random capillary glucose >11 in symptomatic person is diagnostic, seek urgent advice & admit due to DKA risk (check ketones!) - don’t use HbA1C as not useful due to sugar fluctuations not being reflected in RBCs (also not good in haemolysis = falsely low, splenectomy  = falsely high, steroid induced, acute illness)
• T2DM = HbA1C >=48 (or fasting glucose >7) needed on 2 occasions 2w apart is asymptomatic
• OGTT only used now in GDM 

Ramadan & Diabetes 
• Main risks are hypo/hyperglycaemia, DKA & dehydration causing VTE 
• Use IDF-DAR risk calculator for low/mod/high risk 
   - If mod/high risk should not fast 
   - Otherwise adjust meds & check BM 1-2x/d
• Diet advice slow release carbs for Suhoor (morning snack) & Iftar (evening meal) dividing calories equally, avoid excess exercise 
• Break fast if BM <4 or >16.5, symptoms or unwell 
• Generally take most meds (eg split dose metformin) majority with evening Iftar (esp if hypoglycaemic effect) - get advice re insulins

Diabetes on insulin/hypo & driving - inform +
• Group 1 
    - Must have had 1 or less hypo in last year
    - Must have full hypo awareness 
    - Must monitor BMs before driving &2h whilst 
• Group 2 (HGV)
   - Can’t have had any hypos +have full awareness 
   - Monitor BMs as above and BD regularly 
   - DVLA will arrange annual consultant review where will need 3mo BM diary 

Diabetic retinopathy screening 
• National screening programme for all diabetics age >12yo - refer to retinal screening programme 
• Non-proliferative (early) can be silent with no visual sx but microaneurysms, cotton wool spots, venous beading, exudates etc 
• Proliferative occurs due to neovascularisation (new vessel formation) which can bleed (vitreous haemorrhage) or pull on retina (retinal detachment) - and affect central vision (macula)
• Primary prevention = glycaemic control, CVD RFs 
• Secondary prevention = laser photocoagulation & anti-VEGF injections to stop progression to blindness (but may not improve vision), last resort surgical vitrectomy .
