Hayfever (allergic rhinitis) & allergic eye disease 

Runny nose sneezing watery itchy red eye

Allergic eye disease - seasonal vs perennial allergic conjunctivitis (rare = giant papillary, vernal keratoconjunctivitis)
• Cold compress, fridged lubricant eye drops, sunglasses to reduce pollen burden,don’t rub eyes 
• If nasal symptoms also start oral antihistamine 1st 2-4w trial
• If only ocular symptoms try antihistamine eye drops (azelastine) or topical mast cell stabilisers (sodium cromoglicate), both TDS/QDS & take several weeks to work (1st combine if failed trial, then add oral if failed 3rd line)- note preservatives can irritate eye 
• Refer ophthalmology if failed above to consider steroid eye drops or immunosuppression

Allergic rhinitis
• Allergen avoidance, hypoallergenic sheets 
• Oral antihistamine or nasal steroid (Nasonex = mometasone, Beconaise = beclometasone) trial 2-4w - if failed try other or combined 
• Try nasal antihistamine/steroid combo (Dymista = azelastine/ fluticasone) instead or nasal sodium cromoglicate 
• Consider oral pred, refer for immunotherapy
