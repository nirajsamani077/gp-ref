Hearing loss RW

Consider simple outer ear (wax, OE) causing CHL vs acoustic neuroma /stroke causing UL SNHL!

Timing (sudden gradual context) & UL or BL
Ear sx (pain dc), tinnitus, vertigo, CN7, headache/vision and FHx /drugs (ototoxics)
Otoscopy + Rinnes &’Webers 

Sudden SNHL (<3d) = urgent ENT referral Same day r/o central cause - stroke acoustic neuroma (also ototoxic viral trauma) they might consider pred high dose within 2d, if delayed Px >30d ago then 2WW instead of same day 

Rapid gradually worsening SNHL = 2WW ENT
• Acoustic neuroma, cholesteatoma, ototoxic, NPCC (middle ear effusion earache Asian adult)

Routine referral for others 
• Tinnitus - UL = AN or Menieres, changing = stress, Pulsatile = vascular or brainstem
• Fluctuating SNHL, vertigo, fullness, hyperacuisis = Menieres 
• Other CHL = COM, Otosclerosis, wax 
• More ear focus CHL = OE, furuncle, FBS 

Ear wax (spont, narrow canal, swimmers, skin)
• Wax softening drops (medical olive oil otex or bicarb) 2-3 drops 3-4x/d for 4-5d using dropper when lying head in side and staying there , lumps of soft wax should fall out within few weeks (avoid fingers or cotton buds to remove, self cleaning mechanism) if not working 
• Irrigation (flushing out) or microsuction (sucking out) done privately via ear care services at specsavers etc - can risk perf or ear canal damage
• Only remove if have to (CHL impacted wax, need to see drum or mould hearing aids)
