COPD treatment ladder RW 

Health promotion for all 
• Stop smoking (survival benefit) - PY = cigs/20 xY
• Pulmonary rehab (survival benefit) if MRC >=3 or needed admission - 3mo programme managing breathlessness, nutrition, smoking, coping = safe 
• Annual flu jab, one off pneumococcal 
• Screen for malnutrition (BMI) & MH (depression)

1st line - SABA or SAMA PRN (usually SABA) 

2nd line - If Sx (MRC >=3, CAT >10) = 4 options 
• LABA or LAMA alone - small group with very few symptoms & exacerbations, LAMA has CVD risk 
• LABA/LAMA combo = most (MRC >=3, CAT>10)
   - Spiolto Respimat 2 puffs OD (soft mist)
   - Anoro Ellipta /Ultibro Breezhaler 1puff OD (DPI) 
• LABA/ICS combo if asthma/steroid responsivity
   - Features = Hx atopy, variability (PEFR >20%, FEV1 >12% or >400ml over time), eosinophilia >0.3
   - Fostair 100/6 pMDI /NEXThaler (DPI) 2puff BD
   - Other pMDIs: Luforbec 100/6, Symbicort 200/6 (budesonide = bigger. particles so stronger dose)
   - Other DPIs: Symbicort Turbohaler/DuoResp Spriomax/ Relvar Elipta (only one with 1 puff OD)  
• Triple therapy LABA/LAMA/ICS combo - if qualifying for LABA/LAMA with eosinophils >0.3 & exacerbations >2/y or needing admission 
  - Trimbow pMDI 2 puffs BD
  - Trelegy Ellipta DPI 1 puff OD 
 
3rd line - step up or specialist 
• Check adherence, inhaler tech, other causes
• Can switch to different device 
• If on monotherapy can combine or add ICS if eosinophils >0.3 (and even >0.1) or a single exacerbation (which leans towards triple therapy)
• If ongoing exacerbations >1/y or admission & eosinophils <0.1 consider refer specialist meds
  - Prophylactic azithromycin (esp if current non smoker, needs work up ++ sputum CT thorax ECG QTc LFT audiology risk of HL - dose = 250mg 3x/w
  - Roflumilast (PDE4 inhibitor) more for severe bronchitis with FEV1 <50% (severe) 
  - Other - theophylline only if inhalers not sufficient, carbocisteine for sputum ++ (consider azithro though & check eosinophils), home nebs, rescue pack, LTOT (survival benefit if meets criteria), lung volume reduction surgery/bullectomy (survival benefit) 
- Carbocisteine, Acetylcisteine (NACSYS) for reducing sputum viscosity 

Other indications for referral 
• Uncertain Dx (2WW ?Ca, brochiectasis with CT)
• On maximal inhaler therapy with frequent exacs (to consider azithro or roflumilast)
• Possible A1AT (<40, non smoker, FHx)
• Rapid decline FEV1 - >500ml over 5y
• Considering home nebs, LTOT
   - FEV1 at least <50% (severe) usually <30% (v severe) with chronic hypoxaemia (sats <92%, cyanosis, secondary polycythaemia, cor pulmonale which needs echo & diuretics) 
 
NB inhaler specifics 
• Check inspiratory effort as can be reduced in COPD and can mean some inhalers can’t be used effectively - use InCheck Dial G16 (https://www.primarycaresupplies.co.uk/in-check-dial-g16-peak-flow-meter/) 
• pMDIs need least inspiratory effort but tend to have higher carbon footprint, whereas DPI (dry powder inhalers) like NEXThaler/Handihaler need most inspiratory effort but have lowest carbon footprint (soft mist inhalers eg Spiolto Respimat LABA/LAMA combo is low effort low carbon but few other options)

Annual RV 
• MRC, CAT, BMI, exacs, spirometry, inh tech/adherence, smoking offering cessation, offering pulm rehab if MRC >=3 
• Screen for malnutrition (BMI <20, cachexia, or obesity can affect functioning, lung capacity, risk of infections) - involve dietician & screen for MH
• Fitness to fly assessment only if FEV1<50%, LTOT or bullous disease (risk of PTX)
