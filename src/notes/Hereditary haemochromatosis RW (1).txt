Hereditary haemochromatosis RW

Iron overload with HFE gene mutation (AR, homozygous C282Y or H63D, variable expression) 

1 in 7 Northern Europeans carriers of mutation

Causes increased Fe GI absorption/release stores 

Consider if 
• Symptoms of HH (liver disease in M, arthritis usually MCPs first in F, rarer endocrine (DM hypogonad Addisons), restrictive CM, bronze skin) 
• Raised ferritin, raised TSAT or deranged LFTs
   - Ferritin >200 in F, >300 in M
   - TSAT >40% in F, >50% in M 
   - If both or either raised test for HFE gene 
• FHx of HH in first degree relative/North European (rare outside of this group) 

TSAT = Fe/TIBC x100 (check using iron studies) or Fe/TF x 70.9
HFE gene = C282Y or H63D homo/heterozygous 

If HH suspected and positive tests as above refer to haem (ensure done LFT, CRP) & will usually do 
• Liver MRI/fibroscan +/- Bx
• ECG/echo 
• Involve rheum (arthritis), endocrine (DM, Addisons, hypogonadism)

Rx - venesection weekly till low TSAT/ferritin <50 then maintainance venesection 3-4x, desferroxamine = 2nd line

If liver cirhosis = 200x increase risk of HCC (normal life expectancy if not and treated) - so HCC surveillance with AFPs/USS and ETOH avoidance
