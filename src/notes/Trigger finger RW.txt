Trigger finger RW

Tendon sheath with pulleys across to allow tendons to stay with bones, and sheath allows tendons to glide easily, pulleys can become thickened (A1 = on flexor aspect of 1st MCPJ) and then tendons won’t glide and become trapped friction inflammation and nodule with trigger /catching when extending finger back and get stuck on flexing (initially can passively correct then stuck)

Like CTS can be associated with similar conditions and DM, but most idiopathic 

Mainstay = steroid injections cure most (80%) but less so in diabetes, can take 2w for result and if relapse can consider repeating 2nd if not diabetic, can consider hand splint but essentially refer OTS for release if failed steroid injection one or two

•infection rare, tendon rupture rare, allergy
•flare in symptoms more common 
•adrenal suppression (no more than 3 monthly)
•at least 2w post covid vaccine or same time as flu 
•newer = PRP, hyaluronic acid
•best evidence in DQTS and trigger may cure completely (rest if short term or not indicated)
