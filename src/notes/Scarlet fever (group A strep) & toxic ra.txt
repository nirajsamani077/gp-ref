Scarlet fever (group A strep) & toxic rash DDx RW 

Severe childhood (2-8y) infection with outbreaks & needs PHE notification - caused by GAS (strep pyogenes) spread by resp/saliva (4d incubation period) & often present in healthy children in skin & throats (non invasive strains) but superantigens & invasive strains can cause severe illness 

Presentation 
• Fever, sore throat & blanching red rash
   - Starts on torso & spreads to extremities after 1d
   - Spots & blotches which look like sunburn with goose pimples “sandpaper” “punctate” rash
   - Can eventually desquaminate (peel)
• Specific ENT “red” signs = strawberry tongue, flushing with perioral pallor, red macules on palate (Forchheimer spots) & cervical LN 
• Comps = 
   - Toxic shock syndrome - severe SIRS sepsis ITU
   - Rheumatic fever & PSGN 
• However usually mild self limiting but needs Abx 

Management 
• Pen V (or Amox) for 10d - PA = azithro 5d 
• Para & Nurofen, fever usually resolves after 24h of Abx so safety net & can return to nursery/school 24h after ABx started, review again 1-2d as admit if not improving & consider throat swab at this point only or if concern ?outbreak 
• Notify PHE, home hygiene & avoid IC/pregnant 

DDx for “toxic” macpap rash
• Slapped cheek (Parvo B19) or Roseola (HHV6)
• MMR - usually starts on face/behind ears spreading to cover whole body, high fever, Koplik 
• Kawasakis - prolonged fever >5, red signs, conjunctivitis & desquamination.
