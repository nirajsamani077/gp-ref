IBD (inflammatory bowel) RW 

1% of population (300k in UK) usually younger adults (2nd smaller peak 50/60s) lifelong remitting relapsing inflammation of bowel segments & extraintestinal effects, genetic & environmental fx
• UC - partial thickness rectum up continuous, bleeding predominant 
• Crohns - full thickness anywhere skip lesions with pain/WL predominant, fistulae, strictures, abscesses, smoking associated (UC = ?protective)
• (Microscopic colitis) - normal colonoscopy but Bx shows inflammatory change, watery diarrhoea, associated with meds (NSAID, aspirin, PPI, SSRI), Rx usually PO budesonide 9mg 8w then taper (if failed other options below)

Features (significant overlap with IBS but red flags)
• PR bleeding, diarrhoea, tenesmus (rectal)
• Weight loss/faltering growth, systemic upset
• Exam - abdo mass, fistulae/PR abnormal, acute 
• Bloods - raised CRP, anaemia 

For all bloods (FBC, UE, LFT/bone, CRP, Coeliac, TFT, CA125 if woman >50 with GI Sx) & stool tests 
• Stool MC&S (r/o infection) 
• FIT test if meets criteria (>40 pain/WL, >50 bleeding, >60 IDA CIBH), if >10 refer 2WW 
• Faecal calprotectin (protein released from inflamed bowel, very sensitive (good R/O if negative) but not specific (even above meds can raise as well as Ca/infection etc) 
 - <100 = Unlikely IBD, make positive Dx IBS if everything else Ok, unless age >50 & calprotectin >50 where confused routine gastro referral 
 - 100-250 = Repeat in 4-6w & refer routine, but if rising >250 then urgent gastro referral 
 - >250 = Urgent gastro referral, if unwell admit 

Secondary care will do 
• Colonoscopy & Bx (confirms Dx & extent) 
• Capsule endoscopy (extent) 
• CT/MRI abdo (extent) 

Management of new IBD under specialist depends on UC or Crohns (CD)

• Inducing remission
- UC = rectal mesalazine 4w unless pancolitis/L sided/refractory 4w add or together with oral mesalazine then steroids (topical usually for proctitis only)
- CD = oral pred 40mg 8w tapering by 5mg/w (budesonide or mesalazine less effective less S/E), if not improving add AZA/mercaptopurine (check TMPT activity first as if low won’t work), if can’t use or not improving add MTX or inflixmab, enteral nutrition alone in kids 
- Given need for steroids (never for maintanence) check FRAX and DEXA, vit D/Ca supps, bisphosphonates & muscle strengthening exercise 

• Maintaining remission 
- UC = Oral (or rectal) mesalazine, if >2 exacs add AZA/mercaptopurine (check TPMT activity 1st)
- CD = May not need (pt choice), stop smoking, options of AZA/mercaptopurine, MTX if failed, surgery for comps (fistulae, strictures, abscess) in >50%

• Managing IBD flare in primary care 
- Admit if systemically unwell, check bloods & faecal calprotectin, Truelove & Witts criteria in UC
 • >6 stool/d, bleeding in every stool, fever, tachycardia (>90), acute abdomen 
 • Anaemia, raised ESR (>30)
- If not admitting d/w IBD nurses and Px oral steroids (pred or budesonide usually 6-8w 30-40mg then wean 5mg/week) depends on advice 

Extraintestinal manifestations (30% of UC)
• Liver = PSC, AIH, gallstones (CD, malabsorption)
• Rheum = Seronegative arthritis/AS, sacroiliitis
• Eye = Anterior uveitis, episcleritis/scleritis 
• Skin = Erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum

Other factors
• No live vaccines if on DMARD/biologics (should be 4w before starting, check Green Book), annual flu, 5yrly pneumococcal 
• Dietician, 85% malnourished, 1/3 have IDA
• Effective contraception (ideally LARC although IBD is UKMEC1 for most) needed 
• Fertility - most have intact but active fistulating or surgical CD reduces, mesalazine/sulfasalazine MTX can reduce sperm count, may need 5mg folic acid preconception if malabsorption or sulfasalazine, stop MTX/MM/biological preconception & refer specialist immediately if becomes pregnant 
• Avoid long term NSAIDs & opiates  

IBD in children 
• 1/3 present under 20 - have a low threshold to test faecal calprotectin & inflamm markers if GI symptoms, weight loss/faltering growth
• Elemental diet as well as other meds can help induce remission, low threshold to admit & involve specialist 
• Significant impact on growth, puberty and nutritional deficiencies /malabsorption 

Signpost Crohns & Colitis UK & CICRA for kids

Additional nurse nuggets 
• Loperamide is fine as long as no strictures, r/o infection first 
• Bowel prep moviprep or picolax, low residue diet
• Video capsule endoscopy for surveillance 
• Orofacial granulomatosis (OFG) in crohns - use pred mouthwash 
• Avoid NSAIDs all IBD - risk of ulceration 
• Use septrin with steroids oral if IC ++/biologics 
• Can inc oral mesalazine 2.4g to 4.8g max dose for 2 weeks initially sometimes
