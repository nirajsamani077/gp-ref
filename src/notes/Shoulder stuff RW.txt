Shoulder stuff RW

4 S approach
• Site - deltoid = SA/GH, AC = AC, back = neck
• Stiff - no passive movement = frozen shoulder or OA, preserved passive movement = SA, AC, neck 
• Serious? = red flags infection malignancy 
• Stable? = refer if ?instability (dislocation hx)

Main DDx 
• SA pain syndrome (rotator cuff) = tendinopathy, tendinitis (calcific), bursitis, tears = impingement 
• Frozen shoulder 
• GH or AC OA
• Referred neck pain 
• Serious pathology 

Stiffness different to loss of passive ROM
Impingement causes twinge from trapping of tendon in SA space (tested by Hawkins/Neers tests)

Don’t image rotator cuff in primary care (USS or MRI) as doesn’t change management, often asymptomatic tear UNLESS history of trauma (unable to abduct shoulder 90 deg after trauma = same day orthopaedic review)

SA pain Rx = reduce/modify activity, NSAIDs, physio (using BESS/ British Elbow and shoulder society leaflet = Bess.ac.uk), steroid injection (at most 2), surgical decompression controversial 

Frozen shoulder = pain stiffness with fibrosed thickened shoulder joint capsule. Often diabetic. Often night pain stiffness insidious onset , loss of PASSIVE movement esp ER,  clinical Dx but as main DDx is GH OA XR recommended for a “stiff shoulder” 
• Freezing (pain & stiffness), frozen (stiff) then thawing (stiffness improving) cycle can last 3yr. Rx = NSAID, physio BESS in frozen and thawing stage steroid injection (intraarticular rather than SA) , capsular release 

OA = GH stiff shoulder XR, ACJ can be acute fall or chronic Oa, usually localised with finger on AC with positive scarf test.
