Enuresis RW

Generally should be having dry nights by 5yo but no min/max age and treatment if distress (traditional age for treatment is 7yo but not now)

Not the child’s fault, often other factors /causes)
• polyuria (UTI, T1DM)
• Bladder dysfunction/ constipation (nocturnal or daytime too - down LUTS pathway)
• Sleep arousal issues (OSA)
• Family hx /genetics 

Red flags - immediate referral 
• Unexplained secondary enuresis (was dry for at least 6mo now not)
• Systemically unwell/ ?T1DM 
• LL weakness/neurology 
• SG concerns

Management 
• Fluid intake at least 1-2L, avoid fizzy/caffeinated 
• Regular toileting in day and before bed 
• Reward system/star chart for agreed behaviours (eg drinking fluid, toilet before bed)
• If fails
    - Enuresis alarm - Improves arousal level in response to need for PU, when sounds child and parent wakes up, go to toilet and reset, over long time can give successful try nights 
    - Desmopressin (desmomelts 120-240mcg, or desmotabs 200-400mcg, never nasal spray which is blacklisted due to hyponatraemia risk) - only if alarm therapy unacceptable or rapid treatment needed, need to stop all fluid 1h before to 8h after taking as can retain too much water and cause dangerously low salt levels), review at 1mo continue Max 3mo then withdraw 
    - Refer to paeds (May use anticholingergics?)
