Addisons/adrenal insufficiency RW

Rare, potentially fatal, steroid/cortisol deficiency 

Either 
• primary “Addisons disease” (adrenal autoimmune most common, adrenalectomy, TB ,haemorrhage)
• secondary (pituitary tumour with hypopituitarism)
• tertiary (exogenous steroids, reducing endogenous production)

Addisons/adrenal crisis 
• Ppt acute stress/infection/MI
• Systemically unwell, postural hypotension, hypoglycaemia, acute abdomen, coma 
• Call 999, if strong suspicion give hydrocortisone 100mg IM (succinate or phosphate)

Stable Addisons  
• Tired, Tripping, Tearful, Tanned, Thin 
• Abdo pain, vomiting, postural drop >20 SBP, weight loss, salt craving
• Pigmentation (friction/sun exposed) in primary due to XS MSH 
• Bloods - low glucose, low Na, high K, high Ca
• 9am (morning) cortisol = best screening test 
   - <100 = likely & high risk of crisis, admit 
   - <400 = refer short synacthen
   - >400 = highly unlikely (but not if on oestrogen)

Management 
• Hydrocortisone (15-25mg 2-3 DD) to replace cortisol & Fludrocortisone (50-200mcg 1-2 DD) to replace mineralocorticoid aldosterone & reduce postural hypotension - started by specialist, 6 monthly repeat, free prescription exemption 
• Sick day rules & steroid card/medic alert bracelet 
  - Double dose in moderate illness (requiring Abx, surgical procedure), don’t need to double fludro
  - If severe illness/oral route lost = Hydrocortisone IM 100mg then discuss/admit, 50mg QDS M 
  - If not on pred or hydrocortisone but oral route present = Hydrocortisone 20mg then 10mg QDS 
  - Specific rules https://www.endocrinology.org/media/4142/ai-and-exogenous-steroids_pis_final.pdf
• Home emergency kit = Hydrocortisone IM 100mg phosphate or succinate (not acetate as slow release used for joint injections), 3-5 vials, 2ml syringe and blue needle if any primary, secondary on steroid replacement. If on oral pred don’t need double dose seek help. 
• Other factors 
   - Written management plan & training (& staff!)
   - Annual flu and one off pneumococcal jab 
   - Flag notes major alert & ambulance register as steroid dependent condition 
   - Inducers (rifampicin, SJW, AEDs) can lower HC levels and increase risk of adrenal crisis 
   - Consider AI (thyroid, T1DM, vitiligo, Coeliac) 
   - Less risks than pred for osteoporosis (but higher if POF or hypogonadism which is more common) - lesser glucocorticoid effect 

Signpost Bart’s Endocrinology - living with Addisons disease 

Anabolic steroids 
• Common, estimates >5% makes, doses often 1000x recommended amount, to help training 
• Effects 
   - Reversible = acne, oedema, HTN, aggression 
   - Irreversible = voice, baldness, CM, tendon rupture, hepatic Ca, BBV with needle sharing, testicular atrophy/gynaecomastia 
• Also used fat burners (clenbuterol, thyroxine, ephedrine), muscle gainers (insulin, GH), fluid down (diuretics), hormones 
• Examine, check baseline bloods/ECG & encourage stopping (don’t need to taper), advise that it’s banned in sports, signpost local needle exchange, consider need for annual bloods 
• Consider body dysmorphic disorder & refer MH
