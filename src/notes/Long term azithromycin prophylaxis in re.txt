Long term azithromycin prophylaxis in resp conditions RW

Specialist decision in COPD/asthma/bronchiectasis 
• Usually if >3 exacs/year or needing hospital
• Has immunomodulatory effects as well as Abx 
• None are licensed which is why it’s specialist 

Work-up 
• Really needs to be a non-smoker
• Sputum, LFT, ECG (QTc) & audiology (risk of HL)
   - If QTc >0.45s men or >0.47s women don’t give 
   - Repeat ECG again after 1mo to check this 
   - If sputum shows non-tuberculous myco don’t 

Usual dosing 
• Azithromycin 500mg 3x a week (or 250mg daily)
• Review after 6-12mo & specialist can consider 
• S/E usually GI common (reduce dose 250mg)
