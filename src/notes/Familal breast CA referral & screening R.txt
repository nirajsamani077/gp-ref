Familal breast CA referral & screening RW

1/5 cases of Breast Ca have significant FHx

High risk so refer for early mammogram/genetics if 
ONE 
• 1st degree female <40
• Male or bilateral <50
• Jewish ancestry 
TWO
• 1st degree females
• 1x 1st & 1x 2nd breast /ovarian 
THREE 
• Any 1st or 2nd degree breast/ovarian 

If FHx have increased baseline risk, HRT doesn’t have additive risk but should consider alternative options, oestrogen only (but rarely as needs TAH) or use for shortest period minimum effective dose 

Lifestyle factors - BMI, ETOH/smoking important 
Ask if known to have BRCA already or not 

Preventative Rx 
• Anastrozole now licensed for use in prevention for post menopausal women at high risk (tamoxifen if premenopausal) for 5yrs 
• If BRCA1/2 mutation and young high risk may consider risk reducing bilateral mastectomy & oophrectomy under specialist MDT (man with BRCA have much higher risk of breast and aggressive prostate CA) 

For Breast screening of general pop 
• 50-70 3 yearly mammogram
• Older can self-refer (unlike Cx screening)
• Trans registered as male won’t automatically be offered but should be referred directly to national screening programme (if taking high dose oestrogen clearly at higher risk)
• Issue with overdiagnosis (1 death prevented for every 3 diagnosed cases which would never cause any issues) & mammogram radiation risk for those younger (<30) with BRCA mutation which increases breast Ca risk (offer USS/MRI instead)
