NSAIDs RW

Topical NSAIDs 
• Much safer than oral, no increased CV GI risk 
• Include topical diclofenac, ibuprofen, ketoprofen
• 1st line for most OA, esp knee OA

Oral NSAIDs 
• Ibuprofen low dose 400mg TDS has lowest GI & CVD risk (high dose 800mg TDS doesn’t)
• Diclofenac has highest GI & CVD risk 
• Naproxen has moderate GI & CVD risk 
• COX-2 inhibitors (meloxicam, celecoxib, etoricoxib) has low GI risk but high CVD risk - so probably avoid 
• Co-prescribe PPI, esp if CVD on antithrombotics where risk is highest (although not omeprazole with clopidogrel), any age >45 or Hx of GORD
•  Naproxen or low-dose ibuprofen preferred, with PPI, used for shortest time @ lowest dose 
• In CKD avoid or monitor eGFR if unavoidable, especially high risk with ACE & diuretics .
• In pregnancy avoid - esp 1st & 3rd TM
