Asthma /preschool wheeze management <5 RW

Usually can’t do tests at this age so trial of treatment based on clinical

Distinguishing factors (all more suggest asthma)
• Duration of wheeze during viral illness (>10d)
• >3 episodes/year, >3x SABA issues/y
• Nocturnal worsening (night waking >1x/w)
• Interval Sx Between episodes (SABA >1-3x/w)
• FHx asthma/atopy, consider parental smoking 
• Multi-trigger wheeze to other allergens exercise smoke etc rather than just VIW higher risk in child 

Rx :
SABA PRN + Very low dose ICS (Clenil 50-100mcg 1 puff Bd) 8 week trial 

If not improving 
• Add montelukast 8w (4mg ON) - can cause nightmares, or 
• Trial 8w moderate ICS (200mcg bd) - first line for NICE 

If improvement on either or 1st line = suggests asthma, stop treatment as a trial & 
• if sx recur within 4w restart low dose ICS LT
• if recur after 4w repeat trial 8w moderate ICS or montelukast

If no improvement review & refer to paeds asthma 
• Specialist might do spiro depending on age (usually >5), FENO, challenge test, CXR
• DDx - recurrent infection, CF, CHD, CLD, reflux, dysfunctional 

Make sure have pMDI + spacer (aerospacer, volumatic + face mask) - don’t do quick deep breaths do 5 “normal” tidal breaths & clean spacers monthly (use detergent, air dry) - if can’t use spacer consider neb, give 1 puff at a time 
• If worried (iWOB, can’t speak full sentences, cyanosis) - give 10 puffs & if sx do not improve quickly call 999

Aims for treatment  
• TOTAL CONTROL, sx free rarely using reliever 
• Poor control
   - Waking at night >1x/w, using SABA 3x/w
   - Issuing >3 SABA/y or <3 ICS/y
   - Exacerbation needing steroid/admission
   - Exercise induced sx 
• If Dx asthma should have emergency action plan 

Doses (beclometasone equivalents)
• V low dose ICS = 50mcg BD
• Low dose ICS = 100mcg BD 
• Moderate dose ICS = 200mcg BD
• High dose ICS = 400mcg BD
