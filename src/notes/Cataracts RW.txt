Cataracts RW

Lens opacity, usually age related but can be due to 
• Trauma or eye disease (uveitis, glaucoma etc)
• Myopia (short sighted)
• Steroids or endocrine (DM, Wilson’s)
• Smoking /ETOH
• Genetic (Downs, Marfans) 

Most common cause of blindness, presents 
• Gradual visual loss, glare worse at night, halos 
• Refractive change needing new glasses 
• Refer optometrist for full assessment - check VA fundoscopy loss of red reflex, slit lamp, IOP 

Management - usually cataract surgery if functional impairment not improved by glasses & affecting QOL 
• If BL remove one and wait to remove other few weeks later 
• Comps =endoophthalmitis, posterior capsule rupture (most common), floppy iris sybddome which has been linked to tamsulosin 

Driving  
• Group 1 : Can read number plate from 20m and binocular VA >6/12 
• Group 2 (HGV) : higher standards needed (check)

Signpost RNIB for info
