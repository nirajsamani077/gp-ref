Meningitis /meningococcal sepsis RW

Consider in sick child, non blanching rash (NBR) & calling ambulance, whilst waiting giving IM benpen 
• Dehydrated, iWOB, drowsy, toxic/fever
• Meningism/ bulging fontanelle a late sign so 
• If NBR/petechial rash which is spreading this is a sign of meningococcal so give IM benpen 
  - >10y = 1.2g, 1-10y = 600mg, <1y = 300mg
• If NBR in well child with no fever - risk of meningococcal disease is low but still low threshold to admit, if not may be viral petechiae, thrombocytopenia/clotting disorder (check urgent FBC & clotting) or NAI! 
• Acute Rx = Resus, WCC/CRP/BC & LP, IV Cef/ IVI

Long term sequelae/follow up post meningitis 
• Treatment of household contacts (PHE notify)
• Audiology within 1mo after well (risk of SNHL), may need cochlear implants 
• Immunodeficiency testing if recurrent episodes or Men B (checking complements by immunology)
• All should have paeds FU 4-6w post discharge .
