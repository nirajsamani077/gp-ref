Flashes & floaters RW

Take any new seriously - main risk of stroke/TIA, retinal detachment or vitreous haemorrhage but most will be simple posterior vitreous detachment (PVD) which is usually related 
• Check VA - if visual loss = immediate referral 
• Also check pupils, fields & fundoscopy 
• RAPD swinging light test for monocular obstruction of visual pathway 
  - Both pupils dilate a bit and returns to not quite contracted state due to obstruction which dims 
• Curtain falling on vision or enlarging shadows suggests retinal detachment 
• Monocular suggests eye, BL suggests neuro 

Other DDx = visual migraine /aura with coloured zig zags and enlarged blind spot (scintillating scotoma)

Rx = for all new immediate eye casualty referral
