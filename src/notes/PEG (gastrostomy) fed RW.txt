PEG (gastrostomy) fed RW

Used if lost oral route (dysphagia, HNSCC etc) - inserted via small abdo incision under LA & tube inserted percutaenously & guided by endoscopy 

PEG tube needs regular care - cleaning of site/tube /fixation device - good links from ASGE & BAPEN websites for carers

Nutrition team will manage individual feeding & refer any issues (CIBH, vomiting, cramps) to them 

Prescribed meds will need to be changed to PEG and may not be able to be given via PEG so check BAPEN website 
• Do = take orally if able to swallow, use OD preps, check licensing & with pharmacy re crushing tabs, regularly flush tube to prevent blockage 
• Don’t = use slow/modified release or enteric coated, use expensive liquids or ones containing sorbitol/granules which can cause irritation 

Specific PEG problems (usually managed by nutrition team) 
• Blocked tube - if not flushing with water (never saline as can crystallise in tube) = refer 
• Damaged tube - should be reinserted if >6w after insertion, risk of comps eg infection /leaking 
• Dislodged tube - immediate surgical referral 
• Buried bumper - internal bumper in stomach works it’s way out to skin, peritonitis risk, immediate surgical referral 
• Infected tube (PEG infection) - immediate surgical referral as high risk infection 
• Leaking tube - PPI increases chances, if one off resolved use barrier cream, if persistent refer 
• Bleeding tube - High risk esp if on DOAC/aspirin/antiplatelet so immediate surgical referral .
