SLE and Sjogrens for GP RW

SLE:
Inflammatory arthritis 50%
Malar butterfly photosensitive rash 30%
Nephropathy at presentation 20%
Alopecia (non scarring), oral ulcers, Raynauds 
Abnormal FBC (Pancytopenias)
Pleuropericarditis (serositis)
Neuro (headaches, seizures, psychosis )
Fatigue very common 
CV risk very important 

Complex multi system CT AI disease 

If suspect refer rheum +
• ESR > CRP and rest baseline bloods 
• ANA, ENA, RF, Anti DSDNA, ANCA, C3/4, APLA (anticardiolipin/ lupus anticoagulant)
• Urine for blood protein ACR

Sjogrens :
Primary or secondary to RA/SLE/scleroderma
Often women with dry mouth eyes vagina - check for inflammatory arthritis systemic sx 
Significantly higher risk B cell lymphoma 
Dry eye drops, artificial saliva , muscarinics, no good DMARDs
