Acne vulgaris PCDS RW

Any treatment takes 6w to have any improvement and 3-6mo to see maximum effect (12w courses) - aim is to prevent scarring 

Skin care advice 
• Avoid picking at spots - can cause scarring 
• Avoid oil based moisturisers and make-up, and remove make up daily (don’t keep on overnight)
• Use non-alkaline synthetic detergent face wash BD widely available OTC (eg Acneicide)

Mild (mainly comedomal) = topical retinoid 
• Differin (adapelene) sometimes or..
• + BPO = Epiduo 1% usually (dual agents reduce resistance so preferred) which causes irritation, photosensitivity and can bleach fabrics 
• Leave on 30-60mins before bed then wash off (slowly increase time till kept overnight) 
• Caution in pregnancy/BF

Mild-moderate (+ some papules/pustules)
• Fixed combo to reduce bacterial resistance 12w
• Epiduo (adapalene + BPO) or Duac (BPO + clindamycin)
• Alt Abx/retinoid combos = Treclin, Aknemycin
• If using any retinoid need good contraception 

Moderate (+ widespread papules/pustules )
• Epiduo or azelaic acid with ORAL abx for 12w
• Lymecycline 408mg OD (or doxy 100mg OD)
• 2nd line - Clary (only option for children <12), Trimethoprim 300mg BD (small risk of severe skin reaction & agranulocytosis)
• Can use up to 6mo if significant reaction 
• Azelaic acid is only option which is safe in pregnancy/BF, tetracyclines unsafe & only >12yo

Severe (+ nodulocystic, scarring)
• Refer for oral roaccutane (isotretinoin) only if >12
• Baseline bloods inc U&E, LFT, lipids, check MH due to risk, ensure good contraception as highly teratogenic (pregnancy prevention programme like valproate) 
• 4-5 month course, takes at least a month to see any changes, 50% permanently cured after 1 course, 20% need a second course 
• S/E - deranged LFT/lipids, dry skin/eyes, severe potential effect on MH/sexual function (counselling before), should be on contraception 1mo prior to treatment and urine preg test will be checked prior, continue contraception at least 1mo after completing (much longer 2y for actiretin)

Refer if 
• Failed 2x 3mo abx courses with fixed combo topical (Epiduo), if had to have roaccutane before or psych distress
• Any scarring should be urgent /semi urgent ref
• Post-inflamm hyperpigmentation can persist for months so early referral for roaccutane 

Preg/BF = topical BPO or 2% erythromycin (oral erythro depending on risks benefits), azelaic acid safe 

Hormonal acne 
• Consider and test for PCOS If relevant (FAI)
• COCP - used to be Dianette but ++ VTE risk so if using only for short time (3/12), risk higher if stopping & starting rather than continuous, best ones are with lower oestrogen (20mcg) eg Mercilon or Eloine, can tricycle, if not working can consider Dianette 
• Spironolactone- under specialist, U&E first, can be used as addition, 50mg Od (max 200mg), not in pregnancy (feminisation of male fetus), not in men due to risk of gynaecomastia 

Acne PPx = blockage of tiny hair follicles with sebum /oils which causes inflammation and build up of a bacteria which makes this worse 
• Hormonal imbalances usually puberty /PCOS, possibly diet, smoking & certain drugs (steroids POP lithium phenytoin) can worsen 
• Enquire androgenic sx and psychosocial impact 

Signpost Acne Support website
