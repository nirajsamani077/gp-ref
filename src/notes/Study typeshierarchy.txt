Study types/hierarchy 

RCT - subjects randomly allocated to intervention or control group

Cohort (look forward) - subjects selected based on exposure & followed up to see if they get outcome/disease (result in relative risk)
Try to match groups
Risk ratios 

• 20 subjects given paracetamol, 16 had >50% pain relief after 6 months, 4 did not 
• 20 subjects given no paracetamol/placebo - 10 had >50% pain relief after 6 months, 10 did not 
• Experimental event rate (EER) = 16/20 = 0.8
• Control event rate (CER) = 10/20 = 0.5
• Relative risk ratio = 1.6 
• Risk ratio >1 means rate of event increased by exposure & can calculate relative risk increase (RRI) = EER-CER/CER = 0.6 = 60% (RRR is opposite) 
• Hazard ratio similar to relative risk ratio but used when risk is not constant to time (like survival over time, which is lower with age etc)

Case-control (look back) - subject selected based on whether they are cases (ie have disease/outcome) or controls & look back to see whether they were exposed or not (results in odds ratio). Quick, inexpensive, better for rarer diseases/cases but prone to confounding 
• Out of 10 cases of MI, 8 were smokers & 2 not whereas 10 controls (no MI) 5 smoke 5 not 
• Odds of MI when smoking = 8/2= 0.8
• Odds of no MI when smoking = 5/5 = 0.5
• Odds ratio = 4/1  = 4

Cross-sectional (snapshot) - prevalence right now, weak evidence of cause/effect 

EBM hierarchy 
1a = systemic review of homogenous RCTs
1b = individual narrow CI RCT
1c = all or none RCT
2a = systematic review of cohort studies 
2b = individual cohort study or low quality RCT
2c = outcomes research
3a = systematic review of case control study 
3b = individual case control study 
4 = case series or poor cohort/case control study 
5 = expert opinion
