Osteoarthritis (hip, knee, hand) RW

Tear, wear and repair (rather than passive “wear and tear”) - often multiple factors (weight, inflammation, mechanical stress causes whole joint complex to have higher balance of destruction of cartilage vs repair - with body trying to repair causing inflammation and more subchondral bone turnover (osteophytes) which eventually means cartilage gets worn away and damaged eventually leading to bone on bone severe arthritis 

Cartilage like the rubber on your tyres, when healthy allows car to glide over the road, when worn away and damaged causes friction and the wheels to breakdown (shock absorber)

Clinical Dx - imaging not beneficial, may be useful in atypical features (many with OA don’t have XR changes, many without OA have XR changes) - many treatment strategies that work 

Exercises/physio, weight loss, analgesia, steroid inj

Therapeutic “joint specific” rehab exercise of great benefit for most , better than painkillers and do no harm - even usual activities don’t stop them as “motion is lotion” and even if you have severe OA you are not doing more damage but probably slowing progression . Physio can help with mobility aids 

Lose 10% body weight (esp knee over hip) as weight bearing joints, improves knee replacement outcomes 

For analgesia in OA
• Topical NSAIDs, then oral NSAIDS + PPI, then steroid injection (short lived benefits up to 2-3mo, can lose its effect over time, only if joint “inflamed”, for hip USS guided, probable max 3-4x per year 
• Do not routinely offer para, codeine, or other opioids eg tramadol, or gabapentinoids 

For surgery 
• Can’t function, can’t walk, can’t sleep, can’t manage anymore with current measures , replacement would mean significant improving QOL (THR/TKR - most last >10-25y, in unicompartmental knee Oa consider partial replacement as less comps )

Knee OA specifics 
• Often relate to Bakers cyst (posterior bulge of knee effusion, rupture causes acute DVT like calf swelling so need to R/O, otherwise consider OTS for aspiration/injection) 
• Arthroscopic knee surgery “washout” of cartilage not recommended 

Hip OA specifics 
• Achy groin pain and stiffness worse at end of day, when severe rest and night pain
• DDx - trochanteric bursitis (lateral pain), RA, sciatica, femoroacetabular impingement (young person with hip pain worse on activity and hip flexion)

Hand OA specifics
• Often DIPs involved with Herbs and Bouchs (remember RA doesn’t affect DIPs, significant morning stiffness >1h)
• Hand/thumb splints useful 
• Steroid inj only effective for IP or thumb CMC
• 1st MTPJ OA is hallux limitus or rigidus
