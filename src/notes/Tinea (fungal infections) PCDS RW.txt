Tinea (fungal infections) PCDS RW

Tinea corporis/cruris/mannum (hands)
• Unilateral, annular red scaly edge itchy, sometimes pustules 
• Mild = Daktarin up to 4w, Lamisil (terbinafine) cream up to 2w (more expensive & effective) 
• Extensive = Terbinafine (Lamisil) 250mg OD 2w
• DDx = Granuloma annulare (bumpy edge), annular ezcema/psoriasis 

Fungal nail infection (tinea unguum) 
• White/ yellow nail change with subungal debris and disintegration of distal nail plate (mild = white and distal, severe = yellow thick)
• Always clippings /scrapings by cutting nail back and removing subungal debris with blade for mycology - collect in folded dark paper square and place in Dermapak - if positive treat but if negative repeat (30% false negative rate) or consider DDx 
• Topical usually low cure rate, single nail, long time - Loceryl nail lacquer twice weekly 6-12 months for finger-toe nails respectively. File nails back before applying 
• Systemic Lamisil 250mg OD for 6-12 weeks usually for finger-toes multiple respectively if symptomatic and mycology positive (high false negative) - best against trichophyton, safe, check LFT before and then at 6 weeks - counsel re signs of liver toxicity, not a perfect treatment (failure rate 25%), 2nd line itraconazole, 3rd line fluconazole 
• DDx - trauma, psoriasis, lichen planus, melanoma 

Tinea pedis (Athletes foot)
• Scaly feet interdigital spaces itchy peeling, usually related to recurrent moist environments eg swimming, communal showers 
• Lamisil Daktarin or Canesten cream BD 1-2w till scale disappears (esp in between toes) then 1w after - Terbinafine probably has best cure rate (70%), add mild steroid if inflamm ++, if failed send scrapings and try oral Lamisil 
• Avoid walking bare feet (reduces transmission) & wear breathable footwear cotton socks keeping feet dry and rotate shoes every 2-3d
• DDx = keratoderma, pitted keratolysis 

Tinea capitis 
• Scaly scalp, occasionally scarring alopecia 
• Send plucked hairs/skin scrapings for mycology 
• Lamisil 250mg OD 4w to reduce risk of scarring alopecia, and household Ketoconazole (Nizoral) twice a week for 2w
• DDx = Seb derm, psoriasis, discoid lupus 

Tinea barbae  
• Inflammation pustules in beard - use Lamisil 4w (similar reduce risk scarring alopecia) 
• DDx = folliculitis, impetigo 

Steroid modified tinea - often tinea mistaken for discoid/annular ezcema or guttate psorasis & given  topical steroids which will cause a specific response where tinea will lose distinct inflamed scaly edge or change into bizzare shapes - stop steroid, take scrapings & start antifungals which usually need to be longer courses >2-4w
