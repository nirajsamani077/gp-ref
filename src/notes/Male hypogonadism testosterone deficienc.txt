Male hypogonadism/ testosterone deficiency RW

Under & over diagnosed - media misleading, testosterone replacement, benefits against osteoporosis /sexual dysfunction but risks of polycythaemia/prostate/ cvd 

Check testosterone (morning fasted 9am)
• ED or loss of early morning erections (common)
• Loss of libido 
• Gynaecomastia 
• Hot flushes “male menopause”
• Consider if A/S + osteoporosis, anaemia, infertility, on steroids/antipsychotics 

Examine - BMI, testicular volume (pea grape plum), SSCs (body hair, penile size), DRE prostate & breast for gynaecomastia 

If morning fasted 9am total testosterone <12 = low
• Repeat in 4w with LH, FSH, prolactin 
• If 8-12 check SHBG for FAI (http://www.pctag.uk/testosterone-calculator/)
- If total <8, free <0.18 = confirmed deficiency 
   • With low LH or raised PRL ?pit adenoma 
- If total 8-12, free 0.18-0.25 = suspected
   • With raised LH= primary, low/normal = second 
   • Refer endocrinology (urgent if ?pit adenoma)

Causes of testosterone deficiency 
• Primary (raised LH/FSH) - Undescended testes, mumps orchitis , Kleinfelters 
• Secondary (low LH/FSH, more common) - Pituiary adenoma usually micro, Cushings/steroids, ETOH, Hbopathy, HH 

Testosterone replacement usually specialist decision with different preps (TD gel/pump or IM injections)
• C/I in prostate CA/severe BPH, male breast CA, active desire for having child (azospermia risk), HCT >0.54/thrombophilia, severe CCF/OSA
• S/E = polycythaemia, gynaecomastia, oedema, acne/skin reaction 
• Usually monitoring 3, 6, 12 monthly 
   - Early morning fasted testosterone = aim total 15-30, if TD should be checked 2-4h after use 
   - HCT = aim <0.54 & if higher stop/reduce/refer 
   - PSA = any increase >1.4 in 1y or 0.4/y in 2y refer urology
