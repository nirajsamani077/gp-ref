Adolescent idiopathic scoliosis RW

Most common spinal deformity in teenagers (3%) with FHx important - defined as lateral curvature of spine >10deg which can be 
• Congenital - NF
• Neuromuscular - cerebral palsy, DMD, Marfans 
• Idiopathic - most common 

Usually a noticed physical deformity or mild back pain (if significant then would be thinking sarcoma)

Examination= neuro exam & Adams bend test
• Stripped down, look when standing & bending forwards with straight knees & palms together (praying to ground) - check for hump to one side of vertebral column = positive 

If Adams test positive refer to paeds spinal specialist & may be asked to request full length standing PA & lateral XR spine 

Management = 3 options 
• Watchful waiting - specialist surveillance 6mo
• Bracing - worn 23h a day so hard to keep 
• Surgery - major op, neuro comps, done in 10%.
