Coeliac disease RW 

Common (1%), underdiagnosed, never Dx IBS without checking TTG first or considering FIT/ calprotectin /CA125

Autoimmune inflammation of small bowel lining in response to gluten (wheat, rye, barley, oats) with genetic/environmental factors - causing malabsorption & symptoms 
• Rare to see classic diarrhoea, WL, FTT in child 
• Any GI sx (CIBH, bloating, indigestion) 
• Deficencies (IDA, B12, calcium/Vit D & manifestations (fatigue, osteoporosis, neuropathy)
• Dermatitis herpetiformis rash (extensors)
• Delayed puberty, short stature, infertility 
• Other AI diseases (T1DM, thyroid, Addisons) 

Testing with IgA TTG (with total IgA as deficiency common meaning test won’t show anything) as long as gluten in diet (at least 10g or 4 slices of bread for 6w prior to testing)
• Strong positive = likely, refer all to gastro for OGD duodenal Dx to confirm Dx, don’t start GFD yet 
• Weak positive = check IgA EMA (endomysial) unless child & refer (if negative or child) 
• IgA deficiency = check IgG TTG or EMA 
• Negative = low threshold to retest or refer if high index of suspicion (eg other AI diseases or FHx), specialist may do HLA testing 

Management = lifelong GFD once Dx Bx confirmed (all should be referred to gastro therefore)
• All with dietician support , wheat barley rye all avoided (breads, cereals, pastas etc)
• https://www.ruh.nhs.uk/patients/services/clinical_depts/paediatrics/documents/patient_info/PAE027_Gluten_free_diets.pdf
• Oats - don’t have gluten but high risk of contamination, can get gluten free oats 
• Only GF breads & flours available on prescription 
• Check DEXA (osteoporosis risk) 1y after starting GFD esp if >55 or other RFs, assess FRAX, calculate calcium intake & supplement Vit D 
• Vaccination - 5yrly pneumococcal due to hyposplenism & encapsulated bacteria risk (as well as Men B, Men ACWY, HiB/Men C) - Green Book!

Monitoring
• Annual review (dietician, Sx, height/weight, TTG)
• Consider DEXA & bloods (FBC, LFT/bone, vit D, haematinics), screening for other AI (TFT, glucose)
• Specialist may repeat Bx 1y to ensure histological recovery - refer if TTG still high after 1y of GFD or persistent Sx /concerns for increased risk of Non-Hodgkin lymphoma /colorectal/ SB Ca

Non-Coeliac gluten sensitivity 
• Symptoms with gluten but negative duodenal Bx
• Poorly understood 
• Consider wheat allergy (IgE mediated “bakers rhinitis” specifically related to wheat products)

Dermatitis herpetiformis  
• Itchy raised blistering rash on extensors, rare but pathognomonic of Coeliac (DDx papular ezcema, psoriasis, scabies, pemphigus)
• Test IgA TTG & refer for skin Bx 
• If confirmed = GFD & derm may use dapsone for symptomatic relief .
