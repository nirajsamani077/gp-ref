Liver cirrhosis RW 

End stage liver disease with chronic inflammation & fibrosis which is initially compensated then decompensated (jaundice, HSM, ascites, varicies/bleeding, encephalopathy)
• PPx = liver damage causes increased portal pressure with shunting to systemic (splanchic) circulation causing bleeding, ammonia toxin BBB, low protein levels in blood and pressures cause fluid loss into bowel cavity & clotting factors)
• Soft signs = spider naevi, caput medusae, gynaecomastia, palmar erythema 

LFT & US abdo can be normal - only sensitive if severe, need specific Fibroscan or ELF test based on RFs for common causes eg ETOH (screening)
• FIB4 score - Age, AST, ALT, platelets to decide whether to refer for ELF/Fibroscan (NAFLD score also has BMI & HbA1C)
• ELF (Enhanced liver fibrosis) blood test measures 3 markers to give score, if >10.51 high risk for advanced fibrosis 
• Fibroscan - Transient elastography, “stiffness” of liver using USS, 8-16+ KPA suggests advanced fibrosis or cirrhosis
• When thinking about who to test use SCAN 
   - Suspect liver disease even if normal bloods 
   - C - Hepatitis C history 
   - Alcohol XS >50 units men >35 units women/w
   - NAFLD with FIB4 >1.3-2.67 or ELF >10.51

Common causes - ETOH, NAFLD, viral hepatitis BC
Rarer - AI (PBC, PSC, AIH), HH/Wilson’s/A1AT, CA
Drugs - MTX, amiodarone, statins, valproate etc 

Liver screen tests - FBC (plts low in fibrosis), AST, GGT, Hep B SAG, Hep C antibody, AMA/ANA/SMA/Igs, ferritin/TSAT, caeruloplasmin, A1AT. Split BR (direct/conjugated) if raised BR. Synthetic function (alb, BR, INR) + US abdo 
• AST:ALT >1 suggests advanced fibrosis ((>2 suggests etoh, <1 suggests viral)
• BR unconjugated: Gilbert’s, haemolysis. Conjugated: CBD obstruction, parenchyma disease 

Management of cirhosis 
• Refer liver team as soon as suspected 
• Management of UC (obesity, ETOH)
• Immunisation - annual flu, one off pneumococcal, Hep B/A, COVID 
• HCC (US, AFP 6 monthly & varicies (OGD at least 3 yearly) surveillance (Bb propranolol for primary prevention of portal HTN, if OGD not required) 
• Treatment of specific comps 
   - Ascites - Paracentesis, R/O/ Rx SBP 
   - Varicies - Banding
   - Encephalopathy - Grade, lactulose, rifaxamin, and identify trigger (SBP infection etc), never stop lactulose in hx of encephalopathy, clears ammonia 
   - Other: Portal vein thrombosis, HRS, infection
• Statins and para safe in compensated cirhosis, but avoid NSAID & ACE if ascites (risk of AKI) 

Autoimmune liver 
• PBC = F>>M usually post menopause, often ++ ALT & GGT (cholestatic), positive AMA & IgM. Treat with UDOCA (ursodeoxycholic acid) & life expectancy normal if blood markers improve 
• AIH = F>M any age, transaminitis, positive ANA SMA & IgG. Treat with pred, good prognosis 
• PSC = M>F middle age, associated with UC, cholestatic picture no specific test, often cholangitis/pruritic jaundice, premalignant (1%/y cholangiocarcinoma & colorectal Ca risk) & worse prognosis. Treatment with UDOCA & steroids/DMARDs but may need liver Tx 
• Pruritis (itch) common feature, treatment = 
   - Cholestyramine 4mg before & after breakfast bile acid seqrestant
   - 2nd line = rifampicin, sertraline, naltrexone
