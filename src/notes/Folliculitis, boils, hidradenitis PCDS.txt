Folliculitis, boils, hidradenitis PCDS

Folliculitis 
• Inflamed tender papules/pustules/nodules of blocked hair follicles (superficial may be due to infection, deep with nodules usually infection) & can cause scarring alopecia - usually staph 
• NB pseudofolliculitis from shaving with reaction from ingrown hair or occlusion from creams can mimic superficial folliculitis (only needs chlorhexidine antiseptic body wash & stop trigger)
• Rx = Mild usually self limiting with chlorhexidine antiseptic body wash otherwise oral fluclox 7–10d (clary if PA - same as cellulitis staph treatment) 
• Specific types 
   - Hot tub folliculitis = triad of folliculitis, axillary LN & breast tenderness usually caused by pseudomonas (swab) - Rx = cipro
   - Folliculitis barbae = beard folliculitis associated with nasal MRSA so do swab and clear MRSA chlorhexidine body wash and mupirocin 
   - Malasezzia folliculitis = yeast folliculitis associated with dandruff/seb derm, use Nizoral shampoo daily for 5d then step down twice weekly 
   
Boil = Furuncle, Carbuncle = collection of boils 
• Collected infection/abscess under skin, Rx as above but consider need for acute I&D
• Multiple boils = Check DM/IC & considering Hidradenitis but can start treatment if suspect (only swab if DC, if staph check PVL status)

Hidradenitis suppurativa
• Recurrent abscesses in multiple sites (axilla, neck, groin, under breast, perianal), >=2 in 6mo, which can form sinus tracts & irreversible scarring 
• Hurley classification 
   - Stage 1 = just abscesses 
   - Stage 2 = sinus tracts and scarring 
   - Stage 3 = diffuse interconnecting
• Refer derm as risk of scarring and can start ABx treatment at same time 
   - Mild = Topical clindamycin 1% BD LT or..
   - Mod/Sev = Lymecycline 408mg OD (can increase to BD if BMI >30/no response to OD after 12w) or Doxy 100mg BD initially 12w but can be used LT if needed (annual FBC UE LFT)
   - Therefore review all 12w after starting - and usual point of referral to derm who might start biologics (eg infliximab) or systemic retinoids 
• Other considerations = chlorhexidine body wash for self management (but not related to poor hygeine), metformin (off licence adjunct), dressings (absorbent & non-adhesive), refer if abscess needs I&D and stage 3 may need full surgical excision or laser therapy
