Talipes equinovarus (clubfoot) RW

Inward turning plantarflexed feet (easy to spot “in & under” sole rotated posteromedially) - checked in 6-8 week baby check & NIPE
• True talipes is not correctable & is congenital 
• Positional talipes is correctable & only needs PT

1/1000 M>>F, 50% bilateral 
Mostly idiopathic but can be related to cerebral palsy, spina bifida, Edwards syndrome 

If suspecting = refer early to paeds for 
• Ponsetti method - progressive casting and manipulation of foot usually from first weeks of life +/- Achilles tendonotomy - then keep “boots and bar” shoes keeping in position at 3mo, then night time brace until aged 4
• Usually resolves unless non compliant with treatment so very important to educate parents .
