Vulnerable adult safeguarding (SG) RW 

2 categories common- care home/dementia & those with learning disabilities 

Consider & suspect model similar to child SG 
• If considering d/w SG lead, CH manager and information gather, social referral /adult SG referral if concerned 
• If suspecting (serious concerns) act to keep vulnerable person safe (admission if needed) & refer as above 

I have a responsibility to report these concerns /safeguarding, legally etc 

Abuse signs 
• Physical abuse -Unexplained /repeated injuries, burns/scalds including cigarette burns, grip or strangulation marks, inappropriate restraint/medicating, change in behaviour - if concerned for physical assault call police 
• Sexual abuse - Sexualised/change in behaviour, relationship without capacity to consent fof sex or signs of sexual assault /STI / pregnancy
• Psychological abuse - addressed rudely, shouted at, humiliated, denies access/rights 
• Financial/material abuse - loss of access to money/possessions, money being spent oddly, can’t consent to getting married/civil partnership 
• Neglect - lack of personal care/hygeine, malnourished, not given meds, recurrent DNA, denied access to food drink aids, not kept away from hazards or supervised appropriately 
• Organisational abuse - Unclear policies, leadership, governance, SG pathways/concerns not escalated, poor staffing or quality of care, financial mismanagement (of organisation & patient), physical overt signs - escalate up & up & up as CH manager may be part of this 

In those with LD similar factors along with social isolation, hate and “mate” crime/cuckooing (criminal gangs befriending someone with LD and using property to sell drugs) - refer adult SG.
