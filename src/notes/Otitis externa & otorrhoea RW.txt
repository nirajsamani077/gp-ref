Otitis externa & otorrhoea RW

Most common cause of ear discharge (otorrhoea) with DDx 
• AOM with perf - discharge after pain + URTI
• FBS 
• Fungal (2%) or malignant (IC) OE
• Cholesteatoma - attic crust SNHL CN7, look well 

Outer ear infection with pain d/c itch and CHL if narrowed canal, swimmers ezcema IC hearing aids 
• Tragal tenderness, narrowed inflamed canal discharge debris, TM normal of difficult to see (so often re review 2-4w) 

Treatment 
• Para NSAIDs cold flannel to ear , keep ear dry 
• Ear calm (acetic acid) OTC 1 spray TDS 7d if mild  contains vinegar acts as mild antibacterial & fungal 
• Otomize spray (dex + neomycin) 1 spray TDS 7d steroid and abx but ensure no perf (although evidence if only ototoxic if >2w use or repeated)
• If more ezcema betnesol or prednesol 2-3 drop 2-3hrly til relief 
• Cipro 0.3% 0.25ml BD 7d in TM perf, or ciprodex as alternative , sofradex by specialist 
• Swab and consider antifungal 1% clotrimazole or pseudomonal - if RV after 7d not improving repeat spray or drops again further 7d then if not improving swab and refer , so only after 14d topical
• If considering Po abx should be sending in ?malignant OE, they may use ear wick ribbon gauze with drops soaked in and keep canal open & follow up in hot clinic (also refer if drops won’t go in needing microsuction or clearance of debris or complete stenosis) to ENT emergency clinic
