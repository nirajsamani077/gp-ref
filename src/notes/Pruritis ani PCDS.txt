Pruritis ani PCDS 

4 causes 
• Poor hygeine - too much too little cleansing 
• Inflammation - ezcema, contact, lichen sclerosis
• Infection - Threadworm (itching worse at night, thin white worms, usually children, mebendazole (Ovex) 100mg one off dose (may repeat dose after 2w to kill residual eggs) + dose for household + hygiene)
• LGI pathology - haemorrhoids, skin tags, IBD and anorectal CA (so all need DRE) 

Dermatitis ladder, consider daktacort, can have short term potent steroid, emollient morning & post defecation

Streptococcal perianal infection can occur in children usually aged 3-5y but primary school - causes by GAS - causing ++ pain on BO, erythema, fissures & itching - if suspecting take a perianal swab MC&S & if confirms GAS = Coamox/clary PO 7-10d 

So consider threadworm and streptococcal in all children with pruritis ani & red flags in older!
