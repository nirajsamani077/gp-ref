Antidepressants 101 RW

Psychological treatments just as effective, and usually first-line for most conditions 

Options
• SSRIs usually first line 
• SNRI (duloxetine, venlafaxine)
• Mirtazapine 

Consider risks of SSRI esp in elderly 
• GI bleeding risk (NSAID, aspirin, DOAC) - ?PPI, offer mirtazipine instead 
• Hyponatraemia (SIADH)
• Falls and fractures 
• Weight gain 
• Sexual dysfunction /ED
• Prolonging QTc (citalopram/escitalopram), think other drugs eg TCA, venlafaxine, lithium, erythromycin, cipro, fluconazole, amiodarone, quinine 
• Interaction with triptans (serotonin syn) - offer mirtazipine instead 

Choices 
• Sertraline & citalopram fewest interactions 
• Fluoxetine & paroxetine most interactions
• Paroxetine & venlafaxine shortest half life (so highest risk of discontinuation symptoms)
• Fluoxetine longest half life (so can’t cross taper)
• SNRI and mirtazipine may be most effective but have highest risks and OD risk 
• Mirtazipine causes most weight gain, most sedation (esp at lower dose 7.5mg) & less sexual dysfunction than SSRI, also most serotoninergic
• Secondary care - vortioxetine, TCA, MAOI, 2x primary care agents 

Specific conditions if needs drugs 
• Depression - SSRI, if more severe can jump to SNRI or mirtazipine, after 4w treatment increase dose or switch (can take 6-8w see full effect, consider increase if no effect)
• GAD - SSRI (usually sertraline), if failed switch to alternate SSRI/SNRI, only if failed then pregabalin 
• Panic - SSRI (any), if failed other SSRI or TCA (imipramine or clomipramine) off license 
• Social anxiety - SSRI (usually sertraline or escitalopram)
• OCD - SSRI (any) often higher doses longer duration, may need switch TCA or antipsychotic (secondary care) 
• Body dysmorphic - SSRI (fluoxetine) often higher doses, as for OCD
• PTSD - SSRI (sertraline or paroxetine) or venlafaxine, antipsychotics in secondary care 

Typical review after starting 
• 2-4 weeks but 1 week if <30 or suicide concerns
• Then monthly first 3 months 
• 3-6 monthly thereafter 
• When first starting may have nausea (usually worst when hungry, eat a snack), dry mouth, heightened anxiety, rarely agitation (try to avoid BDZ for this) & suicidality 
• Normally look to stop when well & stable for 6mo (12mo for GAD, OCD, BDD as higher risk)

Withdrawal effects (paroxetine, venlafaxine)
• Usually start after few days of changing/stopping
• Anxiety, agitation, GI upset, sweating 
• Different from relapse usually months after stop 
• Usually lasts 2w but can be longer 
• Reduce risk by slower tapering of dose (months, slower if taking for longer), only fluoxetine every other day due to longer half life 

Switch = 
• SSRI /venlafaxine (not fluoxetine) to SSRI / venlafaxine (not fluoxetine) = after taper stop and then start new one next day 
• Fluoxetine usually stop leave gap 4-7d before starting new one (can’t cross taper ) 
• Mirtazipine starting or switching to SSRI /venlafaxine can usually cross taper (apart from fluoxetine)
• Too rapid switching can cause serotonin syndrome (delirium, profuse swearing, ANS, myoclonus)
