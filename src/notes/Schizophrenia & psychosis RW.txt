Schizophrenia & psychosis RW

Disorder of perception causing severe mental illness and social withdrawal - cause of suicide!
- “Experiencing the outside world abnormally, not able to distinguish what’s real & what’s not”
- “Brain issue but no specific test, strong FHx”

Diagnosis = Persistent (>1mo) 1 positive 1 negative impacting on function & lack of insight 
• Positive sx - Auditory hallucinations, paranoid delusions, thought disorder (can’t follow, flight of ideas etc)
• Negative sx - Social withdrawal, blunt affect, apathy, loss of speech/engagement, catatonia

Refer urgently all suspected psychosis to CMHT & crisis team if acute psychosis imminent risk to self or others 
• Early referral for ?prodrome “emerging psychosis” or first presentation of psychosis if unexplained decline, borderline psychotic sx, abnormal behaviour or strong FHx (10%) - although 4/5 times symptoms resolve 

Work up - usually in secondary care 
• Organic cause - baseline bloods esp TFT, B12, HIV/BBV, syphilis serology, NMDA receptors (encephalitis) & urine tox 
• Before antipsychotic - FBC for clozapine (but usually if failed others), BMI/waist to height, HbA1C/lipids, PRL, ECG, QRISK 

Management - usually in secondary care 
• Starting on antipsychotic & monitoring, usually shared care agreement after 12mo stable & should be continued at least 2y post remission - SE weight gain metabolic EPES hyperprolactin anticholinergic & CVD/VTE risks 
• CBT, family therapy and psychosocial
• Ensure crisis planning, relapse prevention plan and annual review (above + adherence, healthy lifestyle & employment support programmes)
