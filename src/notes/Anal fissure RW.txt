Anal fissure RW

Common & painful (1 in 10 over lifetime) 

Longitudinal tear in squamous lining of distal anus, usually constipation related (also IBD/STI) causing pain & fresh PR bleeding 

Acute < 6w = usually resolves with analgesia 5% lidocaine gel just before BO unlicensed) & stool softeners (Movicol, docusate), can use antispasmodics (buscopan, mebeverine) for post defecation proctalgia

Chronic >6w = consider GTN ointment (0.4%) Bd 6w to relax muscles and reduce pain (50% get headaches which usually settle but if not consider unlicensed Diltazem ointment 2% much more expensive (£120 Vs £30 per 30g tube) & refer if not improving (Botox, surgery (lateral sphincterotomy), more sinister cause eg IBD
