Inhalers & carbon footprint (vs inspiratory effort needed) RW

Preventing admissions and good control = critical 
• SABA >3/y, ICS <3/y or exacs = poor control 

Consider SMART inhalers (SIT-AIR, SIT-MART) as less inhalers = less carbon footprint. Only if >12 

DPI (dry powder inhalers) better than pMDI (pressurised metered dose inhalers /puffers) for carbon footprint but usually need more inspiratory effort which can be affected by COPD/asthma 

Inspiratory effort screen - breathe in quick and deep over 3s - if can’t will need to use pMDI or soft mist (only COPD LAMA/LABA Spiolto Respimat), can check formally with InCheck Dial G16

If need pMDI then use lower carbon footprint ones eg Salamol or Airomir rather than Ventolin Evohaler/ Symbicort pMDI which have highest footprint 

When disposing - recycle (best), don’t dispose in normal household waste, incinerate by handing back into pharmacy is an ok option
