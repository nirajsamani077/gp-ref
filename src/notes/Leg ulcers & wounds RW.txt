Leg ulcers & wounds RW

Common - 1% of population, 50% don’t heal @6m
• Venous ulcers (80% of leg) - related to VV, DVT, obesity, pregnancy, usually gaiter area shallow sloughy with other varicose ezcema changes (Rx = compression, elevation & surgery for VV)
• Arterial ulcers (20% of leg, 20% of foot) - PVD (DM, smoking), pressure points punched out deep irregular necrotic with lack of pulses/IC symptoms (Rx = check ABPI, refer vascular, clopidogrel)
• Neuropathic (80% of foot) - DM, trauma, pressure, usually sole or toe tips, deep with minimal sensation (Rx = remove pressure, DM control & podiatry) 
• Mixed ulcers are most common in reality!

Assessment = NVI, infection & footwear. ABPI!

Compression stockings & elevation 
• <0.8 contraindicates compression stockings (although not absolute unless <0.5 - only grade 1 few layers or inelastic wraps, anything 0.5-0.8 should probably be under specialist)

Dressings 
• Liase with practice nurse & use local ICB formulary, consider whether adherent or non-adherent, absorbable or less, frequency of changing & DNs 

Infection - pain, cellulitis signs, discharge, fever  
• 1st-line = Fluclox 500mg-1g QDS 7-10d (PA = clary 500mg BD or doxy, if pregnant erythro)
• Don’t swab at first time, only if not improving, common for wound colonisation 
• 2nd-line = Coamox 500/125mg TDS 7-10d (PA = cotrimoxazole) 
• Refer acutely if systemic, NV compromise, IC, signs of osteomyelitis/nec fasc -need MRI & IV ABx 
• If not improving base off swab & refer ulcer clinic 

New drug for venous ulcers = pentoxifylline 400mg TDS under specialist 6mo off license to improve healing process Bb

Wound assessment 
• TIME = Tissue viable? Infection? Moisture? Edge?
• Avoid swabbing unless failed initial Abx treatment 
• Biofilm = layer of colonised bacteria and protein gelatinous substance it produces, makes wound healing much harder but can mimic infection 
• Refer tissue viability team to assess fully .
