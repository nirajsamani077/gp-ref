HIV RW

Transmission from body fluids (sex, anal riskiest), needle sharing, Bt, mother to baby (vertical) - not spread by things like kissing or social interaction. 

Offer testing in high prevalence area, high risk groups (MSM, IVDU, Sex workers, Prisoners, if from country with high prevalence >1% - consider testing annually), certain conditions associated with AIDS (TB, Kaposi, oesophageal Candida) or non-AIDS chronic illness (eg malignancy, chronic infection, seb derm/psoriasis)

Serocoversion in 2-4w post contact ,most infective 
• Fever, myalgia, glandular fever type, LN
• Oral ulcers, macpap rash 

Use BHIVA for guidance on risks 
100,000 with HIV in UK, nearly 10% undiagnosed 

Refer Umbrella but can do rapid POC tests in community (finger prick) checking HIV Ab needing 3 month period since exposure for positive or formal lab serology with HIV Ab and p24 antigen which can be positive in half the time
• Normalise test, ensure to R/O state is voluntary and if positive effective treatment earlier better 

If positive all need anti retroviral treatment regardless of CD4 count to have undetectable viral load (transmissibility) which means near normal life expectancy but associated risk with CVD/resp/renal/bone disease 

See antiretrovirals section - ART. Lifelong treatment not a cure but a treatment which prevents comps
