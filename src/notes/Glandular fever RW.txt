Glandular fever RW

Kissing disease (EBV at UNI)
Fever cervical LN prolonged pharyngitis triad 
Splenomegaly, palatal petechiae, deranged LFT/abnormal FBC (lymphocytosis, thrombocytopenia, sometimes haemolytic anaemia), rarely GBS/ME

Don’t need to test if mild & typical story- usually self limiting within the month, bed rest fluids analgesia avoid contact sports/impact risk of splenic rupture 

If not then test with FBC + Monospot (high risk group check HIV, CMV, Toxo)
• If Monospot neg and no lymphocytosis is not glandular fever, if neg but lymphocytosis check EBV serology (IgM)
