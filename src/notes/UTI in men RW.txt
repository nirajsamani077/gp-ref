UTI in men RW

Send all for MC&S, at least 7d ABx - UTI is rare in men so if >1 consider USS, referral to urology 

Ensure any micro haematuria resolved post ABx - refer 2WW if non resolved >60

If catheter UTI 7d broader spec & consider changing catheter if not inserted within 7d before starting Abx ideally 

1st line is still TMP 200mg Bd or nitro MR 100mg BD 7 days (check renal fx), 2nd line cef or if organism sensitive co-amox or cipro (tendon rupture risk) 

Consider prostatitis (90% men with febrile UTI have prostate involvement!)
• Nitrofurantoin not effective for prostatitis as doesn’t reach therapeutic levels in prostate
