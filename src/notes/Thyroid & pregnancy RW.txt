Thyroid & pregnancy RW

Hypothyroidism 
• If pregnant or planning pregnancy thyroxine doses need to be increased (& subclinical hypothyroidism needs to be treated) as increase T3/4 needed to meet demands & increased TBG which mops up active T3/4 so more needed 
• Increase dose by 20-30% which is usually by 
   - 25mcg if thyroxine dose was <100mcg
   - 50mcg if thyroxine dose was >100mcg
• Monitor TSH every month & aim TSH <2.5 low normal, after delivery reduce back to pre pregnancy normal after rechecking @ 6w 

Hyperthyroidism 
• If WCBA planning pregnancy on carbamizole need to speak to specialist as teratogenic esp in 1st TM so change to propylthiouracil & specialist to manage (may change back after 1st TM & carbamizole safer in BF)
• Significant risk of IUGR, PET, stillbirth 
• Avoid any radioactive iodine 6mo prior to conceiving 

Autoimmune & post-partum thyroiditis 
• Check thyroid autoantibodies - can occur new 
• Post-partum thyroiditis common esp if high risk Hx (FHx, previous, AI Hx) so if symptomatic (may stop milk production) or high risk - check TSH 
  - Suppressed - Check TRAB to R/O Graves, if negative = post partum thyroiditis, if T4 raised = overt, manage with propranolol only and repeat again 1-2mo 
  - Raised - if T4 low overt, only give thyroxine if symptomatic, BF or TSH >10 (or planning another pregnancy!) & continue for 12mo at least, monitor TFT every 1-2mo 
  - Normal - may not be thyroid related but repeat again in 1-2mo if suspicion as may not be sensitive 
• Usually triphasic -3mo thyrotoxic, 3-6mo hypothyroid then euthyroid resolves (although 30% remain hypothyroid long term)
• Higher risk for future thyroid disease so annual TSH should be checked, esp risk around conception & not correcting hypothyroidism!.
