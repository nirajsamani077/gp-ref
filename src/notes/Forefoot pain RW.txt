Forefoot pain RW

Big toe (medial forefoot)
• Hallux valgus (bunion)
• Hallux limitus /rigidus (OA 1st MTP)
• Sprain (turf toe), synovitis , sesamoiditis 

Other toes (lateral forefoot)
• Metatarsalgia (synovitis)
• Morton’s neuroma 
• Stress fracture 

Bunion = lateral angulation of inturned big toe, May coexist with OA, medial tip bursitis on shoes , sometimes FHx. Need roomy broad shoes, bunion pads, orthotics and toe spreaders, if severe or painful/recurrent XR & refer 

OA 1st MTP = stiffness, use hiking boots, ortotic physio steroid injection fusion surgery 

Sesamoiditis = plantar pain during toe off walking due to inflammation of accessory bone that lies just below 1st MT head, cushioned insoles 

1st MTP sprain (turf toe) = acute hyperextension injury (usually sporting/astroturf), refer OTS may need boot time off sport orthotics 

Metatarsalgia = “ball of foot” pain, maybe synovitis, pain like walking on pebbles with tenderness on plantar MT heads. Orthotics, padding, NSAIDs, avoid triggers 

Morton’s neuroma = benign fibrotic thickening of forefoot nerve causing pain, more common in women with high heels, usually 3rd webspace, like walking on pebble and better with shoes off. May be neuro sx. Footwear & MT pads, NSAIDs, if persistent consider USS, dome orthotic, steroid injection, surgery (plantar plate tear similar = thickening of MTP capsule with overload then tear)

Stress fracture = often overuse, often 2nd MT (ballet) or 5th MT metaphysis which is high risk, constant pain with tenderness, MRI best, needs stiff footwear activity mod sometimes surgery 

Toe issues 
• Mallet = distal flexion 
• Claw = prox extension distal flexion 
• Hammer = prox extension distal neutral (blunt)
• Footwear, pads, orthotics
