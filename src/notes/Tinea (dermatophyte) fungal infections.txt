Tinea (dermatophyte) fungal infections

Tinea capitis (scalp ringworm)
- scarring alopecia (remember alopecia areata non scarring) usually in children with itchy well-defined (usually poor defined in seb derm) annular lesion with outer red border sometimes 
- may form kerion (inflamed boggy mass with pustules/crust if untreated 
- scalp scrapings may show trichophyton (common) or microsporum from cats/dogs (green under woods lamp)
- topical ketoconazole shampoo 2wks to reduce transmission, oral terbinafine (trichophyton) or griseofulvin (microsporum)

Tinea corporis (ringworm)
- trichophyton infection causing limb/trunk annulled red lesions with central papules/pustules
- oral fluconazole 

Tinea pedis (athletes foot)
- itchy peeling skin between toes in teens 
- scrapings help diagnosis 
- mild - antifungal terbinafine or imidazole cream OTC +/- mild topical steroid for 7 days, if severe oral terbinafine
- foot hygiene, cotton socks, dry toes fully
