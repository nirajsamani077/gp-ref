Malnutrition & nutritional supplements Fortisips RW

Average needs = 
• Calories 30cal/kg/d (70kg = 2100cal)
• Fluid 30ml/kg/d (70kg = 2.1L)
• Protein 1g/kg/d (70kg = 70g)

Screen opportunistically for malnutrition (esp chronic disease, poverty, MH, frailty) = MUST
• BMI, weight loss (unexpected), acute illness 
• Max 2 points for BMI <18.5, WL >10% or illness 
• High risk = >=2 points = dietician assessment 
• Medium risk = 1 point = Food First approach 

Food First approach (natural food >>> supps)
• Little & often, multiple small meals, full fat & sugar, can add butter/cream, nourishing drinks 

If Food First failed then oral nutrition supps (ONS)
• Can be prescribed on repeat if specific conditions eg GI disease, chronic disease related malnutrition, prev surgery (gastrectomy)/comps 
• Best Buys OTC are Complan shakes & soups (~£1 per sachet) with good mix of calories protein 
• Never use alone, always need real food, not a replacement just a supplement, real food is better!
• If need to prescribe usually dietician will guide - 1.5kcal/ml is gold standard, use milk-based for better calories & protein, usually 1/ day as top up titrating up, give different flavours 1 week supply initially and then no more than 1 month supply each time, monitor every 3mo with BMI .
