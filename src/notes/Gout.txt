Gout 

Acute joint almost always big toe, middle aged obese men (probably cvs risk related) 
Usually quite significant overnight pain swelling 

Certain foods high protein (breaks down to purine to Uric acid), hops in beer

Big toe often as affects more degenerate joints, also cooler as more peripheral allowing crystals to crystallise 

Examine all - r/o septic joint 

Usually foot involvement if not big toe (mid foot ankle), rarely knee/polyarticular gout 

Colchicine specific for gout (can also give nsaid), allowed even in CKD (eGFR >15 reduced dose). Usually 500mcg BD-QDS up to 6g in one round (so 3d) - should give diagnostic clear response if gout 
NSAID can be given on top of colchicine 
Some people get D&V with colchicine, or if severe CKD give pred 15mg 5d 
RICE also 
Should improve in 24h, if no better or worse contact again ?sepsis 
F/U with uric acid level 3-4weeks after attack for all (will be low normal initially during attack) - if raised confirms gout. 
If 1st attack & low risk for recurrence counsel re allopurinol consider, otherwise give esp if recurrent 
Risk of long term joint damage (premature OA) and kidney stones for untreated gout 

Need to give allopurinol with colchicine cover as can trigger an attack when starting allopurinol (mobilising crystals)- titrate up from 100mg to normal urate, if they get gout initially means treatment is working). Higher risk acute sensitivity or SJS myelosuppression on initial high dose (300mg) which is why start low. Otherwise v well tolerated. 
Cardioneutral 

Febuxostat if can’t tolerate allopurinol or recurrent gout on allopurinol.
