Stress reaction, grief reaction & adjustment disorder RW

Extreme OOP depression/anxiety symptoms in response to acute stressor/multiple stressors 
• Impairs function, no relevant prior MH, doesn’t meet criteria for primary MH
• Cannot be explained by other factors or normal bereavement which is varied 

Still in increased risk of DSH & suicide so ask and risk assess

Good prognosis - usually symptoms resolve fully within 6mo of stressor going away & does not predict future MH problems 

Management = supportive & emotional listening, social support & problem solving/behavioural activation “microsteps” to returning to normal - counselling & continuity very helpful, use CBT framework of thoughts/emotions linking with and back to behaviours/physical symptoms 

Grief reaction specifics - “grief is normal” but abnormal if persistent distressing disabling symptoms >6mo from bereavement “prolonged grief disorder” may be disproportionate/not accepting reality or adjusting & occurs up to 10% of time - main intervention is CRUSE support from professionals with bereavement counselling and CBT/psychological intervention - screen closely for depression, PTSD (if violent traumatic death) & substance/ETOH

Signpost getselfhelp.co.uk & CRUSE  
In children The Family Bereavement Programme /The Edwards Trust good support .
