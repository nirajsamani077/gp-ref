Menopause & HRT counselling RW

Falling oestrogen levels once stop producing eggs /ovulating and having menstrual cycles/going through the change - happens for all women but body has gotten used to high levels oestrogen hormone and therefore drop in this can cause issues eg 
• Flushes, sweats, mood swings, poor sleep , fog 
• Poor libido, dryness discomfort down below 
• Higher risk of brittle bones (osteoporosis) and even circulatory problems like heart attack or strokes which are both also related to getting older 

Usually if >45 typical story don’t need tests but Ix
• Abnormal bleeding - check LMP cycles IMB PCB
• Atypical sx consider ETOH, stress, thyroid etc 
• Check FSH (x2 >30, hormone from brain which stimulates ovaries to make oestrogen which normally goes up when there is no oestrogen left) only if 
  - <45 = early menopause. <40 = POF 
  - >50 amenorrhoic on contraception, if 1x FSH >30 can stop contraception in 12mo 

Treatments lifestyle, hormone replacement therapy (HRT) which comes in tablets gels patches to replace that hormone along with another important hormone progesterone or some alternatives to HRT but first check a few things to see what might be suitable 
• LMP cycles brief Gynae & smears, bleeding issue
• Smoker, weight/BMI, other general health meds 
• History of blood clots (VTE) self or family
• History of breast CA in self or family 

It sounds as though you would qualify for HRT which could really help your symptoms though there are a few risks related to the questions I just asked you - would you like me to explain?
• Endometrial - if giving oestrogen just by itself can cause the lining of the womb to thicken up abnormally and increase risks of things like cancers but this is why we give a progesterone hormone along with the oestrogen which completely protects against this risk (can be in tablets or combined patch or Mirena coil which provides contraception too & can stay in for longer…matches your cycle if still having periods within last year where after you take progesterone for last 2 weeks of cycle like your body would normally do). But if bleeding on HRT need to be assessed quite quickly to rule this out.! 
• VTE - small increased, usually higher if history smoker etc, much higher if taking tablet version of oestrogen rather than patches /gels where risk is very very low . Similar story for CVD stroke where usually it is the tablets with risk, generally newer studies found to have slight benefit. Keep an eye out for sx like sob leg swelling etc 
• Breast Ca - small increased risk, moreso with combined than oestrogen only, lot of media from older trials like WHI and Million women study some of which debunked, risk is relative rather than absolute meaning that generally in all women the risk is low eg 3% increased to 5% so low numbers but risk itself is doubled, but other common RFs like alcohol, smoking, obesity and even not breastfeeding have a higher impact on risk - longer  use >10y higher risk & risk tends to last up to 10y after stopping, FHx generally if genetic component consider Alts, but generally safe for most, Breast aware , micronised prog better 
• Stroke - small increased risk usually those >65, much less so transdermal than oral (no evidence for MI), maybe Cardioproctive in younger group evidence unclear 

Alternatives 
• For flushing - Alternative natural remedies like black cohosh and soy have v limited evidence and unregulated, clonidine may also benefit but perhaps more troublesome s/E 
• For mood - traditional antidepressants (SSRI SNRI) and CBT 

Lifestyle for all - probably very important 
• Exercise, healthy routine, diet, sleep stress etoh 

Whichever option tried can review things 3-6mo
