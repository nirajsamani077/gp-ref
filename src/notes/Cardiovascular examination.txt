Cardiovascular examination

Position 
• 45deg chest exposed 

End of bed 
• Age, obesity 
• Obvious syndromes that can affect heart (Downs, Noonans, Turners, Marfans, Williams, Holt-Oram, Ank spond)
• Colour - malar flush, slate grey rash in photosensitive areas (amiodarone), bruising (anticoagulation for valve/AF)
• Chest wall (sternotomy scar and if present legs for venous grafting scar, thoracotomy scar lifting arms up as hidden in axilla, inframammary (lift up breast) for mitral valvulotomy, current/previous PPM/ICD, previous central access, listen to audible click for prosthetic valve (loudest sound = closure, click before carotid pulse = aortic and click after carotid pulse = mitral, if 2 clicks = double e  I’m g rheumatic/endocarditis), visible apical pulsation, gynaecomastia (spiro, dig)
• Clues around bed (O2) 

Nails and hands 
• Clubbing 
• Cyanosis 
• Tar staining
• IE signs (splinters, Janeway lesions, Oslers nodes) on nails and palms 

Pulse
• Feel both sides same time 30s
• Rate, rhythm, volume, character - radial then brachial onto collapsing 
• Collapsing pulse by holding proximal forearm/wrist hand flats with one hand and supporting elbow with other - lifting forearm after checking pain so it is parallel with arm and heart level - change of character with more prominent/water hammer/tapping = collapsing 
• Check for RR delay and if any deficit consider causes (CoA, cervical rib, subclavian stenosis, radial artery injury, AVF, Takayasu, Blalog-Taussig shunt in TOF with sternotomy + thoracotomy)
• Offer for RF delay 

Face 
• General - malar flush,  de Mussets sign (head bobbing in AR)
• Eyes - pallor/suffusion of conjunctiva, corneal arcus, xanthalesma, marfanoid changes (ectopia lentis, blue sclera, shimmering of iris)
• Mouth - cyanosis (central under tongue), poor dentition 

Neck 
• General - Plucked chicken skin of neck in pseudoxanthoma elasticum (cause of early CVD)
• JVP  - turn head slight left, vertical height over sternal angle >3cm = raised. If SR - Double pulsation = a wave prior and v wave with or after. HJ reflux if unsure of position - check pain, gentle RUQ pressure should rise JVP
• Carotids palpate - same as radial 

Chest inspection 
• Scars/line/PPM - as above - predominant in overall inspection. 

Chest palpation
• Apex - 5th ICS MCL. If felt use finger point for max impulse. If not tilt to LLP and or check on right (might be dextocardia). - check location (displaced?), character (
• 
• Expansion upper & lower chest 
• Tactile vocal fremitus - 99 with ulnar hand border across ribs upper mid and lower for feeling loss 

Chest percussion all zones anterior and posterior 
• Resonant, dull, stony dull, hyperesonant

Chest auscultation all zones anterior and posterior 
• BS - Vesicular, reduced, bronchial breathing 
• Added sounds (exp/insp) - wheeze (mono or polyphonic), creps (fine or coarse, early or late, cough clearing), pleural rub (creaking leathery sound)
• Prolonged expiratory phase (usually I:E = 1:2, if 1:3 or more sign of obstruction eg COPD)
• Vocal resonance - 99, increased over consolidation/fibrosis/reduced over air/collapse/effusion (whispering pectoriloqurey 99 whisper enhanced if increased) 

Ankles - for pedal oedema in cor pulmonale 

To complete examination 
- O2 saturations, vitals 
- PEFR/bedside Spirometry 
- Sputum, CXR if available
