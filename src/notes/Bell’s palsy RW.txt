Bell’s palsy RW

Isolated LMN facial (CN7) palsy = affecting forehead whereas UMN (stroke) forehead sparing 
• Droop, sag in mouth with dribbling, unable to close eye or raise eyebrow (if able to it’s not Bells, it’s UMN!), numbness, loss of taste, hyperacuisis 

Cause - by definition usually idiopathic but  
• Ramsey Hunt - CN7 VZV with rash in ear (use aciclovir only in this situation)
• MS - Bilateral /recurrent, other neuro
• Parotid tumour - IL hearing loss/otalgia, lump 
• Children/IC/infection = refer urgent as atypical 

Management 
• Pred 50mg 10d if presents within 72h - increases chances of full recovery with less comps 
• Aciclovir only if suspected Ramsey Hunt
• Eye care - pads/taping, lubricating drops to prevent exposure keratopathy, ref ophthalmology
• Refer ENT if not improved at all on pred after 3w period (most improve fully by 3mo but small proportion have residual weakness & need plastics so refer usually 3-6mo)
