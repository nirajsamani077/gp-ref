Angina /IHD RW 

Any recent onset /new or rest pain = R/O ACS 
• >15mins, diaphoresis, NV, SOB, jaw/arm radiation
• Current pain - Call 999/urgent admission, 300mg aspirin & GTN spray 1 puff 5mins then 1 puff. 
• Pain free now - Try to get ECG but if not urgent admission, don’t delay, steps as above 

If typical stable angina story (typical pain, triggered by exertion and relieved by rest or GTN after 5mins) with CV RFs 
• Arrange ECG & bloods (HbA1C, lipids) +/- CXR
• Start aspirin 75mg and give GTN spray 
• Refer to rapid access chest pain (RACP) clinic for dynamic/confirmatory tests
 - Exercise ECG, stress echo, myocardial perfusion scan (SPECT), cardiac MRI depending on likelihood 
 - If suspected rather than definite angina (history not typical) would have CTCA (NPV = 99.5%) 
   • No CAD = not angina, no further cardiac Ix
   • Non obstructive CAD = start Rx/secondary prev
   • Obstructive CAD = above, if LMS/TVD refer for formal angio & consider PCI/CABG 

Lifestyle (secondary prev) - smoking, weight, ETOH, diet exercise, BP, diabetes 

Drug treatment for angina 
• Secondary prev - aspirin (clopidogrel only if other vascular disease eg CVA PVD), atorva 80mg, ACE
• Symptomatic = GTN for all (1 spray under tongue, wait 5 mins, 1 spray again, if not relieving call 999)
  - Bb or rate limiting CCB (prefer Bb, never both)
     • Atenolol 25-50mg up to 100mg
     • Diltazem or Verapamil MR
  - Add DHP CCB (amlodipine) to Bb 
  - Specialist decide next steps (refer cardiology) 
     • Long acting nitrates (ISMN) 
     • Nicorandil - risk oesophageal/skin/eye ulcers 
     • Ivabradine 
     • Ranolazine
     • Elective PCI/CABG 

Nitrates - tolerance occurs with SR regimens, use asymmetric dose regimen (>10hr washout period)

NB: Takotsubo CM “broken heart” apical ballooning of heart with coronary spasm due to acute emotion/stress mimicking MI/AHF with ECG changes trop/BNP rise but normal coronaries on angio - supportive Rx & 95% return to normal LV function within 3w (ACE Bb MRA can reduce cardiac load short term & can usually be stopped), may have serial echo’s
