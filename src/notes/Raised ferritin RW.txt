Raised ferritin RW

Males >300, females >200

First check FBC, LFT, CRP and TSAT (Fe/TIBC x100) on iron studies 

If raised TSAT (>50% M, >40% F) ?iron overload 
• If normal FBC refer to gastro (liver)
• If abnormal FBC refer to haematology & check HFE gene 

If normal TSAT unlikely iron overload 
• Consider malignancy, inflammation, infection, obesity, ETOH as a cause 
• If ferritin >1000 refer to gastro (liver) 
• If ferritin <1000 give advice re lifestyle (weight loss, ETOH, improving BP/diabetes) and repeat ferritin + TSAT in 3-6 months & if still high refer to gastro (liver)
