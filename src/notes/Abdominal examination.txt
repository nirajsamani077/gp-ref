Abdominal examination 

Position 
• Flat, abdo exposed

End of bed 
• Age, obesity 
• Colour - jaundice, anaemia 
• Visible fistula 
• Abdomen - scars, stomas, ascites, PEG
• Comps of treatment (steroid signs) 
• Clues around bed (pumps, machines, bracelet)

Nails 
• Clubbing (cirrhosis, IBD)
• Leuconychia (white nails - hypoalbuminaemia) 
• Kolionychia (spoon shaped - IDA)
• Mee’s/Beau’s lines (transverse lines, ESRF)

Hands 
• Palmar erythema 
• Palmar crease pigmentation (vitiligo, Addisons)
• Dupytrens 
• Asterixis in hepatic encephalopathy (hands out)

Pulse and ask for BP (postural if ?Addisons)

Face 
• General - Cushingoid from steroids, systemic sclerosis (microstomia, telangectasia), 
• Eyes - pallor of conjunctiva (anaemia, pull eyelid down whilst looking up), scleral icterus (jaundice, pull eyelid up whilst looking down)
• Mouth - oral aphthous ulcers (IBD, SLE), lip pigmentation (Addisons, Peutz Jeughers) or lip telangectasia (Osler Weber Rendu), macroglossia (b12/iron deficiency, amyloid, acromegaly), oral hairy leukoplakia/candidiasis (HIV AIDS signs), gum hypertrophy (ciclosporin) 

Neck, back and chest - sit forwards 
• Spider naevi (SVC distribution, >5 = pathological, main cause CLD but also hormonal due to XS oestrogen eg pregnancy, pill etc) 
• LN - especially left supraclavicular (Virchows node = Trosiers sign or disseminated UGI malignancy), offer to examine axillary LN
• Axillary acanthosis nigricans (DM insulin resistance, thyroid, Cushings, acromegaly, obesity, PNP)
• Gynaecomastia in men (palpable breast tissue - CLD, renal disease, thyroid, spironolactone, hCG secreting malignancy, idiopathic) 

Abdomen inspection closer 
• Scars - especially at flanks for nephrectomy
• Stomas 
• PEG
• Distension/ascites 
• Caput medusae 
• PD catheter 
• Raise head slightly to see any obvious hernia or divarification of recti 

Abdomen palpation 
• Tell first, ask if any pain and start away, if any pain will stop, kneel down to level, look at face 
• Palpate 9 areas superficial and deep for any tenderness or masses 
• Liver - hand radial border parallel to costal margin starting RIF up to RUQ. Move up during inspiration breathe in & out. If enlarged calculate finger breaths and if border smooth/craggy, tender or pulsatile. Then percuss from RIF upwards along midclavicular line until liver dullness and then down from top, upper border normally 5th IC space. 
• Spleen - hand from RIF to LUQ, advancing on inspiration, using finger tips, moves down on inspiration, cannot get above or ballot (unlike palpable left kidney), if palpable use left hand to fix it along 10th rib line and feel for notch + percuss along same line for dullness. If not palpable roll onto right (1/3 way to you) and then repeat (at same time as rolling for shifting dullness) 
• Kidneys - hand behind inside lateral border of rectus and anterior hand on CPA, palpating down on anterior hand to “ballot” kidney between hands - if ballotable = enlarged 
• Bladder - suprapuic + percuss for dullness 
• AA - between umbillicus and epigastrium with 2 hands and fingers together 
• Shifting dullness for ascites - percuss midline (should be resonant) then across to left for dullness from fluid, then roll towards you wait for 15s then percuss in same spot and should be resonant as fluid has moved = ascites of 1.5L. Then palpate and percuss LUQ/flank for mild splenomegaly 

Abdomen auscultation
• Bowel sounds (best over RIF) = reduced, normal, tinkling
• Over liver/epigastrium for venous hum associated with portal HTN and arterial bruit (CA/haemangioma) or rubs (abscess)
• Over kidneys for RAS - renal artery bruit diagonally above umbillicus 4cm each side 

Ankles - for pedal oedema in hypoalbuminaemia, nephrotic syndrome or UL obstructions
