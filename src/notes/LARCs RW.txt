LARCs RW

Implant (Nexplanon) 
• Radioopaque SD small matchstick size rod for 3y progestagenic (etongestrel), costs £80 approx, can be used until 55, very effective, reversible fertility, same C/I UKMECS as POP (breast Ca liver mainly) , probably most effective 
• Rare issues with insertion (pain infection etc)
• Common for unscheduled bleeding (6/10 have some change, 2/10 much heavier, 3/10 amen), can use COCP on top (and even POP off licence)
• Can fit first 5d cycle , post partum 3w post top 1w or quick start, precautions 7d with preg testing 

Injectable /depot (Depo Provera)
• DMPA 12 weekly /3 monthly , UKMEC breast Ca liver also consider CVD risk costs around £6 per injection, IM but Sayana Press is SC version better in those with obesity or wanting to self inject 
• Should review after 2y use, Lt risks of osteoporosis (reversible)
• Most amenorrhoeic so better for bleeding but can be 3mo moreso, can delay up till 14w off licence (can reduce to 10wkly if bleeding towards end), COCP 3mo if needed 
• Some fertility reversal delay up to 1y
• Consider less good >45yo consider alt 

Mirena IUS /Levosert IUS 
• V effective for HMB and good contraceptive, licensed up to 5y and til 55 if inserted after 45 (but need change every 5y if part of HRT), off licence is 6y for contraception otherwise 
• Small T shaped plastic rod, thins endometrium stopping implantation and reducing heavy bleeding and pain. 52mg LNG. Thickens mucus. Doesn’t inhibit ovulation 
• Kyleena Jadess smaller only for contraception, easier to fit , maybe less effective/amen 
• Risks - v rare uterine perf, more common expulsion checking threads 1/20 moreso first few days after, infection (PID). Usually v well tolerated apart from first 3mo bleeding erratic tends to settle 
• Helpful in lots of conditions even endometrial hyperplasia without Atypia
• If lost threads main causes expulsion, perf or pregnancy so need to r/o pregnancy, examine and consider EC and USS pelvis, if in utero reassure can consider replacement if not XR abdo/pelvis if present = perf needs laparoscopy for removal only urgent if unwell (only specialist to reinsert), if not found then likely expulsion 
• Also risks infection and malposition to consider, if pregnant with IUS <12w refer to remove device 

CuID 
• Less used now >300mm Cu, if fitted >40 can stay til menopause, heavier Painful periods risks of PID or ectopic but no hormonal issues and good contraceptive (eg Nova-T), good option for EC as effective immediately direct toxic effect on endometrium/sperm 

Remember contraception tables typical use v perfect use  
• Implant is most effective (0.05% annual preg risk), vasectomy and Mirena close seconds 
• Condoms 2% perfect use 20% typical! 
• COCP and POP <1% perfect use but nearly 10% typical use! 
• LARCs and steri better as remove “typical use”
