Rhesus disease & testing RW

RhD status checked in booking, if Rh neg (and partner Rh pos) then baby may be Rh pos which means in a sensitising event (birth, bleeding, procedure) can have severe cross-sensitising reaction causing severe anti-D antibodies that cross placenta and can cause severe haemolytic disease & IUD 
• Treated with anti-D Ig which stops this effect during a sensitising event and given 28+32 to Rh negative mothers 
• Anti-D is a blood product and has risks BBV & CJD + allergy but usually screened for these 
• New non-invasive prenatal testing of fetus by checking cell-free DNA in mums blood can tell us if baby is Rh neg also or pos meaning can target who actually needs anti-D .
