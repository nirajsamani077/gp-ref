Chronic liver disease 

CLD signs 
• Leuconychia, clubbing, Dupytrens, palmar erythema 
• Spider naevi, Caput Medusa, gynaecomastia, testicular atrophy, reduced body hair 

Assessing hepatomegaly
• RIF to RUQ mass move down with inspiration
• Cannot get above 
• Finger breaths and border quality (craggy, puls)
• Dull to percussion 
• Auscultate for bruit (HCC) 

Evidence of hepatic decompensation 
• Ascites 
• Encephalopathy 
• Asterixis/flap 

3 Causes of hepatomegaly (and ascites)
• Cirhosis - alcohol, NAFLD, viral hepatitis, autoimmune (PBC, PSC, AIH), other (HH, A1, Wilson’s) 
• Carcinoma/mets or infiltrative (MP, amyloid)
• CCF (Pulsatile with TR)

Ix: 
Liver screen bloods 
• Baseline Bloods including clotting (INR), albumin for synthetic function 
• Hepatitis serology (A, B, C) +/- EBV/CMV/HIV
• Autoantibodies - AMA, anti SM, LKM, ANA 
• Specific - ferritin, a1, caeruloplasmin
• AFP for HCC 
USS abdomen - MRCP/ERCP if duct dilation 
Ascitic tap 
Liver biopsy 
If liver mets suspected CTTAP, biopsy, scopes 

Comps of liver failure /portal HTN
• UGIB due to variceal bleeding 
• SBP 
• Encephalopathy 
• Coagulopathy 

Child Pugh score 
Liver cirrhosis, link with mortality, score A, B, C
Measures - Albumin , BR, Clotting , Distension (ascites), Encephalopathy 
Mortality 50% in 1y for C (0% if A)

Ascites treatment 
• ETOH abstinence 
• Fluid/salt restriction 
• Diuretics (spiro, furosemide)  
• Drainage (diagnostic of therapeutic)
• Liver transplant
