Diverticular disease RW

If suspected DD - all should be referred to confirm Dx  = outpouch in the colon wall which gets stool trapped and can get infected, pressure higher within bowel - unknown cause, mostly sigmoid 
• Diverticulosis = Asymptomatic diverticula
• Diverticular disease = Mild sx diverticula 
• Acute diverticulitis = Fever pain systemic upset 
• Complicated AD = Perf, abscess, fistula, stricture

Px = LLQ pain, CIBH (diarrhoea), PR bleeding

Any acute complicated diverticulitis (sepsis, ++ tender abdomen, ++ bleeding, obstruction) = hospital for CT, IVs, surg 

Acute uncomplicated diverticulitis only give Abx if systemically unwell (but not above) or IC/comorbid 
• Clear fluid for 48h (bowel rest) then review 
• Co-amox 500/125 TDS 5d (pen allergy = Cef (or TMP) + met 5d, cipro can be used step down IV)
• Safety net & review after 48h esp if no Abx 
• If new ?DD don’t forget to refer for colonoscopy 

Otherwise if DD (symptoms) refer for scope (or CT colonography) & manage (diverticulosis no Rx)
• High fibre diet for life, prevents constipation and poo stuck in diverticula causing inflamm/infection 
• Bulk forming laxatives (fybogel/isphaluga) 
• Para & antispasmodics (mebeverine, buscopan)
