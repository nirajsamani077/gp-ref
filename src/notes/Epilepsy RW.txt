Epilepsy RW

Common, diagnosis by neurologist (refer any first seizure urgent to be seen within 2w & DO NOT DRIVE if suspected, first aid 999 recovery position afterwards in case of 2nd 
• Will do EEG, MRI, bloods & specialist tests 

AEDs initiated by neurologist usually 2+ seizure & aiming to be seizure free without S/E (achieved 70% of time), consider WCBA & risk of SUDEP

In children - 50% focal, 30% refractory, association with inherited, LD, ASD, ADHD +other 
• SOL, post stroke/bleed/meningitis
• Rarely autoimmune/metabolic/porphyria 
• Acute mimic - hypo, electrolyte (Na), drug/ETOH
• DDx - cardiogenic, vasovagal, non-epileptic attack disorder 

“Unusual electrical activity in brain causing abnormal movements & loss of consciousness” 

Hx - witness, video, trigger, aura, before, during (LOC? Movements? Eyes? Tongue biting/incontinence?), after (post ictal drowsy, headache/confusion). Consider subtle episodic tingling/smell/taste….

Types 
• GTC - LOC, muscles stiffen then jerk, post-ictal 
• Focal - Full/partial awareness, aura common 
• Absence - blank stare LOC, no post-ictal 
• Myoclonic - muscles jerking, no LOC/post-ictal
• Tonic/atonic - muscles stiffen or floppy = drop 
• Infantile spasms - childhood severe poor prog if untreated, same day referral 

Chronic management 
• Eplepsy specialist nurse involvement 
• Adherence to AEDs critical 
• WCBA /pregnancy implications, LARCs, high dose folic acid & involving specialist 
• Screen MH, cognition, ADLs/work/carer 
• Driving & DVLA rules 

AEDs (Anti-Epileptic Drugs) - monotherapy ideally 
• Valproate shouldn’t be used any F or M <55 due to serious risk of NTD if pregnant and infertility, only if 2 specialists agree no other alternative & if taking all women should be on pregnancy prevention programme (PPP), use effective contraception (LARC), annual acknowledgement of risk form by specialist & annual review 
• Prescribe by brand (especially CBZ, phenytoin)
• Common S/E
  - Rash, risk of SJS with CBZ esp if Asian (check genetics first HLA status) 
  - Leucopenia, hyponatraemia, deranged LFT
  - Weight gain
  - Fracture risk (vit D & check FRAX) 
  - Enzyme inducers (CBZ, phenytoin) = other drugs less effective (eg COCP)
  - Enzyme inhibitors (valproate) = toxic levels of other drugs (eg DOACs)
• Drug resistant = haven’t been able to control with 2+ drugs, tertiary centre options (ketogenic diet, vagal nerve stimulation, surgery) 
• Stopping AED specialist decision, >2y seizure free, very gradual withdrawal, considered completely resolved when >10y seizure free 
• Common reasons for increasing seizure = lack of compliance, coexisting conditions, ETOH/drugs + withdrawal, hypoglycaemia 
• Ensure emergency plan for prolonged seisure (>2mins), status (>5mins) - including BLS 
   - Call 999 
   - Buccal Midaz or rectal Diaz
   - 2nd dose after 5mins
   - Keep hazards away, recovery position after 

Epilepsy & SUDEP Action a great resource 

Factors for older people with epilepsy
• Almost always a medical cause when >65
• Post-stroke/bleed, neurodegenerative (Ad, vascular), transient epileptic amnesia = recurrent GTA with olfactory hallucinations), autoimmune 
• Keppra (leviteracitam) & lamotrigine preferred due to better S/E profile & less interactions (unlike CBZ, phenytoin)
