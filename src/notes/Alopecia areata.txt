Alopecia areata

Non-scarring alopecia well demarcated patches 
At edges may have short exclamation mark hairs 
Underlying skin normal or slightly red 
Can have nail changes (pitting, onycholysis (loosening), koilo, leuco)

Types 
• Totalis - whole scalp (rare)
• Univeralis - all body hair (rare)
• Ophiasis - occipital band like 
• Sisiapho - vertex sparing temporal 

Autoimmune - unclear cause, check TFT, ferritin

DDx = scarring or non-scarring 

Non-scarring alopecia - AATT
• Alopecia areata 
• Androgenic - female/male pattern loss occurs over crown and frontal scalp, replaced by finer hair (thinning), change in hairline, with age
• Telogen effluvium - profound shed, rapid cycle loss, 3mo after stress, lasts 3-6mo then regrows, can occur after childbirth/drugs (chemo, TCA, allopurinol) and chronic can be due to stress/thyroid/IDA overlap with areata 
• Traction (pull out), trichotillomania (psych pull)
• Others - Psoriasis, syphillis, bleaching g 

Scarring alopecia - permanent alopecia due to inflammatory destruction with scarring 
• Discoid lupus, lichen planus, scleroderma 
• Tinea capitis - itchy scaly scalp, kerion
• Frontal fibrosing alopecia - hairline specific 

Pull test- firmly grasp small area of hair, slide fingers across, if >3 hairs pulled out suggests active hair shedding 

Rx: 
Watch & wait - 50% regrow by 1yr, 90% eventually (esp if evidence of regrowth already)
Cosmetic advice (camouflage keratin micro fibre product, styling, extensions, covering, wigs need referral to derm) 

If >50% hair loss or pt wishes - try topical steroid (potent betnovate scalp shampoo or v potent 3 months)

Refer to derm for confirmatory scalp Bx if uncertain Dx or no response to steroids (after 3mo) + for further Rx (intralesional steroid/topical immunotherapy/minoxidil, dithranol, phototherapy, wig)

For androgenic - apart from watch & wait, topical minoxidil 2% solution or 5% foam (Regaine for women/men) OTC, finasteride 1mg PO for men only) up to 1y (review at 6mo, refer after 1y) or hair transplantation (privately)
If features of androgen excess such as associated hirsuitism or acne check free androgen index + prolactin and refer to endocrine
