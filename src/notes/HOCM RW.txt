HOCM RW

Often A/S in young., FHx SCD <40 (1/500 adult)

LV outflow tract (intraventricular septum) thickening leading to obstruction (LVOTO) causes exertional (increased load) SOB, CP, dizzy/syncope 

Jerky pulse, AS murmur ESM increased with Valsalva (reduces preload) & reduced with squatting (increases afterload) +/- MR murmur 

ECG almost always abnormal (septal Q waves, LAD, LVH, P wave abnormalities) 

Refer all urgently for echo, exercise ECG, genetic counselling & avoid strenuous exercise/driving until specialist RV

Managed medically with diuretics /Bb but do not impact prognosis, surgical options (ICD if risk of arrhythmia, septal ablation or heart transplant)
