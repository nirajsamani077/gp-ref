SCC & Bowen’s RW

Usually older, chronic sun exposure, IC & chronic inflammation also RFs but 65% of SCCs come from malignant transformation of AKs (even though overall transformation rate very low)

SCC Features 
• Enlarging non-healing papule/plaque/ulcer keratinised bleeding ulcerating with altered sensation, poorly defined margins 
• Sun exposed areas of head & neck (high risk = ear is very highest risk location, nose lips eyelids scalp, at trauma site = Marjolin’s ulcer non-healing >3mo usually LL and usually mets more l)
• May affect surrounding LN if invasive & mets

Refer any suspected SCC 2WW derm immediately
• Excision Bx usually confirms & gives depths/degree of differentiation/perineural invasion 
• Derm will F/U regularly over 2-3y period & if high risk of recurrence need high factor sunscreen & regular self examination 
• Prognosis = good (>90% 5y survival) & low mets rate but often recur and larger >2cm have worse prognosis 

Bowen’s disease 
• Along spectrum with AK & SCC - essentially SCC in-situ = full thickness dysplasia which hasn’t breached basement membrane - >15% progress to invasive SCC 
• Well dermarcated scaly red solitary patch 1-2cm slow growing sun exposed area 
• Refer if uncertain urgent (or if multiple, high risk areas or poor response otherwise treatment in primary care in similar way to AKs - Efudix, Aldara, Solaraze, Actikerall or curretage with close F/U)
