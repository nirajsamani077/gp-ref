Goitre /thyroid enlargement /thyroid Ca RW

Examination - position, size, fixed or not, hard, moves up on swallowing = part of thyroid, red flag = fixed craggy mass
• Examination generally poor indicator so refer any thyroid enlargement , usually 2WW if concerning or >3w and neck lump /hoarseness 
• Any stridor = urgent A&E assessment 

If found incidentally on other imaging 
• Examine, check TFT and refer 2WW if concerning features /risk factors for thyroid CA/young/FHx of MEN or Hx of neck DXT
• Usually secondary care will request USS rather than us, determines who needs an FNA (triple assessment exam USS FNA)
• Most euthyroid and no malignant concerns don’t need any treatment 

If malignancy found (thyroid Ca rare, most well differentiated papillary/follicular, medullary (associated with MEN2) & anaplastic rarer with worse prognosis) via 2WW management is 
• Surgery - hemi or total thyroidectomy with adjuvant DXT/radioactive iodine (destroys residual normal thyroid tissue)
• Need long term TSH suppression with thyroxine to keep TSH <0.1 as could be hormone sensitive, often needs high doses (175mcg to reduce risk of thyroid ca recurrence), secondary care decide specifics, remember risk of AF/CVD/osteoporosis 
• Rare but serious comps of surgery = hypoparathyroidism causing low Ca, vocal cord palsy
