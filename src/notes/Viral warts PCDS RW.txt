Viral warts PCDS RW

Caused by non-oncogenic HPV, common (10%), don’t have malignant potential - will resolve spontaneously without Rx but may take years & no Rx guarantees resolution 

Hands/feet (verrucae) or plantar warts most common - multiple well defined sore round lesions hyperkeratotic with pinpoint black dot vessels
• DDx=  hyperkeratotic corns & calluses which are more to do with friction and need warm water soaks, pumice stone or paper paring down or scalpelling by podiatrist + flexitol heel balm (salicylic acid + urea)

Other types 
• Mosaic warts - closely grouped warts on foot
• Filiform warts - small on face with stalk 
• Plane warts - multiple small lesions slightly elevated smooth surface 

Anogenital & oral warts - multiple pearly fleshy filiform, refer to GUM to R/O other STI & IC 

Management 
• No treatment as many resolve within first few months (1/3), can be hard to treat & recur but consider treatment if painful, cosmetically sensitive area (face) or IC (would need referral)
• Usually topical salicylic acid (SA) or cryotherapy although duct tape left on for 6d removed & soaked/filed down then left overnight, repeat again for total 8w = widely available & could work 
• SA - Verrugon/Wartex or with lactic acid (Bakuza/Salatac) use every day for 12 weeks + warm water soaks and filing/parring down, can consider doing under occlusion (waterproof plaster or duck tape) changed every 4 days, 50% cure 
• Cryo - similar cure rate, 6 freeze treatments once a week, stop if no benefit
• Specific others 
   - Filiform warts - SA too hard to apply 
   - Plane/facial warts - SA and cryo not advised as sensitive place, can try differin (adapelene) or refer 
• Refer derm if IC or large extensive or cosmetically sensitive - Unlicensed treatments (like AKs) - Efudix, Aldara or Actikerall or curretage/cautery/laser, risks of scarring
