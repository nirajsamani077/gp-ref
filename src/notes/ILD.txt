ILD 

Symptoms: SOBOE, dry cough, fatigue, WL

Exam - 3 C’s - clubbing, cyanosis and crackles (fine late end-inspiratory crepitations non cough clearing or positional) 

Signs of treatment 
Steroids Cushingoid, thin skin, bruising, proximal myopathy
Oxygen/LTOT 
Ciclosporin: gum hypertrophy, HTN 
Scars from lung transplant

If suspect ILD comment on whether: 
Upper or lower zone fibrosis 
Possible underlying cause eg slate grey amiodarone
Evidence of resp failure (O2, flap)
Evidence of cor pulmonale (RV heave, loud P2, raised JVP, peripheral oedema)

DDx for clubbing & creps 
Bronchiectasis/CF - usually early coarse change with cough and sputum ++ (pot)
Lung CA 
Supparative lung disease (eg pneumonia, abscess) 
Heart failure 

Idiopathic with no underlying cause
Idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis/IPF (Usual interstitial pneumonia on histology) = most common cause 
Others: 
NSIP (non-specific interstitial pneumonia)
COP (cryptogenic organising pneumonia)
LIP (lymphocytic interstitial pneumonia)
AIP (acute interstitial pneumonia)
RBILD (respiratory bronchiolitis-associated interstitial lung disease)
 
Underlying cause = 5 main areas 
Rheum - RA, SLE, dermato/polymyositis, systemic sclerosis, ankylosing spondylitis, Sjogrens syndrome, MCTD

Occupational/environmental: pneumoconiosis (asbestosis, silicosis, berylliosis, byssinosis, coal) EAA (farmers lung, bird fanciers lung), heavy metal/chemical inhalation (mercury, lead)

Drug-related & DXT (BANS Me) - bleomycin, amiodarone, nitro, sulfasalazine, MTX
Post-infective - Old TB, fungal, viral, ABPA

Other systemic disorders: sarcoidosis, vasculitis, IBD, malignancy 
 
Upper Zone Fibrosis
Sarcoidosis 
Old TB and ABPA (histiocytosis rare) 
Ank spond 
Most pneumoconioses (apart from asbestosis)
DXT 

Lower zone fibrosis 
IPF 
Most rheum conditions (RA, scleroderma, SLE)
Asbestosis 
Drugs (BANS Me)

Asymmetrical causes: TB or malignancy

Bloods: ABG, FBC (eosinophilia, anaemia, polycythaemia), CRP/ ESR, Igs , complement, autoimmune screen (ANA, ANCA, RF, anti-CCP, CK, anti-GBM.), serum precipitins (hypersensitivity, aspergillus), serum ACE (sarcoidosis)

Imaging: CXR initially, Bilateral reticulonodular interstitial infiltrates (parenchymal shadowing), volume loss, distribution, UC e.g. bihilar lymphadenopathy may suggest sarcoidosis, pleural plaques may be seen if asbestosis? HRCT gold standard = Ground-glass/honeycombing, more sensitive. 
 
Special tests:
Lung function tests: Restrictive pattern with reduced transfer factor
Bronch & BAL (eosinophils/neutrophils/lymphocytes, malignant cells) 
Video assisted, open, transbronchial biopsy if no clear diagnosis to look at histology
Echo: to assess for pulmonary hypertension

Management:
Conservative - MDT, Stop smoking, remove causative allergen/medication, vaccines (influenza, pneumococcal), claim compensation (asbestos), pulmonary rehab

Medical:
Treat the underlying cause
Manage complications e.g. infective exacerbations, pulmonary hypertension and cor pulmonale, lung cancer, respiratory failure
Steroids/ Immunosuppressants e.g. azathioprine, cyclophosphamide
Antioxidants e.g. N acetylcysteine
Pirfenidone (antifibrotic  and anti-inflammatory agent)
LTOT

Surgical: Single/double lung transplant
