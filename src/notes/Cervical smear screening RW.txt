Cervical smear /screening RW

Screening = 25-49 3yrly, 50-64 5yrly

HPV negative - return to normal recall unless 
• treated CIN - test of cure, repeat again after 6mo
• untreated CIN
• follow-up for cervical cancer/incompletely excised CGIN/SMILE or borderline changes 

HPV positive - check cytology
• abnormal cytology  (any of borderline, dyskariosis, or invasive SCC/glandular neoplasia)- refer for colposcopy 
• normal cytology - repeat in 12mo, if positive again repeat in 12mo, if positive again refer (as long as cytology negative) & if becomes negative return to routine recall 

If inadequate sample - repeat in 3mo, if inadequate again then refer for colposcopy 

About HPV (wart virus) 
• Causes almost all cervical Ca, specific subtypes 16 & 18 = oncogenic subtypes (99%), so negative high risk HPV is very reassuring
• Spread by close sexual contact, most women will come into contact & many will be low risk subtypes, condoms have limited protection
• No “cure” or prescribed treatment for HPV, usual body system will clear in few years 

Should be offered to trans men with a cervix - may be registered as male & so not offered but should liase with national screening team for this - lesbians should be offered in same way as hetero as it is any sexual contact - if never sexually actively then risk is extremely low .
