Hip replacements 

Most common = cemented hip replacement, uncemented becoming more popular in younger active pts but more expensive

Hip resurfacing where metal cap attached over femoral head, used in younger patients to preserve femoral neck if hip replacement needed later 

Post-on instructions 
• crutches or walking stick up to 6 weeks, use physio and home exercises
• avoid crossing legs, sitting on low chairs or sleeping on side to prevent dislocation 
• 4 weeks LMWH to prevent VTE
