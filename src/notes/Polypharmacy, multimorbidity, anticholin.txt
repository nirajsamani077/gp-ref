Polypharmacy, multimorbidity, anticholinergic burden & STOP/START tool RW

Adverse drug reactions cause many preventable hospital admissions & polypharmacy contributes to falls /delirium (costly itself) as well of cost of unused medicines!

Formal annual structured medication review (SMR) is PCN DES requirement (usually pharmacist) if 
• >10 repeat medications (some say >5)
• Severe frailty or residential home patient 
• On high risk meds (opiates, Z-drugs, gabapentinoids, BDZ, ACE, NSAIDs, diuretics, DOAC/warfarin/Antiplatelet), steroids 
• Recent admission with new meds or approaching EOL (reactive RV)

Steps 
1) Listen to patient - what matters to them 
2) Essential drugs - only stop on expert advice, still working to achieve goals? adherence? cost effectiveness (formulation, licensing)?
3) Non-essential drugs - indicated? risks benefits? can we safely deprescribe with a plan? interestingly benefits of “prevention” drugs like statins, sulfonyureas, bisphosphonates, BP meds wane as more frail with higher risks and are good ones to review & deprescribe (relaxed targets!)
4) Monitoring requirements  - FBC, UE, LFT & if elderly or extremes of muscle mass calculate Cockcroft Gault CrCl which is more accurate than eGFR, consider liver function - ensure doses are suitable 
5) Sick day rules SADMAN drugs, steroids etc 

Can use STOP/START tool to assist, lengthy tool but system specific - important to STOP long-use 

Anticholinergic burden a specific issue as high risk of falls, delirium, cognitive impairment - cumulative effect if Px multiple, strong AC drugs are 
• TCAs - Amitryptilline, clomipramine etc 
• Antimuscarinics - Oxybutinin, tolteridone etc 
• Antihistamines - Promethazine, hydroxyzine, chlorpromazine, diphenhydramine (in Nytol OTC)
• MH drugs - Paroxetine, Clozapine, Procyclidine 
• Many drugs have mild AC effect - look this up 

Multimorbidity (>2 long term health conditions including MH) has big crossover with polypharmacy & frailty - think about frailty phenotype & acute geriatric giants, SMRs and personalised management plan, continuity & admission avoidance.
