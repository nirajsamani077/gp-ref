Challenging behaviour & parenting children tips RW

Behaviour that risk to safety of self or others 
• Conduct disorder = most common reason for CAMHS referral in UK
• Cross over with MH issues, neurodevelopment, physical illness & safeguarding 

Initial management in primary care(prior to referral)
• Parenting strategies & parenting courses 
   - Descriptive praise & rewarding good behaviour “great job, wonderful, fantastic!”
   - Think-through - one step at a time instructions
   - Reflective listening - connection, not correction
   - “Special time” together parent child Eg walks 
   - Non-directive play time where you follow the lead from the child to play & encourage good stuff 
• REFER if persistent or significant risk - to CAMHS (or neuro if ND concerns & social care if support)

Conduct disorder 
• Specific antisocial behaviour, breaking rules, aggression - includes oppositional defiance & antisocial behaviour disorders 
• Specific ICD-10 criteria, persisting >6mo
• Rx = Parent training, child social programmes, if meds ADHD or risperidone under specialist 

Signpost The Challenging Behaviour Foundation & NSPCC .
