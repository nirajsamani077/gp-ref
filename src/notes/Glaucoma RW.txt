Glaucoma RW

Most common cause of irreversible blindness with chronic RNFL damage & optic neuropathy due to excess aqueous humour in anterior chamber & associated raised IOP (>21mmHg) but not always 
• Usually primary open angle (POAG) gradual onset peripheral bilateral visual loss usually detected on routine screening >40yo
• Very rarely chronic closed angle which is AS unless severe AACG 
• Angle = drainage point of aqueous humour in anterior chamber, closed angle obstructs drainage, open angle but trabecular meshwork obstructed

RFs 
• POAG= Age, FHx, Black, T2DM, myopia, steroids 
• AACG = Age, female, hypermetropia, drugs eg TCA Topimarate antipsychotics antihistamines 
• Secondary glaucoma uveitis or neovasculation “bits” blocking off trabecular meshwork, steroids 

AACG (Acute angle closure glaucoma) 
• Severe painful red eye vomiting abdo pain visual loss with clouded cornea, tender hard eye, fixed semi dilated pupil 
• Refer immediately eye casualty, check IOP using tonometer where available (air puff one)
• Subacute intermittent ACG can occur in low light settings recurrently (bedtime) with painful red eye & blurred vision 
• Rx = Acetazolamide PO/brinzolamide drops to bring down IOP acutely, iridotomy (laser, surgical)

POAG treatment & drops  
• Treat if IOP >24 & visual impairment/risk of it 
• Drugs either decrease outflow or aq production 
• First line drops (one or both) 
   - PG analogue (latanoprost) OD lengthens lashes 
   - Bb (timolol) BD (not in asthma brady/severe HF)
• Second line drops (add ons)
   - Sympathomimetic (brimonidine or pilocarpine) but not if TCA due to risk of interaction 
   - CA inhibitor (brinzolamide) 
• Only use PF (expensive) if allergy to preservative 
• Surgery = laser trabeculostomy
• FU ophthalm 6-12 monthly 

Driving - group 1 inform if both eyes vision affected (not just one eye), group 2 inform 1 eye
