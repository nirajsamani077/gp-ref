PET & gestational HTN RW

Any BP >140/90 significant in pregnancy, 3 groups 

Chronic (pre-existing) HTN = HTN <20/40
• Don’t use ACE/ARB/TZD in WCBA planning preg (switch to labtetol,nifedipine,methyldopa) or if under renal specialist discuss with them 
• If pregnant with HTN manage as above (BP may go up so tight)
• Ensure on aspirin 75-150mg from 12w to delivery as higher risk of PET, check Urine dip regularly and if suspecting check PIGF (may predict)

Gestational HTN = HTN >20/40
• Should rule out PET then treat with needs as above under obstetrics (BP normally goes down in 2nd half of pregancy with pressure on IVC)
• Growth scans and consultant led ANC 
• Similar otherwise to pre-existing, higher risk of chronic HTN & PET in future pregnancies 
• May plan IOL 37/40 if poor control/compromise 

Pre-eclampsia (PET) = HTN + proteinuria 
• Sx = Headache, vision, vomiting, RUQ, swelling 
• Urine protein >1+ do PCR & if >30 significant
• PET RFs (primip, prev/FHx age>40, CVD, DM, CKD, BMI >34, multiple, interval >10y) = aspirin 75-150mg from 12w to delivery 
• Refer immediate PET bloods & management with labetalol, nifedipine, methyldopa & regular BT BP urine growth scans under obstetrics, if severe BP >160/100 decision re delivery MgSO4 steroids etc
• Post natal - enalapril safe for BF (if afro carribean nifedipine fine) - avoid diuretics if BF, at 6-8w check dip urine check BP & if persistent abnormalities check UE refer otherwise step down treatment with diaries etc as long as <140/90
Always assess for PET .
