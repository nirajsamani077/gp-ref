Hearing, vision & speech in children RW

Hearing loss /assessment 
• SNHL picked up by National newborn screening programme - whilst in hospital have otoacoustic emissions test (done up to 3mo) - measures functions of hair cells in ear, if abnormal (not passed 2 attempts) referred for auditory brainstem response (ABR) 
• Causes of HL otherwise usually conductive = OME (glue ear), TM perf, FBS, wax or SNHL (post meningitis or TORCH)
• Glue ear common = dull retracted TM fluid level loss of light reflex, mouth breathing suggests large adenoids which is RF for this 
• If concerns recheck Rinnes Webers & refer audiology for tests - can only do pure tone audiometry >5y so other tests earlier than this 

Vision change /assessment 
• Check red reflex (can use tropicamide 0.5% if struggling) - if absent ?retinoblastoma or congenital cataract so refer urgent 
• Eye contact by 4w and fix & follow by 8w, estimate VA by max distance that keeps eye contact whilst you smile . 2m = 6/36, 3m = 6/18
• Assess pupils, EOM & nystagmus using a toy & if any nystagmus or restriction refer 
• If eyes look unequal or neglecting one eye- refer 
• Red/watery eye could be true conjunctivitis (if neonate swab and refer), or congenital nasolacrimal duct obstruction (test = 1% flourescin to eye, if remains dry at 5min = obstruction). Most resolves spontaneously by 18mo
• Eyelid lump could be chalazion (resolves spontaneously, lid hygeine warm compress & single dose azithro if infected) Vs haemangioma = refer urgently 
• Concern for squint = cover uncover test, check corneal reflex (small white reflection when shining light in centre of pupil), then put toy in front & cover then uncover the good eye, if good eye moves then suggests squint - refer ?neonatal strabismus (convergent or divergent squint)

SALT in children 
• Speech problems can be related to hearing 
• Main types of problem 
   - Language impairment - poor vocab/late talker (expressive), can’t understand (receptive)
   - Speech impairment - lisp, stuttering 
• Refer if red flags (HV useful) if <3 esp if regression, poor receptive or concern about social & >3 if expressive remains poor or significant stuttering >12mo (usually resolves after age 4) .
