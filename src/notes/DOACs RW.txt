DOACs RW 

First line anticoagulation in most situations as stroke prevention (AF) & treatment of VTE 
• DO NOT use for prosthetic mechanical heart valves or Antiphospholipid syndrome - warfarin
• Before starting check CrCl with Cockroft-Gault formula & NOT eGFR as is renally excreted & can accumulate in renal failure (eGFR affected by muscle mass & other factors so less accurate) 
• Apixaban Bd is oldest and has most safety data vs OD dosing (rivaroxiban/edoxaban) where a missed dose may increase risks (short half life which is why compliance critical!)
  • Recent drive to use Edoxaban for AF due to pharmaceutical factors - manufacturers can provide additional incentives for using/monitoring eg tools, smartphones, therapy reviews - od dosing no reversal agent, not necessarily better 

Specific DOACs - all factor 10A inhibitors apart from dabigatran which is thrombin inhibitor
• Apixaban (Eliquis) - 5mg BD for AF (VTE first 7d 10mg BD), reduce to 2.5mg BD if CrCl <30 or any 2 of age >80, weight <60kg or Cr >133 - antidote =  Ondexxya. Don’t use if CrCl <15
• Rivaroxaban (Xarelto) - 20mg OD for AF (VTE 15mg BD 21d then 20mg OD), reduce dose to 15mg OD if CrCl <50 - antidote = Ondexxya. Don’t use if CrCl <15
• Edoxaban (Lixiana) - 60mg OD for AF (not licensed for VTE), reduce dose to 30mg OD if CrCl <50, weight <60kg or on erythromycin/azoles, no approved antidote. Don’t use if CrCl <15
• Dabigatran (Pradaxa) - 110 or 150mg BD depending on age in AF (VTE 150mg BD after had 5d LMWH) - antidote = Idarucizumab 

Shared points  
• Check FBC UE LFT prior to starting, and check CrCl (usually >30 is fine) & CI in liver disease & then annually (> frequently if elderly 3-6monthly) 
• Fewer drug interactions (less than warfarin) but higher bleeding risk with NSAID, amiodarone, fluconazole, rifampicin, phenytoin (PPI helps!)
• SE - GI, abnormal LFTs bleeding, anaemia 
• Rivaroxiban + aspirin can be used for secondary prevention of CVD if symptomatic PVD or high-risk IHD (age >65, RFs ++).
