Lower back pain RW

3 Ns
• Nasty red flags - B Sx, CES, CA, inflam, midT, # (urinary retention late sign for CES and used to divide who to operate on as may be too late if retention) 
• Nerve compressive (radicular pain) - sciatica, deep gluteal syn (non discogenic sciatica, usually buttock predominant), spinal stenosis , SIJ 
• Non specific - no red flags, no radicular pain = sprain or strain 

Neuro = UL leg pain /sensory worse than back usually back of leg/thigh (L5/S1) or front thigh (L4) with positive SLR   /slump test (sit up, slump, extend knee and DF ankle = pain). Always think PR for anal tone if concerned about CES

Don’t image any in primary care 
Nasty = same day emergency referral for MRI/CT
Nerve = only refer OTS to consider MRI if Sx >3mo despite best treatment, impact on fx, progressive neuro (MRI will often show disc bulge or herniation, doesn’t correlate with Sx)

Use START Back tool for non specific -10pt questionnaire to decide treatment 
• Low risk = Self help (VA exercise), reassure 
• Mod risk = above + physio 
• High risk = Physio +/ - pain clinic /CBT

If meds
• Neuro = NSAID + PPI short term, weak opiate max 12 weeks then refer if not helping, not neuropathic meds, can consider spinal LA/Steroid injection and surgery (microdisectomy), can consider v short term diazepam as muscle or mind relaxant 
• Non specific = NSAID + PPI short term, not para alone, acute script weak opiates only if can’t use NSAIDs 

Always safety net for CES - severe both sides cannot pass Urine or open bowels or lost control, cannot feel when wiping bottom or significant change 

Inflammatory back pain (seroneg spondylo)
• >3 months in young (<50)
• Night waking, better with movement/NSAIDs
• Buttock pain (SIJ)
• Psorasis, enthesitis, significant FHx 
Either refer rheum immed or check HLAB27 CRP ESR

Any unexplained Bone or ST swelling = urgent X-ray or USS within 48h so usually SDEC or CAU, consider NAI

Lumbar spinal stenosis (neurogenic claudication) 
• >50 degenerative canal 
• Back buttock leg pain or tingling when waking 
• Improves with forward flexion (shopping trolley) 
• Normal foot pulses, maybe LL neurology 
• Most improve wirh conservative - physio etc, use analgesia sparingly (short term for flares), minority refer for LA/steroid injection or surgery (severe, neuro signs, no improvement 6mo conservative)
