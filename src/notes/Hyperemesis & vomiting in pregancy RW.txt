Hyperemesis & vomiting in pregancy RW

NV very common in early pregnancy as a HCG Sx - occurs in 80% & can have many other causes apart from this eg UTI, gastro, abdo or meds 

Hyperemesis gravidarum (HG) = specific severe protracted NC unable to keep things down with 5% weight loss, dehydration & electrolyte imbalance (<1% of pregnancies)
• Other causes in 3rd TM = PET, acute fatty liver 

Use PUQE score 3 questions for last 24h have you felt nauseas, have you vomited, have you retched?
• Score for objective but not ?that useful 

Check urine ketones & dip in all - if >1+ ketones should be considering admission or trial of antiemetics & admission if not helping 
• If doing bloods check FBC (high HCT indicates dehydration), UE, LFT, TFT (biochemical thyrotoxicosis with raised HCG) & amylase 

Management 
• Non med (mild)= ginger, vitamin b6, acupressure  
• Med (mod sev) = cyclizine 50mg TDS, stemil 5-10mg TDS, metclopramide 5-10mg TDS, ondansetron ?safety issue with risk of cleft palate, oral pred specialist 
• Severe HG criteria = admit IVI USS bloods etc & VTE prophylaxis due to increased risk - can have impact on IUGR SGA and risk of recurrence in further pregnancy (15%)
