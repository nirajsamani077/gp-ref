Paediatric constipation RW 

Diagnosis 
• <1y - <3 stool/week, rabbit dropping/large hard, distress/bleeding on passage, anal fissure 
• >1y - above + overflow diarrhoea/soiling, poor appetite, colicky abdo pain improved by passage, retentive posturing, straining, 

Most constipation is idiopathic/dietary/meds/fissure pain but red flag diagnosis are 
- Hypothyroidism
- Hirshsprungs
- Hypercalcaemia
- Safeguarding/child abuse
- Anatomical anal/rectal issue 
- Coeliac/Crohns 
- Neurological (LD, spina bifida/SC problem)

Reassuring if obvious ppt, normal exam (check abdo anus spine LL - no PR!) & systemically well/growing well 

Red flag indicators 
• Meconium not passed <48hrs from birth 
• Ribbon stool 
• FTT
• Significant distension 
• Leg weakness/sensory change, motor delay 

Management 
• Good explanation - backlog of hard poo stretching bowel/rectum, painful to pass & feedback loss of control, vicious cycle, can cause soiling which is not child’s fault, although diet fluid fibre etc can help it won’t cure constipation alone, best to clear off old stuck hard poo (disimpaction) which can be messy and get worse before better (eg soiling) and then lower dose laxatives for maintenance, bowel will not become lazy and regains it’s mojo (use ERIC website for videos)

• Reward good behaviour (star chart for sitting on toilet for agreed time 30mins after food, for 10mins) make it fun, raise feet onto stool & rock, abdo massage & bicycling 

• Assess & treat faecal impaction (BNO 1w, soiling or palpable stool should have this) 
   - Movicol Paed Plain disimpaction regimen 1st
   - Add stimulant laxative (senna) if no luck >2w
   - If can’t tolerate macrogol - use lactulose (osmotic) or docusate (softener) with a stimulant 
   - If failed refer, if considering suppository/enema
• Maintenance laxatives - Once consistent watery slimy poo (type 7) then step down to maintenance = 1-4 sachet daily for soft poos, adjusting, don’t stop suddenly, wean off if consistent soft poos. Similar step up to disimpaction (stimulant if >2w, Lactulose/docusate) 

Disimpaction doses (escalating regimen day-day) 
• <1yo = 1/2 - 1 sachet daily 
• 1-5yo = 2, 4 (day 2-3), 6 (day 4-5), 8 sachet 
• 5-12yo = 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 sachet day by day 
• >12yo = adult formulation, as above max 8
• Mix plain or flavoured powder into correct volume of water (on packet) usually 62.5ml or 125ml, can keep in fridge up to 24h but only use water 
• Lactulose dosing - <1y = 2.5ml BD, 1-5yo = 5-10ml BD, >5yo = 10-20ml BD 
• Stimulants - senna, bisacodyl, sodium picosulphate, docusate (softener + stimulant)

If not improving 
• Refer paeds if no response >1mo (under 1) or >3mo (over 1), support with HV or Paed continence nurse, abdo massage/leg bicycling 
• Usually breastfed not weaned is rare so Ix, if bottlefed can give extra water between feeds  (infant <1yo ~700-1000ml fluid requirement/d) 

Hirshsprungs (rare 1/5000) 
• Congenital loss of nerve connections in large bowel (aganglionic segments) causing functional obstruction (lack of transit/peristalsis)
• Associated with MEN and Downs 
• Failed meconium <48h, chronic constipation/FTT (usually presents <2yo). Needs paeds surgeon
