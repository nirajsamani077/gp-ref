Insomnia RW

Poor sleep with impaired daytime functioning, associated with anxiety/depression, chronic disease & drugs (secondary)

Acute Vs chronic 
• Acute /short term <3mo usually related to stress and may persist even after stressor gone - usually listening, reassurance, sleep hygeine advice, really try not to Px sleeping tablets & discuss rationale (Z-drugs will help you fall asleep 20mins quicker but will only feel like having 7mins sleep more over whole night, most of benefit placebo affect, risk of addiction and making things worse after stopping) - only Px short single course 3-7d if highly distressed disabling severe. Re discuss 1mo if not improving consider CBT-I or short term Px 
• Chronic /long term (>3mo, at least 3 nights a week) all need CBT-I, sleep hygeine, never long term sleeping tabs usually acute short courses if desperate , daridorexant new drug if CBT-I failed 

Sleep hygeine 
• Fixed bed time and wake up time 
• Avoid tech and caffeine /etoh /heavy meal late 
• Keep phone outside of bedroom 
• Make sure bedroom quiet dark cool 
• Relaxing activity (meditation, mindfulness, book)
• Avoid napping in day, be active exercise 
• Write down worries on notebook and tell yourself you will deal with them in morning 
• Realise that you can’t continuously sleep, cycles usually 3-4h then wake short period and back to sleep - keep a sleep diary for 2w

CBT-I
• Interactions of negative thoughts/catastophising (“I can never get to sleep”), with feelings (low mood anxiety), with behaviour (bad habits, physical sx, medicating)
• Tactics like bed as a stimulus for sleep, regularising patterns , relaxation therapies 

Sleepio or sleepstation Or  sleepful which is actually free!

Drugs (sleeping tablets)
• Any drug with sedative effect can impair driving so illegal to drive when impaired - inform everyone 
• Only if severe for shortest time 
• Z-drugs, BDZ, melatonin all associated with addiction, withdrawal, falls, accidents, future risk of dementia 
• Options 
   - OTC Nytol (diphenhydramine) - sedative, AC
   - Other sedating antihistamines - promethazine (phenergan), hydroxyzine, chlorphenamine
   - Sedating antidepressants - mirtazapine (if comorbid depression, lower doses higher sedative effect), trazodone common but off licence 
   - Z-drugs/BDZ hypnotic drugs (zopiclone, zolpidem usually) - reduces time to get to sleep by 20mins but subjective improvement only 7mins total per night, significant risks as before (addiction, tolerance, rebound on stopping falls etc) so only v short courses, don’t represcribe, never on repeat etc. Zopiclone 3.75-7.5mg
   - Melatonin (slow release) - licenced for older >55 with short term sleepiness, initial trial for 3w then reassess (if effective continue 10w), 2mg OD (3mg for 5d for jet lag), gen well tolerated and doesn’t cause daytime sleepiness 
   - Daridorexant - new drug for chronic insomnia. Daridorexant is the new kid on the block approved by NICE and fine for GP's to issue when CBT hasn't worked or isn't appropriate. Non addictive and effective. 

Deprescribing Z-drugs 
• Explain, engage pt, use CBT-I framework and offer other non-drug options, significant benefits to stopping, often not taking the drug causes withdrawal Sx not actually true insomnia (so the problem is the drug, not the insomnia) 
• Can either taper down to a lower dose or taper down all together, 25% every 2 weeks, can get mild transient withdrawal sx will supervise 

If all else fails refer to sleep clinic 

TIPS 
Short term: Promethazine (hydroxyzine hydrochloride at low dose, 10mg works for short term ) or Zopiclone, particularly if struggling with acute adjustment-related issues. 

Chronic: CBT-I then Daridorexant. 

Vague, non-severe issues - suggest OTC magnesium, or Melatonin. Melatonin. I use it off licence because it's an OTC med in Canada and USA, Should be green (but isn’t ) for adults >55 and a thirteen week trial. Children and <55 specialist start.

Melatonin is quite safe, I issue to most people above 55. Young just promethazine PRN.
