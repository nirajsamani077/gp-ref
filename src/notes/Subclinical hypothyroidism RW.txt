Subclinical hypothyroidism RW

Raised TSH normal T4 
Always repeat after 3mo with TPO antibodies 
Lack of evidence for LT CV benefit of treating (although those with TSH >10 do have higher risk)

Factors influencing treatment or not 
• TSH >10 on 2 tests 3mo apart 
• Symptoms, including goitre - careful attributing
• Pregnant or trying to conceive (need increase thyroxine in pregnancy due to TBG)
• If >65 or CVD be cautious - risk of AF /bones 

Approx 5% progress to overt hypothyroidism/ year 
Would recommend annual TFT if not treating unless v clearly no thyroid disease & low risk (then 2-3 yearly)

Remember thyroxine on empty stomach 30mins before any meds breakfast or coffee - 100mcg usual starting dose for 60kg, 125mcg for 80kg, 150mcg for 100kg (1.5mcg/kg/d ~), if elderly AF then 25-50mcg
