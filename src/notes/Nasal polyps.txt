Suspected new nasal polyps =
- Good air entry = Long-term nasal  steroid spray 
- Poor air entry = Specialist , consider 6 weeks fluticasone nasal drops BD 
Assess response and step down to maintenance dose  . 2 puffs bd higher dose, 1 puff bd or 2 puffs od  is maintenance  

Known nasal polyps =
- Complete obstruction = ENT
- Deterioration = 20-25mg pred 14 days with 6 week course  fluticasone nasal drops twice  daily on specialist advice only

Nasal douching advice and sinus precautions
- In a bowl, mix 1 level teaspoon of salt and 1 level teaspoon of bicarbonate of soda in about 1 pint of cooled boiled water.
- Stand over a sink, cup the palm of 1 hand and pour a small amount of the cooled mixture into it.
- Sniff the cooled mixture up 1 nostril and let it run out.
- Repeat sniffing at least 3 times up each nostril or until your nose clears up.

Aspirin sensitivity and asthma as causes 

ENT referral
- For confirmation of diagnosis or if “red flags” eg obstruction, bleeding 
- Poor response to nasal steroid spray e.g. mometasone furoate (Nasonex) after 3 month trial , or oral pred/fluticasone drops on specialist recommendation 

Patients with persistent symptoms despite maximum medical treatment may be referred for consideration of nasal polypectomy endoscopic  sinus surgery.
