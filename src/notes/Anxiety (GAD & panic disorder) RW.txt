Anxiety (GAD & panic disorder) RW 

GAD 2-step questions for screening 
• Feeling anxious or on edge?
• Can’t stop or control worrying?
3 for every day, 2 for most days, 1 for few days 
Score >=3 suggests anxiety then ask about functioning (avoidance) and specific situational/panic attacks (physical sx) or generalised “free-floating” tension + comorbid depression/risk assessment 

Use CBT framework to discuss interactions between thoughts/emotions with behaviours/physical symptoms and vicious cycle and cross over (eg poor sleep or etoh due to anxiety or low mood can perpetuate the problem and make the physical problems even worse) 

“Worrying is a normal part of life, but becomes a problem when it happens inappropriately, frequently or gets in the way of our day to day functioning, worrying is a safety mechanism but actually chronic worry doesn’t keep us any safer”
• Good to use allocated “worry time” 15mins/day 
• Break the cycle of “catastrophising” - common in health anxiety, use “two track” approach of physical and mental well-being 
• Anxiety response “flight or fight” unpleasant but not itself dangerous (or useful)

Similar management to depression, low intensity if mild-moderate (guided self-help, behavioural activation to break cycle, group exercise, Mindfullness/meditation, counselling and CBT computerised group or individualised) - but if severe these + SSRI +/- referral CMHT 
• If SSRI failed then try 2nd or SNRI, if failed refer CMHT to consider pregabalin 
• Avoid BDZ (no evidence, addictive, poor LT outcome), sedating antihistamine or antipsychotics 

Specific 
• Panic disorder - graded exposure & breathing exercises (slow steady breathing out) really useful, 2nd line after 12w failed SSRI = TCA (imipramine or clomipramine), propranolol can sometimes be used as short term adjunct (masking but not curing) eg flying 
• Agoraphobia - fear of leaving house leading to panic sx and avoidance/anxiety. Similar management to panic disorder with graded exposure (habituation) etc but has high relapse rate so meds usually needed
