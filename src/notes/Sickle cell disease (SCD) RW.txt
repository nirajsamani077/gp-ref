Sickle cell disease (SCD) RW

Rare AR single gene disorder - fault with B-globulin Hb chain which creates sickle shaped RBCs when deoxygenated which causes 
• Haemolysis causing anaemia & endothelial dysfunction 
• Sticky RBCs causing vaso-occlusion /ischaemia 

More common in Afrocarribean, Indian & ME 

Genetic types 
• Homozygous = HbSS, one HbS from each parent  with trait causing most severe sickle cell anaemia 
• Compound heterozygous = one HbS & one other abnormal Hb variant (HbC or thalassemia)
• Trait = one HbS and one normal (HbA)

Presentation 
• Picked up newborn heel prick/AN screening 
• If HbSS usually presents FTT infections joint swelling splenomegaly <6mo of age 
• Acute comps ppt by infection, hypoxia, stress etc
   - Painful/vasoocclusive crisis 
   - Haemolytic crisis /anaemia 
   - Splenic sequestration crisis 
   - Acute chest syndrome 
   - Infection (pneumococcal), osteomyelitis 
   - Priapism (painful sustained erection >1h) 

Management of acute crises  
• Admit all to hospital urgently or if not acute but clinically suspecting infection broad spec Abx 
• If doing bloods check retics 
• Otherwise all is in hospital - trust the patient they know their crises 

Chronic issues 
• Splenic - pen V until 5yo, encapsulated imms
• Anaemia - haemolysis, common baseline 60-90
• Chronic pain (AVN), poor growth, retinopathy, leg ulcers, recurrent priapism, gallstones, chronic lung/CVD/CKD 
• WCBA - folic acid 5mg, all contraception safe, male can have ED/hypogonadism

Secondary care management (MDT)
• Elective BT for anaemia 
• Hydroxycarbamide if recurrent crises 
• Stem cell transplant - only curative treatment 
• Surgery - splenectomy if recurrent crises .
