Bronchiectasis RW

Persistent purulent cough/sputum & recurrent LRTI
• Commonly misdiagnosed as COPD as chronic cough with sputum & obstructive spirometry, with non specific initial CXR findings
• Always consider lung CA on DDx - smoker >40
• Other common sx - haemoptysis, SOB, wheeze 

Can be well localised (lobar) or widespread, abnormal dilation of lower airways causes inflammation trapping of mucous infections & airway obstruction (cough clearing crackles)
• 50% idiopathic 
• Post-infection eg TB pertussis ABPA common
• CF, primary ciliary dyskinesia, CT disease,fibrosis 

Refer resp if querying consider with  
• Bloods - FBC, Igs (immunodef), IgE aspergillus 
• Sputum MC&S (colonisation pseudomonas)
• CXR +/- direct access CT thorax 

Management - MDT similar to COPD/ILD
• Chest PT - mucous clearance 10mins a day 
• Pulm rehab 
• Imms - annual flu, one off pneumococcal, if immunodef specialist may do 23-valent polysaccharide pneumococcal vaccine and measure abs 4–8w later to ensure response
• Mucolytics & bronchodilators 
• No ICS unless other cause eg ABPA which also responds to oral steroids 
• Exacerbation - always send sputum 
   - Abx 7-14d & change only if not sensitive 
      • 1st line = Amox, Clary or Doxy 
      • 2nd line = Co-amox or Levoflox (tendon risk) give 1st line if repeated Abx, prev resistance etc  
      • If not improving 48h admit IV above 
• Recurrent/frequent exacerbations (>3/year)
   - Specialist may consider propylactic/ LT Abx esp if colonised with pseudomonas can have LT
     • Colistin nebs, azithro 500mg 3x/w or both
     • If >5 exacs may have IV Abx every 3mo 
     • If on prophylactic abx use different in exac 
     • Not if sputum showed non-tuberculous myco

Annual RV 
• BMI, smoking, sats, sputum, spirometry 
• Exac frequency 
• MRC score for SOB
    - 1 = strenuous exercise 
    - 2 = on inclines /slopes 
    - 3 = on level ground 
    - 4 = has to stop ~100m
    - 5 = at home/getting dressed .
