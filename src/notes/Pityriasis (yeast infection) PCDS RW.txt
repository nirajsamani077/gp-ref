Pityriasis (yeast infection) PCDS RW

Pityriasis rosea (associated with HHV)
• Young, herald patch (largest, sharp borders, bright red and scaly) few days before usually trunk smaller oval dull red macules/patches with fine peripheral scale (not usually palmoplantar - think syphillis)
• DDx = guttate psoriasis (teardrop shape, fine silvery scale, recent URTI), tinea (scrapings if uncertain) or viral exanthem
• Resolves by itself 6-12w, can use emollient and Hc 1%/eumovate if itchy, menthoderm to soothe

Pityriasis versicolor 
• Young (late teens), usually upper trunk large confluent oval lesions (fawn in light skin, hypopigmented in dark skin) with fine scale (usually hard to see, has to be evoked by stretching skin), can also be hyperpigmented 
• Yellow green flouresce under Woods light 
• Nizoral (Ketoconazole) shampoo once daily for 5 days (lather, leave on 10 mins then wash off), if recurrent repeat monthly (recurrence common)
• If resistant/widespread = Sporanox (itraconazole) 200mg OD 7 days or fluconazole 300mg weekly for 2w 
• DDx = vitiligo, psoriasis, ezcema

Pityriasis alba 
• Hypopigmented facial patches in young (usually post inflammatory hypopigmentation), much less well defined than vitiligo 
• Self limiting, no treatment needed unless underlying ezcema (Rx as facial dermatitis ) 

Pityriasis capitis (dandruff)
• Can be related to sebum production (like Seb derm) and malasezzia colonisation 
• flakes larger due to skin cells (DDx Seb derm with greasy scaly scalp, typical distribution, red skin, also ezcema and psoriasis) 
• Medicated antidandruff shampoos once/twice a week for 1 month - coal tar preps (T-gel, alphosyl, capasil, polytar) which inhibit cell division, selenium sulphide (selsun) which reduces cell production or ketoconoazole (nizoral) 
• Regular antidandruff shampoos - Oilatum, Head & Shoulders, Neutrogena T-gel daily, Nizoral daily
