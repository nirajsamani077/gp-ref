2WW criteria 
• Oropharyngeal (maxfax) - Unexplained lump or red/white patch (erythro/leucoplakia) >3w
• Laryngeal - >45 persistent unexplained neck lump or hoarseness >3w (also urgent CXR)
• Thyroid - any unexplained thyroid lump 

RF- Smoking/ETOH for all. HPV oncogenic subtypes (16, 18) in younger with delayed latency from oral sex (up to 30y) - has better prognosis as more chemoradiosensitive 

Going to 2WW most without any imaging apart from low risk young with small reactive LN & no other red flag symptoms (dysphagia, haemoptysis, hoarseness, UL nasal obstruction/otalgia 
• Acute tonsillitis rare >45yo so consider any UL sore throat or hoarseness esp if persistent!

After 2WW - initial USS /FNAC or nasendoscopy, staging with CT or PET-CT

Rx - Full array of options, Smoking cessation, SALT,  suppressed thyroid post neck DXT 

Oral lesion ?better initially assessed by community dentist first though unless already >3w therefore 2WW (if uncertain make specific plan for dental check asap with arranged follow-up with you) .
