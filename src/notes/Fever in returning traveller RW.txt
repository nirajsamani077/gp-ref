Fever in returning traveller RW

Risks of highly transmissible or severe infections 
• Viral haemorrhagic fevers - Ebola
• Respiratory - SARS, MERS, COVID, TB, influenza 
• Viral contagious resistant strains - Measles, chickenpox, influenza 

Assessment 
• Full places people timeframes & vaccinated/taking prophylaxis/treatment out there.
• Consider specific exposures
   - Food, contaminated water, soil
   - Animals (Toxo Qfever)/bites (rabies cat scratch)
   - Insect bites (Lyme, malaria, dengue, Zika, chik)
• If septic/severely unwell admit - think malaria , if ?highly transmissible think PPE & transfer direct to SR with specialist (eg active TB or resp)
• Look for rash, tick bites, LN, HSM + normal stuff 

Ix if not admitting - usually admit resp/systemic 
• Malaria thick & thin films & bloods (quickest)
• Travellers diarrhoea - stool MC&S with Giardia 
• Hep A /BBV screening if jaundice (gonna admit)
• Prolonged fever consider typhoid or paratyphoid (enteric fever) - rash abdo pain diarrhoea .
