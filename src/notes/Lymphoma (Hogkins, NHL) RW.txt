Lymphoma (Hogkins, NHL) RW

Lymph node /lymphoid tissue (HSM) cancer 
• Any persistent >6w lymphadenopathy or mass anywhere cervical, axillary, inguinal, abdo - especially with B symptoms (fever night sweats weight loss) = 2WW haem for urgent Bx 
• 6th most common CA, with lots of subtypes 
   - Hodgkin’s lymphoma (10%)
      • Reed-Sternberg cells, ETOH causes LN pain
      • Bimodal age (20, 70), good survival (80% 5y)
   - NHL (90%) affect T/B cells & low or high grade 
      • Usually older & worse survival depending 
      • Some associated with EBV, H pylori, Hep C
      • Most common high grade- diffuse large B cell 
      • Most common low grade -follicular B cell 

Bloods not that useful (FBC or inflamm markers), rely on persistent LN (probably anything >3w or unexplained) & B symptoms 

Head and neck LN common - often steroids (dexamethasone) a treatment to reduce swelling but affects Bx results so do not use - risk ++ of SVC obstruction 

Management led by haematology/oncology .
