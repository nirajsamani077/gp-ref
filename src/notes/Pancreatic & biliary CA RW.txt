Pancreatic & biliary CA RW

2WW criteria 
• New painless jaundice >40 = 2WW 
• >60 with weight loss + any GI symptom/ new DM = 2WW direct access CT abdo 
• Upper abdo mass (eg liver edge) UGI 2WW 

Often late presenting with worst 1y survival of all, with vague symptoms & typical sx only present when advanced/metastatic 
• 10% familial (BRCA,Lynch, Peutz-Jeghers)
• 10% resectable @ Dx
• 10% endocrine (rest exocrine so enzyme def)

RFs - ETOH/chronic pancreatitis, smoking, obesity 

Ix points 
• Similar to ovarian CA - IBS is rare in older people >50 so go looking even if normal tests!
• CT abdo better than USS as may miss 10% CAs 
• Don’t request tumour markers (CA199) in GP unless for monitoring under specialist instruction 

Rx 
• Curable = surgical resection + neoadjuvant chemo (only small proportion) 
• Palliative = biliary stent, chemo, nutrition (Creon) for malabsorption due to loss of pancreatic exocrine function
 - 50,000 units main meal, 25,000 units snack 
 - Take capsules whole with first mouthfuls
 - Don’t break as enzymes get broken down
 - All products are pork-derived, consider this is Muslim or Jewish but leaders say it is acceptable 

Biliary CA (cholangiocarcinoma or GB cancer) extremely rare - surgical management with MDT.
