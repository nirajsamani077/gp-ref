Child safeguarding (SG) & NAI 101 RW

Consider = maltreatment a possible cause so discuss and info gathering, Suspect = serious level of concern & immediate local SG referral (discuss with SG lead /ICB SG nurse) & social care referral 
• If imminent risk to safety, suspecting child trafficking/sexual exploitation call police
• If suspecting look to arrange admission to safe place away from perpetrator (admit on call paeds consultant) 
• If considered & referred social care may be given “Early Help” with high risk families eg practical, emotional, school, parenting support, weekly HV as a preventative measure - 4 Levels of Need framework defined in each area 
  - Level 1 = Normal basic needs universal services 
  - Level 2 = Early help additional support 
  - Level 3 = Early help complex child in need
  - Level 4 = Sig risk, protection, don’t need parental consent to share info 
  - Similar concept in adults 
• Accurate documentation & info gather from other agencies (school, HV, practice nurse etc)

RFs - Substance/ETOH, MH, lack of support, lack of engagement with services, family/personal Hx
• If suspect child trafficking = police 

Physical abuse signs 
• No good explanation for injury (consider) 
• Bruising /petechiae (suspect)
      • Non mobile child (<6mo usually)
      • In definable shape (hand, stick, ligature, bite)
      • On non-bony parts of body/face 
      • On neck (strangulation) or ankles/wrist (ligs)
      • Burns scalds cold injury without explanation 
      • Obvious - # without fragility, IC/retinal bleed 

Sexual abuse signs 
• Any sexual activity with girl <13 = unlawful even if it is consensual with peer. Any STI or pregnancy at this age = sexual abuse till proven otherwise 
• Persistent/recurrent anogenital symptoms or signs of injury /FGM / anogenital warts (with no hx of consensual sex with a peer)

Neglect 
• Not brought in/brought late child, recurrent DNAs
• Not given meds, no jabs/screening  
• Observed poor hygeine (recurrent scabies/lice), unsafe home, malnutrition, poor dentition (been to dentist?), oral injuries, persistent bed wetting 
• Not being supervised & being abandoned 
• Odd behaviour in child (scavenging, sexualised)

Record the “voice of the child” - document what they say, speak alone where possible, look at child’s response when adult taking and direct questions to child 

Info sharing about a child at risk governed by GDPR doesn’t limit sharing info /breaching confidentiality if individual is at risk - try to get consent if you can but don’t need it 
• But adults more complex as if capacity & refuses then we can’t share unless matter of public interest, serious risk of harm, adult being coerced - speak with indemnity provider
