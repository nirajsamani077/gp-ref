Childhood epilepsy 

Infantile spasms - first 6mo of life with salaam attacks & progressive neurodevelopmental, EEG hypsarrhythmia, serious and poor prognosis, vigabatrin/steroids 

Lennox-Gastaut - extension of infantile spasms in 50%, atypical absences/falls, 1-5yo, severe mental handicap, slow spike EEG, ketogenic diet 

Absence seizures - 4-8, few-30s, no warning & quick recovery, valproate & ethosux with good prognosis (95% grow out)

Juvenile myoclonic epilepsy (Janz) - teenage girls, generalised seizures after sleep deprivation, daytime absences, shock like myoclonic seizures, valproate 

Benign Rolandic epilepsy - most common, M>F, UL facial paraesthesia lead to generalised around sleep/wake 

Neonatal - secondary to meningitis, hypoglycaemia, head trauma. B6 (pyroxidine) dependency = give B6. Benign familial seizures
