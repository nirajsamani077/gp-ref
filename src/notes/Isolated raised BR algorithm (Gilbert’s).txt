Unconjugated or conjugated ?

UBR (direct or conjugated BR <20% of total) = Gilbert’s (most likely) or haemolysis (check retics, LDH/hapto, film, FBC)

CBR (>20%) = formal liver/biliary disease (ETOH, NAFLD, hepatitis or other) 

Gilbert’s syndrome 
• Common (up to 10%) benign mild intermittent rise in UBR (without other deranged LFT or haemolysis), triggers stress /infection /ETOH 
• Genetic component (usually AR), defect in enzyme which conjugates BR in liver to form bile 
• Non-pruritic jaundice (ULN = 20, usually not much higher and very rarely >80) 
• No BR on urine dipstick & ensure no signs of cholestasis (pale stool, dark urine, pruritis) or HSM/CLD on examination
• Not a disease, entirely benign (some studies show antioxidant effect), reassure, may cause jaundice during intercurrent illness, ensure inform people you have it & if persistent or signs above look for obstructive /other cause
