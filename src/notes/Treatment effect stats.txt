Treatment effect stats 

Absolute risk reduction/increase 

                    Outcome present     Outcome absent 
Exposed                 a                                   b
Control                   c                                   d

Experimental event ratio (EER) = a/a+b 
Control event ratio (CER) = c/c+d 

Absolute risk reduction = CER - EER 

eg: 20 olanzipine takers, 16 achieved remission, 4 didn’t vs 40 placebo takers 12 achieved remission 28 didn’t 
EER = 16/20 = 0.8
CER = 12/40 = 0.3
ARR = 0.3 - 0.8 = -0.5 = 50% ARR

Relative risk is EER/CER rather than so for this would be 0.8/0.3 = 2.66 so 2.6x more likely to achieve remission with olanzipine 
For relative risk reduction (RRR) = ARR/CER or
CER-ERR/CER = 0.5/0.3 = 1.6 or 160%

NNT is the reciprocal of ARR so 
1/ARR = 1/0.5 = 2 

NNT = how many patients would require an intervention to reduce the expected number of outcomes by 1
