COCP RW 

Both hormones oestrogen and progesterone help to prevent ovulation, thickens cervical mucus and keeps womb lining thin and stable 

Benefits 
• Effective contraception (but not as good as LARC, depends on taking missed pill rules etc, up to 10% real-life failure rate in first year)
• Helps regularise and reduce heavy/painful periods
• Helps with PCOS, endometriosis, PMS
• Reduce risk endometrial/ovarian CA (BC higher) , and also colorectal 

1st /2nd gen - NET (1), LNG (2)
3rd gen - Desogestrel, gestodene
4th gen - Drospirenone, dienogest

UKMEC C/I (4 absolute, 3 relative risks > benefits)
• 4 = Migraine with aura, BP >160/100, current CVD/stroke, smoking >15/d >35y, VTE or thrombogenic mutation 
• 3 = BMI >35, smoking < then above
• Consider enzyme inducers, lamotrigine, malabsorption (LARCs better) 

Risks - generally low, relative rather than absolute
• Breast/cervical Ca (back to baseline after stop), avoid if carry BRCA 
• VTE - annual risk 2/10000 up to 6/10000, risk of death from VTE 2/100000 much lower than most lifestyle things, risk higher on 3/4th gen pills and higher if stopping starting pills a lot 
• Stroke /MI - esp hx migraine with aura (not if without = UKMEC2), higher oestrogen pills 
• After age of 50 risks higher 

Before starting check 
• UKMEC criteria - smoker VTE breast/cervical ca migraine with aura
• BP and BMI, smoking 
• Counsel on risks and s/e - breakthrough bleeding usually settles 3mo (try lowering oestrogen, changing progestagen, back to back - check compliance and Ix preg STI lesion if persistent), headache (caution re migraine with aura)/mood swings/weight gain can happen (consider back to back or changing progestagen) 
• Missed pull rules , sick day rules 

Regimes 
• 21/7 for a bleed but can tricycle packs with break for better bleeding and less risk of failure, or can even just take continuously up until a bleed eventually happens which it will as eventually womb lining will build 
• If first 5d of cycle can start without precautions, if after this or swapping from other contraception use 7days precautions
• If starting after emergency contraception, after Levonelle can start straight away (7d precautions) but after EllaOne wait 5d then start (7d precautions ) and all preg test at 3w 
• Annual review all BP BMI smoking

Missed pill rules & UPSI, generally take missed pill
• If any extending pill free interval need EC, 7d P
• If not and 1 pill missed then no EC needed, take both today and yesterdays pill and continue pack 
• If 2 pill missed 1st week after break need EC 7d P 
• If 2 pill missed 2nd week no EC 7d P
• If 2 pill missed 3rd week before break no EC miss break and 7d P 

Preps - usually start Microgynon 30mcg
• 30mcg pill 
   - 2nd gen (LNG) - Microgynon ED = dummy pills
   - 3rd gen - Marvelon (desog), Femodene (gest)
   - 4th gen - Yasmin (drosp)
• 20mcg pill 
   - 1st gen (NET) - Loestron
   - 3rd gen - Mercilon (desog), Femodette (gest)
   - 4th gen - Eloine (drosp)
• 35mcg normally wouldn’t use 
• Phasic pills have different oestrogen mcgs at different times (eg Logynon) ?more natural 
• Different “bioidentical” oestrogens apart from ethinyl estradial (Qlaira, Zoely) more expensive 
• Patch (evra) weekly & ring (nuvaring) 3 weekly more expensive 

Any pt on CYP inducers or Lamotrigine will reduce effectiveness of COCP (and also POP implant etc) so really best for Mirena or injectable
