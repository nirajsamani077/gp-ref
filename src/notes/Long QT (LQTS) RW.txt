Long QT (LQTS) RW 

QTc = M >0.45, F>0.46ms, significant >0.5ms

FHx SCD <40, preventable cause of death in young, risk of polymorphic VT & VF arrest 

Drugs (often dose dependent)
• Citalopram/escitalopram 
• Erythromycin/clary/cipro, fluconazole
• Amiodarone/ sotalol, TCA, chlorquinine
• Hydroxyzine, haloperidol, ondansetron 
• Absolute C/I in anyone with congenital LQTS - if not avoid Px 2 together or if Hx of electrolyte issue 

Electrolytes - low K/Mg/Ca/temp 
Cardiac - MI, myocarditis, SAH
Specific genetic conditions
• Jervell-Lange-Nielsen (deafness)
• Romano-Ward (no deafness) 

Congenital LQTS (AD)
• LQT1 - exertional or swimming syncope 
• LQT2 - exertional, emotional or auditory syncope 
• LQT3 - rest/night syncope 

Refer all to cardio & avoid ppt (2 x long QT drugs) 
Beta blockers lifelong & ICD if high risk
