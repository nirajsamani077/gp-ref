Abdominal migraine RW

Common (nearly 5% of kids) F>M often FHx - related to “gut-brain” axis as functional disorder causing episodic central abdo pain & other symptoms (cyclical vomiting, photophobia, headache) interfering with life - keep an abdo pain diary 

R/O red flags 
• Acute abdomen & DKA (dip Urine!)
• Recurrent UTI (dip Urine!)
• CMPA & Coeliac (TTG, exclusion), Crohns 
• Malignancy - weight loss, abdo mass 

Make a positive diagnosis & explain gut brain axis 
• Most will grow out of it by age 10!

Rx = avoid triggers (80% attacks resolve in dark quiet room & paracetamol/nurofen)
• Acute - may try intranasal sumitriptan 
• Prophylaxis - may try pizotifen 0.25mg BD 
• Refer specialist if unsure/not resolving 

BMJ abdo migraine info sheet useful 

Functional abdominal pain/dyspepsia/constipation also related - similar management but positive Dx & letter to school may help, should try to continue regular activities & have psych support .
