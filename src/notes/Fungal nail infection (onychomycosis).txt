Fungal nail infection (onychomycosis)

Dermatophyte (trichophyton rubra) 90%, candida
Unsightly thickened rough waxy nail 
DDx - psoriatis, lichen planus, yellow nail, trauma 
Ix - nail clipping/scrapings advised (high false neg)

Only treat if symptomatic & bothered by appearance + diagnosis confirmed by micro 
- if single nail = topical amorolfine lacquer, 1yr for toenail, 6mo for fingernail 
- if multiple nail = oral terbinafine 3-6mo for toenail, 6wk-3mo for fingernail has better success, check LFT after 6wk & regularly, can affect taste 
- oral itraconazole alternative antifungal, used if candida
