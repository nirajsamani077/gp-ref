Nocturia without LUTS RW

Common, if associated with LUTS follow path (eg BPH voiding or OAB storage) otherwise 
• Nocturnal polyuria - XS fluid in evening, pedal oedema/HF, OSA
• 24 hour polyuria - DM, DI, primary polydipsia 
• Drugs - Diuretics, CCB, SSRI
• Consider chronic retention with overflow 

Urine frequency volume chart 
• >33% urine passed at night in older men 

PU fully before bed
Avoid high fluid amounts caffeine etoh before bed 
Elevate legs if oedema 
Unlicensed options (pm diuretic, bedtime desmo)
• Furosemide 40mg 4pm
• Desmopressin single dose bedtime avoid water 1h before 8h after, increases water absorption risks ,low Na 

Chronic urinary retention (CUR)
• Common underrecognised due to slow progression and lack of sx (nocturnal enuresis suggests high pressure )
• Can be low pressure or high pressure (associated renal impairment & BL hydronephrosis on USS - so check both urgent) 
• Residual (post void) volume >500ml-1L or palpable /percussible bladder 
• Ensure not on anticholinergics (cause UR) or SADMAN drugs (risk of AKI)
• Admission if renal impairment/sepsis 
• Urgent urology if high pressure CUR with BL hydronephrosis but normal renal function. Routine if ?low pressure with recurrent LUTS/UTI
• Rx - usually ISC / LTC or bladder outlet surgery
