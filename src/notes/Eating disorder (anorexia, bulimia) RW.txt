Eating disorder (anorexia, bulimia) RW

Highest mortality of any psychiatric condition, usually young teenage girls & on the rise - centered around a preoccupation with weight/body image with weight and food intake central
• Underweight  = Anorexia nervosa
• Normal/overweight = Bulimia nervosa (if binges then purges including vomiting, meds or excess exercise) or Binge-eating disorder (binges only)
• Other specified eating disorder (OFSED) which doesn’t meet above criteria but is most common
• Avoidant & restrictive food intake disorder (ARFID) - selective eating for other reasons (not body image) eg autism 

Any suspected eating disorder = refer CAMHS or CMHT immediately & SCOFF questionnaire 
• Sick from food? 
• Control over food?
• One stone lost or gained last 3mo?
• Fat when other people say too thin?
• Food dominates your life? 
>=2 score indicates possible eating disorder 

Acute admission concerns 
• BMI <15 (or<2nd BMI centile) or rapid weight loss 
• Bradycardic, hypotensive (postural), dehydrated, syncope, hypothermic, proximal myopathy (sit up squat and stand test)
• Acute suicidal/self harm of food/drink refusal 
• Abnormal bloods (low anything apart from ALT & HbA1C) or ECG (arrhythmia, long QTc from Defs)

Signpost family to Expert Carers Helping Others (ECHO) for resources & BEAT charity giving telephone coaching to family - all on Beat Eating Disorders website 

Treatment specialist - Fluoxetine +/-olanzipine for AN, family therapy (+/- CBT) & annual monitor 
• BMI, BP, HR, full bloods inc haematinics, bone, HbA1C, TFT and bicarb.. (ECG only if concerns)
• FRAX, DEXA - but if low weight drugs limited 
• Dental checks - erosions from purging (also effects of laxatives, diuretics, caffeine)
• WCBA - usually amenorrhoeic, but if pregnant very high risk, LARCs preferable
• Consider eating disorder in T1DM with high HbA1C or recurrent DKA - very common!
• Enquire other MH, drug/ETOH & carer .
