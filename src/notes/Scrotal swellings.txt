Scrotal swellings 

Epididymal cyst 
- most common
- posterior & separate to testicle 
- association: PCKD, VHL, CF
- supportive, may need scleropathy or removal of large symptomatic 

Hydrocele
- fluid accumulating in tunica vaginalis which may be communicating (patent processus vaginalis so peritoneal fluid into scrotum in newborns usually resolves in few months but repair if persists >1y) or non-communicating (excess fluid in TV) 
- can occur from infection, torsion, tumour 
- soft non tender anterior & below testicle, can get above (unlike hernia), transilluminates, can become so large that can’t make out testicle separately 
- USS needed, also to r/o underlying tumour 
- usually conservative treatment 

Varicocele
- testicular vein enlargement, “bag of worms” 
- associated with infertility 
- 80% L side, if R side drain direct from R renal vein so may have right RCC 
- USS with Doppler needed 
- usually conservative but surgery if pain or to treat infertility (debatable), if grade 1 mild or sub clinical reassure & monitor, if grade 2/3 check semen analysis and if abnormal may need surgery
