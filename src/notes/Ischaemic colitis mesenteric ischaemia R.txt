Ischaemic colitis/ mesenteric ischaemia RW

Acute presenting abdo pain due to vascular loss to bowel which causes inflammation, ulceration, bleeding, necrosis (RF age AF thrombotic state)
• Ischaemic colitis = transient over hours moderate colicky abdo pain & bloody diarrhoea
• Mesenteric ischaemia = severe sudden onset embolic cause (AF), abdo pain disproportionate to degree of tenderness

Both need urgent surgical review & admission 
• CT abdo (colonoscopy only ischaemic colitis) 
• Surgical resection (vs conservative in ischaemic)
• Consider CV RFs long term (smoking & ACE/Bb may reduce bowel circulation but risk of stopping)
• Recurrence common >10% & chronic symptoms.
