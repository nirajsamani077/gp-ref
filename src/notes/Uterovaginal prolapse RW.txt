Uterovaginal prolapse RW

Very common, but descent that doesn’t reach the introitus without symptoms is not prolapse and just physiological (so symptoms are key)
• PV heaviness/dragging/bulge
• Urinary, bowel symptoms (even having to digitally evacuate!) 
• Sexual dysfunction
• Hx of age, parity, traumatic birth, obesity, surg

Examination 
• Anterior wall = cystocele, Posterior wall = rectocele, Apical = uterus/vaginal vault post hysterectomy prolapse
• Check when straining standing even reducibility and check POP-Q score for staging 

Stage 1 = Doesn’t reach introitus 
Stage 2 = Reaches introitus 
Stage 3 = Past introitus most out
Stage 4 = Complete vaginal eversion 

Treatment 
• Physio for PMFT - 16 week supervised 
• Pessaries - ring pessary easy to fit made of silicone to keep prolapse up and stop it descending for stage 1/2 (doughnut for stage 3/4) usually GPwSI can fit , most don’t affect intercourse, may take a few goes to find the right one, change 6 monthly, may have local effects expulsion and even worsen bladder incontinence but most tolerate v well 
• PV oestrogen - if uterovaginal atrophy PM
• Procedure - Urogynae usually mesh (controversial)

Signpost RCOG PIL pelvic organ prolapse
