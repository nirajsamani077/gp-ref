Cost analysis (econ eval)

Cost effectiveness analysis (CEA) - cost per single clinical measure or effectiveness (eg lives saved, symptom reduction) - done when cost benefit can’t be done as benefits can’t be monetised

Cost benefit analysis (CBA) - costs and benefits of intervention all measured relative to money and things like life years saved given monetary value 

Cost utility analysis (CUA) - things like QALYs which are where outcomes are measured more broadly eg 1 year of life in perfect health (DALY = loss of 1 year of life in perfect health) 

Cost minimisation analysis (CMA) - least costly way to achieve same outcome 

Costs can be 
• direct - costs directly associated with healthcare intervention (staff time, supplies, travel cost) 
• indirect - costs from reduced patient productivity (time off work, time spent caring for relatives)
