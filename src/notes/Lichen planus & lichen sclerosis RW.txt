Lichen planus & lichen sclerosis RW 

Lichen planus 
• AI poorly understood condition affecting skin or oral/anogenital mucosa (skin = purple polygonal itchy violaceous papules, oral = symmetrical reticular white lacy lesions)
• Association with Hep C so enquire RF & offer test 
• Rx = Very potent topical steroids (dermovate) & low threshold to refer for pred, retinoids, PTX (for oral erosive can use soluble pred 5mg dissolved in 15mg water used as mouthwash - if failed refer as possibly premalignant Bx & for pred etc - anogenital sensitive usually just HC) 

Lichen sclerosis 
• Anogenital skin chronic inflammation (PV itch in PM women, male with phimosis, child with constipation) with crossover with AI (esp thyroid) - commonly misdiagnosed as thrush or atrophic 
• In PM significant as risk of scarring causing obstruction and SCC transformation in 5%!
• Pale white thin fragile areas of skin with excoriations
• DDx = lichen planus, dermatitis, SCC in situ!!!
• Check TSH in all due to association but main treatment = Refer all for Bx to R/O SCC & only use moderate topical steroid (eumovate) & emollient 
   - Once R/O use very potent steroid (dermovate)
   - Use Od 4w, then alt day 4w then twice week 4w
   - Then PRN with ongoing surveillance for SCC checking for ulceration lumps every 3-6mo .
