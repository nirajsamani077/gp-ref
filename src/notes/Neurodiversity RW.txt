Neurodiversity RW

Brains are different 
• “Neurodivergent” people have diagnoses like ASD, ADHD, Tourette’s, dyslexia/dyspraxia - almost always present in early life /childhood 
• “Neurotypical” people don’t have these diagnosis
• Often the Dx co-occur rather than in isolation so having one is a risk factor for having others 

Dyslexia 
• LD affecting reading/writing (eg slow, lack of ability, hard to sequence letters/words)
• School may identify or parents should speak to school’s SENCO (special education needs coordinator) who can do the full screening tests and refer for a formal Dx (although don’t need a formal Dx for school to put in place support) - all NHS funded in children 
• Adults dyslexia assessment only available privately, but may be able to get financial assistance for assessment by employer under Equality Act
• British Dyslexia Association a great resource 
• Dyscalculia related but specific to numbers/maths, often with strong other subjects, same process for assessment & funding 

Dyspraxia 
• LD affecting coordination & movement - clumsy, awkward movements, difficulty learning practical skills & if severe difficulty with coordinating speech 
• Dx more involved as need to RO cerebral palsy under paeds specialist (and other motor conditions eg DMD) - involve school, PT/OT and paeds 
• Similarly for adults no formal NHS pathway but involve PT/OT who can help with Dx & needs Ax 
• The Dyspraxia foundation for support 

PANDAS = rare paediatric autoimmune neuropsychiatric disorders associated strep infection - not neurodivergence, acute onset neuro/behavioural change, involve specialist & admit paeds .
