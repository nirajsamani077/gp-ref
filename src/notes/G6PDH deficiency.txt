G6PDH deficiency 

X-linked recessive, most common RBC defect 
Seen in Mediterranean & North Africans 
Causes intravascular haemolysis, neonatal jaundice, splenomegaly & gallstones 
Blood film - Heinz bodies, bite/blister cells 

Triggers - Beans (broad/fava), sulph drugs, cipro, primaquine (antimalarial)

Check G6PDH assay 3 months after acute episode as haemolysis at time leads to false negative 

Hereditary spherocytosis similar but AD, North European, more chronic but infection can ppt, spherocytes on film & EMA binding test for Dx
