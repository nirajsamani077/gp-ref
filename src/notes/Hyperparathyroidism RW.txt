Hyperparathyroidism RW

See hypercalcaemia algorithm 

PTH produced by parathyroid glands in neck, controls calcium metab by adjusting GI absorption and renal loss, bone mineralisation and conversion of vit D from diet/ sunlight to calcium 

Types 
• Primary (PHPT) - adenoma of parathyroid, high Ca, high/normal PTH, often found incidentally, bones stones moans groans
• Secondary - usually due to v low calcium level from severe vit D def, renal/liver disease, GI malabsorption causing PTH to rise to compensate, so low/normal Ca and high PTH, replacing vit D can unmask PHPT
• Tertiary - chronic secondary due to CKD means chronically raised PTH and calcium with loss of the feedback loop (parathyroid starts to produce PTH independently of Ca levels), high Ca high PTH

Refer all suspected to endocrine 
Will check Vit D, urinary calcium, renal USS, DEXA

Management
• Hydration ++, avoid TZD lithium, keep vit D normal range >50
• Surgical management (parathyroidectomy) more likely if Ca >2.85, symptoms or renal/bone disease 
• If declined/unsuitable - cinacalcet (increases sensitivity of Ca receptors to help reduce PTH and calcium), consider bisphosphonates under specialist advice if increased FRAX/DEXA
• Usually needs annual bone profile U&E whether had surgery or not, DEXA 2-3 yearly
