Multiple sclerosis (MS) RW 

Chronic AI inflammation of CNS nerves & brain wirh demyelination of nerve fibres slowing conduction of impulses, common 20-30yo
• RRMS - most common relapse then remission
• SPMS - RRMS to progressive 
• PPMS - rare progressive form 

Common Sx (disseminated in space & time) 
• Visual - optic neuritis (subacute painful visual loss, loss of colour vision), diplopia, INO
• Sensory/motor - focal weakness/sensory change (ascending), worse when flexing neck (Lhermittes)
• Gait/balance ataxia 

Refer all suspected urgent to neuro for Dx (MRI)/Rx
• MDT MS nurse PT OT SALT 
• DMARDs to prevent relapse, avoid live vaccines
• Relapses (new sx, DDx trigger infection) = same day neuro advice, never start steroids without advice (usually PO methylpred 0.5mg 5d or IV)
• Vit D has no effect on relapse or disease activity 
• Common symptoms 
   - Fatigue - self help, consider amantidine/SSRI 
   - Spasticity - baclofen, gabapentin or both (never stop suddenly risks rebound, withdraw gradually 1-2w), THC:CBD spray an option 
   - Oscillopsia - objects move, gabapentin
   - Optic neuritis - ophthalmology to manage 
• Advanced care planning / RESPECT 

Spasticity 
• Velocity-dependent resistance to passive movement (rigidity = sustained resistance, contractures = shortened reduced laxity of muscles joints etc)
• Refer any new spasticity to neuro for UC 
   - Neurodegenerative (MS, MND, PD, pos t stroke)
   - Metabolic (SACCD B12 def, copper deficiency)
   - Spinal (MSCC, spinal cord injury) 
   - Genetic rare (hereditary spastic parapariesis)
• Baclofen 1st line 5mg TDS initial titrate up & do not stop suddenly (risk of rebound), S/E dizziness drowsiness incontinence 
• Alternatives: Gabapentin, tizanidine, dantrolene 
• Neurorehab physio important 
• Specialist: Botox, intrathecal baclofen pump
