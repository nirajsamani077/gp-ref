PSA requests & issues RW

Men >50 urged to check PSA
Check for LUTS, haematuria, ED and FHx/black 
• If yes do DRE, PSA and urine MSU
• If no discuss pros and cons - a screening test but no screening programme as it is generally a good “rule out” but if positive can be due to other things apart from ca (BPH with age, UTI, even exercise and ejaculation), leading to unecessary tests (like biopsies or MRI) and anxiety, and some evidence now that some ca’s with normal PSA . Similarly many have very localised slow growing Ca that will not change in many years or affect life expectancy or quality, so investigations/treatments may have more effect), still a useful test but some caveats so important to be informed 

If doing test ensure 
• No UTI or urology procedure within 6w
• No exercise or ejaculation within 48hrs 

Age-related PSA cut off (roughly decade down n) 
• 40-50 = 2.5
• 50-60 = 3.5
• 60-70 = 4.5
• >70 = 6.5

Useful stats for discussion 
Of 7 men who have the PSA test 
• 1 with raised PSA, all will need TRUS Bx (with >1/2 side effects & 1% readmission) or MRI, and of those 1/3 have CA
• 6 with normal PSA, 1/6 have CA 

Multiparametric MRI now used before Bx in men with raised PSA where first treatment would be curative - to get prostate volume & calcuate PSA density & allow for targeted Bx and could eventually avoid Bx all together 

Still do DRE if normal PSA with symptoms as 1/6 have CA! 

PSA screening programme has no impact on all cause mortality
