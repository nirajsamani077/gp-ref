Hep A & E RW

RNA virus
Faeco-oral spread (esp travel food water in developing counties contact/needles/MSM in UK) 

2-4 week incubation 
Viral prodrome then…
Acute hepatitis (RUQ pain, tender hepatomegaly, jaundice, deranged LFT with ++ ALT) 

Hep A IgM positive suggests recent infection 
• Notify PHE 
• Management supportive but most going to admit, hand hygeine very important 
• Exclude from school/nursery/work 7d after onset jaundice (most infective time) 

Hep A vaccine can be given - initial + booster if 
• High risk travel
• High risk activity (MSM, IVDU, occupation, BT)
• CLD

Hep E - FO spread, similar to Hep A, shellfish/pork, transaminitis, higher mortality .
