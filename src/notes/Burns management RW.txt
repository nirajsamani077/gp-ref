Burns management RW

First aid 
• heat - 20mins under cool water, cover under layered cling film, remove rings and elevate reduces swelling 
• electrical/chemical - remove source

Size measures - 1 palm = 1% TBSA
• Wallace’s rule of 9% - head & neck, each arm, each anterior leg, each posterior leg, anterior/posterior chest, abdomen 

Depth measures 
• superficial (1st deg) - usually red painful 
• partial thickeness (2nd deg) superficial dermal - usually pale pink, painful, blistered & blanches& blanches& blanches
• partial thickness deep dermal - usually white with non blanching erythema, reduced sensation
• full thickness (3rd deg) - variable, painless, white, white 
• Best check is CRT using sterile swab end , if doesn’t blanch = deep dermal or full thickness, if does blanch = superficial 

Refer 
• all deep dermal/full thickness 
• superficial >3% TBSA (2% for kids) or sensitive/ peripheral areas (IVI if >15% or >10% in kids)
• atypical (inhalation, chemical, electricial, NAI)

Management 
• superficial - symptomatic with analgesia, emollient 
• partial superficial dermal - clean, leave blister intact, non adherent dressing, avoid topical cream, RV in 24hrs
