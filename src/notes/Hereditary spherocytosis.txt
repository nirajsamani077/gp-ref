Hereditary spherocytosis

AD spherical RBCs —> haemolytic anaemia (most common in north Europe) causing FTT, gallstones, pre-hepatic jaundice, splenomegaly & aplastic crisis (parvovirus b19 infection) - extravascular haemolysis

FBC - raised MCHC
Film - spherocytes
Haemolysis screen - raised retics

If those with FHx and these tests positive don’t need any other test (if unequivocal then specialist tests e.g. EMA binding/cryohaemolysis) 
Used to use osmotic fragility test - no longer reliable 

Acute crisis - transfuse if needed
Long term - splenectomy, folate supplementation 

G6PDH is similar but is 
- X-linked recessive (male only) 
- African/Mediterranean 
- Intravascular haemolysis 
- Drug triggers 
- Heinz bodies on film
