Microcytic anaemia algorithm RW

Repeat FBC with ferritin
Consider iron studies , Hbopathy screen, Coeliac 

If ferritin low = IDA
• GI blood loss esp malignancy LGI or UGI. Examine. Arrange FIT test any adult (even any >60 with any anaemia). Top and tail scopes 
• Gynae blood loss /other GU dip Urine etc 
• Malabsorption (Coeliac, gastric surgery, PPI)
• Poor diet (veg vegans) 

If ferritin normal or high - check iron studies 
• Anaemia of chronic disease (ACD) - often low serum Fe, low TIBC, low transferrin, low TSAT (with high or normal ferritin) 
• Thalassemia - Hbopathy screen 
• rare = sideroblastic anaemia 

Consider dual pathology - IDA with ACD, usually IV iron needed for ACD as oral iron not well absorbed, Hb only partially corrected 

Replacement 
• Ferrous sulphate 200mg Od/ fumarate 210mg Od / gluconate 600mg Od taken on a completely empty stomach (otherwise absorption affected, esp with food /tea /PPI. Don’t need to give BD-TDS 
• Recheck FBC after 1mo and ensure cause Ix 
   - If Hb normalised - continue ferrous 3mo then stop 
   - If Hb improved - continue ferrous 3mo further then recheck, if normalised then continue 3mo then stop
   - If Hb not improved - check adherence and refer for ferrinject (also if unable to tolerate oral/absorption issues, or on haemodialysis) 

If not improving with oral ferrous may have malabsorption or funtional iron deficiency , or ACD

Upon improvement and stopping ferrous - check FBC /ferritin 3 monthly for 1y for recurrence 

Iron deficiency & iron studies specifics  
• Ferritin is best correlation for total iron intracellular body stores but problematic as increased in inflammation, malignancy, liver/renal disease so may be Fe deficient but raised/normal ferritin (ACD can have normal/high also)
  - Usually 20-300 normal range, 15 in periods 
  - CKD <100 lower cut off, <200 if dialysis as inflammation and renal failure raises levels 
• Iron deficiency or microcytosis without anaemia common, risk of “hidden” cancer, do FIT & urine dip/CXR if focal symptoms & check iron studies, consider alt day ferrous if deciding to treat 
• Specific signs - koilonychia, glossitis, alopecia, restless legs (check ferritin) & Plummer Vinson structure (dysphagia, IDA, oesophageal web)
• Other iron studies tests (all in bloodstream)
   - Serum iron - iron bound to transferrin in blood, varies a lot with diet infection inflamm so poor 
   - Transferrin /TIBC - iron transporting protein, high/normal in iron deficiency (to maximise utilisation) & low in ACD - alternative test to TIBC which is the availability of iron binding sites on transferrin and same tests results. Oral iron can increase transferrin/TIBC
   - TSAT - ratio of serum iron to TIBC/transferrin, Fe/TIBC x100 (check using iron studies) or Fe/TF x 70.9 - better test for iron overload than deficiency. Normal = <45% (0.45)
