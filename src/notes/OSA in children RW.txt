OSA in children RW 

15% of children snore, 1-5% have true OSA 
• Simple = enlarged tonsils/adenoids, obesity
• Complicated = Downs, craniofacial, cerebral palsy, Prader-Willi

Consider if snoring/apneas (can even cause enuresis), daytime behavioural sleeping problems or massive tonsils/mouth breathing on exam 
• Also check nose for polyps/enlarged turbinates if loud mouth breathing, ears for glue ear & check BMI

Refer paeds if ?OSA - if just snoring with no significant nighttime daytime or massive tonsils then reassure 

Rx = Adenotonsillectomy will cure, weight loss (refer tier 2/3 services), if failed/contraindicated CPAP and or nasal steroid to Rx allergic rhinitis or after surgery to stop adenoids re accumulating (smaller lymphoid gland tissue than tonsils).
