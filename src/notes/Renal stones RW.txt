Renal stones RW

Classic sudden severe colicky loin to groin pain restless (whereas other peritonism cause to lie rigid) usually around costovertebral/renal angle 
+ urinary sx (visible haematuria , strangury/straining) + nausea/vomiting 

Don’t miss
• Infected obstructed stone (systemic, fever)
• AAA in older vasculopaths
• Testicular torsion in men 
• Ectopic pregnancy/ovarian torsion in women 

Generally admit for non con CT KUB (USS in children/pregnant women), urine MSU, bloods (esp calcium, urate)

Rx 
• NSAIDs- indocid/diclofenac PR, IM ketorolac, PO
• IV para or opiates 
• Tamsulosin /prazosin (relax ureters to allow distal smaller stone <10mm to pass), large = >20mm or stag horn 
• Definitive Rx 
   - Shockwave lithotripsy 
   - Ureteroscopy
   - Percutaneous nephrolithotomy 
   - May need stent or nephrostomy if obstructive 

Prevention 
• Fluid intake 2.5L/D, avoid fizzy drinks (phosphorus promotes stones), reduced salt (<6g)
• Calcium oxalate stones post common (low oxalate diet red meat choc etoh etc)
• K citrate and TZD off licence for prevention
