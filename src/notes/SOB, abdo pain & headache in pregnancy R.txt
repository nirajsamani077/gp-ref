SOB, abdo pain & headache in pregnancy RW

SOB DDx 
• Can be physiological due to higher O2 requirement, progestagenic effect & gravid uterus pressing on diaphragm but always R/O other cause
• Emergencies = PE, peripartum CM & severe anaemia 
• Common = Always consider pneumonia/sepsis or worsening pre-existing UL asthma too 
• Low threshold to admit for adjusted D dimer/CTPA/VQ/echo/FBC if any abnormality or concern
• Should have flu jab 

Abdo pain 2nd/3rd TM - ask about bleeding, headache/vision. vomiting, swelling of limbs 
• Emergencies = Preterm labour or labour (?tightenings), PET, abruption, acute fatty liver & chorioamnionitis (SROM/PROM?)
• Common = Braxton-Hicks, SPD /round ligament pain (stitch like pain, DOE, physio and analgesia, no cure apart from delivery)
• Don’t forget non obs = UTI, appendicitis, pancreatitis, cholecystitis 

Headache - always check Bp, urine & neuro 
• Emergencies = PET, CVST (venous sinus thrombosis with subacute headache + neuro/raised ICP) with DDx IIH or bleed - needs PET work up imaging MRI MRA MRV 
• Can also have reversible cerebral vasoconstriction syndrome (RCVS) post partum where several months thunderclap headache, refer  anyway as needs MR angio 
• Common = Tension, migraine, cluster (will be the cause 2/3 of time)
• Don’t forget meningitis 
• Management - can’t use NSAIDs, in 3rd TM risk of premature closure of PDA, para /codeine, triptans safe but don’t initiate first time in preg,most migraine prevention may be safe but risk of IUGR - discuss .
