Non traumatic knee RW

For non traumatic most common is OA 
• Anterior = PF OA, patellofemoral pain syndrome, patellar tendinopathy, prepatellar bursitis, Osgood schlatter TT in young 
• Medial = Medial Oa, MCL/MM injury, pes anserine bursitis
• Lateral = Lateral Oa, LCL injury, ITB syndrome (ileotibial band)

Patellofemoral pain syndrome “squatters knee”
• Chrondromalacia patellae /anterior knee pain 
• Young (<40), active sports hiking etc, gradual anterior knee pain worse with activities, much worse on squatting or getting up from chair. Early active physio 6-12w with patellar taping to reduce tracking and foot orthotics, most get better 

Patellar tendinopathy “jumpers knee”
• Overuse tendon injury in jumpers or weight lifting 
• Tender lower pole of patellar worse on jumping 
• Eccentric loading exercises reducing activity and physio (single leg squats, knee extension with band resistance), recovery in months 

Osgood TT “young knee” 
• Adolescent TT pain due to tibial apophysitis as patellar tendon pulls on developing TT
• Tender TT, RICE, can take up to 1y to heal 

Iliotibial band syndrome (ITBS) “runners knee” 
• Lateral knee pain when foot strikes ground anywhere from knee up laterally to hip 
• RICE, NSAIDs and then physio for ITB stretching exercises (side bends/leaning) & review running technique
