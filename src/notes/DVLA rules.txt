DVLA rules

Single seizure = 6 months, 12 months if MRI or EEG findings

Multiple seizures/epilepsy = 12 months seizure free to get licence, shouldn’t drive while meds being withdrawn until 6 months after last dose

Cardiology :
Elective angioplasty- 4 weeks 
CABG - 4 weeks 
MI - 4 weeks (1 week if treated by angioplasty)
Ppm - 1 week 
ICD - if for arrhythmia 6 months , if prophylactic 1 month, hgv never again 
Heart transplant. - 6 weeks 
Aortic aneurysm. - notify if 6cm, banned if 6.5cm + 


Syncope scale: 
Single faint = none
Single syncope explained & treated = 1 month 
Single unexplained syncope = 6 months 
>1 unexplained syncope = 12 months 

Stroke/TIA = 1 month, inform if persistent neuro 
Multiple TIA = 3 months, inform 

Craniotomy = 1 year, can be 6 months if benign meningioma or pituitary tumour if seizure free & no visual impairment 

Narcolepsy/cataplexy - only once sx controlled
Chronic neuro (MS) - inform and fill PK1 form 

OSA - inform in all cases when causing daytime sleepiness (Epworth >10), if moderate/severe sleep clinic needs to ensure sx controlled 

Mania/psychosis - inform + stop 3mo if stable, 6mo if unstable 

Mild dementia/LD - inform + may be able to drive 

DM
• Regular(group 1) - if diet/metformin don’t need to inform, any hypo tabs must have no hypos within 12mo & inform after 2 hypos, if insulin must inform + no hypo within 12mo, check BM 2-hourly while driving, if <4 eat & stop for certain time 
• HGV (group 2) - if on any hypo drug or insulin must inform & 5 strict criteria to drive such as no severe hypo within 12mo, full hypo awareness, regular BM monitoring around driving/generally, understands risks of DM & no debarring impairment (need to fill VDIAB1I form)

Alcohol /drugs 
If misuse or  cannabis- banned for 6mo
If dependence  or harder stuff - for 1yr

Visual 
Needs to be >6/12 (20m Reg on test) 
If field affected check 
If monocular vision inform 
If bleoharospasm consultant
