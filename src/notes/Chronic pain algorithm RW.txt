Chronic pain algorithm RW

Pain > 3mo which can be primary (no clear cause or impact of cause OOP to pain) often chronic pain syndromes (fibromyalgia, CRPS) Vs secondary to underlying cause (Oa, endometriosis) 

Main management for primary (and secondary) chronic pain 
• Exercise programme (not physio) - supervised group based physical activity 
• Psychological therapy (CBT or ACT acceptance & commitment therapy for helping with psychological distress associated with chronic pain , mindfulness techniques)
• Acupuncture - 5h total, may help, via community based physio or FCP
• Consider antidepressant (off -label) even in absence of depression (amitryptilline, duloxetine, SSRI) - may help for chronic pain but studies not conclusive and shared care decision /trial 
• Refer pain clinic 

Do not offer for chronic primary pain
• Para, NSAIDs, opiates, gabapentinoids, steroid injections, TENS /biofeedback (no evidence for chronic pain, may have some harm, shared care decisions) 
• Mindfullness, chiropractor massage need more research 

PAIN
• Physical activity/Psychological (CBT ACT)
• Acupuncture & Antidepressants
• Involve pain team /integrated pain team 
• No para NSAIDs opiates gabas tens 

Specific chronic secondary pain syndromes 
• LBP/sciatica - START Back tool for non specific to help advise re self help, physio, CBT, pain clinic. If meds - non specific = NSAID + PPI short term, not para alone. If neuro/sciatica - NSAID + PPI short term, weak opiate max 12 weeks then refer if not helping, not neuropathic meds, can consider spinal LA/Steroid injection and surgery (microdisectomy), can consider v short term diazepam as muscle or mind relaxant 
• Neuropathic pain (non sciatic) - general = amitryptiline, gapapentinoid or duloxetine, tramadol only for rescue therapy (no other opiates), trigeminal neuralgia CBZ
• Complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS) - post fracture or surgery, disproportionate pain/sensory change, acute phase usually resolves without intervention <1y, chronic persists with cool oedematous limb. PT OT useful, refer pain clinic, oral pred and bisphosphonate may have role 
• Headache/migraine - acute nsaid/aspirin, para, triptan. Prophylaxis and avoid overuse of acute = MOH. No opiates or gabapentinoids 
• IBS - Initial FODMAP mebeverine buscopan, if failed low dose TCA (5-10mg amitryptilline), SSRI 2nd line, no opiates! CBT/hypnotherapy if >12mo (refractory IBS)
• OA - Weight loss, ice packs, exercises/physio, topical NSAIDs, then oral NSAIDS + PPI, then steroid injection (short lived benefits, max 3-4x/y), don’t routinely offer para/codeine/opiates 

Deprescribing drugs of dependency 5 R’s
• Refrain from prescribing in first place 
• Review the medication and the patient 
• Reframe their understanding of chronic pain, biopsychosocial & CBT framework 
• Reorganise the medication with a suitiable weaning plan and alternatives (PAIN)
• Refer to pain clinic or CMHT if not working 

Morphine conversion equivalents & tapering 
• 1mg oral morphine = 10mg oral codeine/tramadol
• 1mg oral morphine = 750mcg oral oxycodone
• 12mcg fentanyl Matrifen patch = 30mg oral morphine & 10mcg butrans patch = 24mg oral morphine (see cancer pain section) - very potent!
• Taper dose by 10% every 1-2w & ideally don’t change to different type as high risk of OD or acute withdrawal (anyone taking >300mg TDD morphine should be done via pain clinic or formal detox service where will be converted to long acting methadone or buprenorphine then tapered)
• If starting as a trial only ever prescribe 1w worth and review - start with immediate release oramorph & if single dose of 20mg doesn’t reduce pain then opiates will not benefit at all in LT
• Inform patients of driving risks (30x more likely to have accident on road, similar to drunk driving) .
