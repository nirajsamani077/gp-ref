Insulin in T2DM

Typically start if poor control despite maximal oral therapy, marked symptoms, high risk of complications - risk balance Vs other factors re weight, hypos, needles, psychological 

Types of insulin - regimens individualised 
• Short/rapid acting used to counter effects of meal/drink taken 15-30mins prior to it and effects last anywhere from 2-8hrs after eg novorapid, humalog, apidra and actrapid (short rather than rapid) - used as part of basal bolus and correction
• Intermediate acting eg NPH of isophane insulin such as Humulin I which usually used OD or BD and mimic effect of basal insulin secreted through day & often first choice in new T2DM in combo with oral drugs in biphasic 
• Long acting - longer basal action insulin glargine or detemir used OD for steady state eg Lantus, Levemir, Semgflee (ultra long acting = Toujeo/Tresiba useful where timing of injections OD not reliable l) 
• Premixes insulins which contain combo of short and longer acting insulin used in biphasic regimen eg Novomix 30, Humalog mix 25

Basal, basal bolus, biphasic = regimens 

Need  
• Insulin injection pens and needles & sharps bin
• BM machine, test strips and lancets -usually premeal, ketone machines also 
• Hypo kit with glucogel (40% oral glucose)and 1mg glucagon IM if severe 
• Medic alert bracelet and insulin passport 
• Insulin storage in fridge 2-8C when not in use and when in room temp need to be used within 28d, use after 30 mins at room temp 

S/E weight gain, hypos (driving imps) and injection site problems (lipodystrophy/atrophy) rotate SC sites abdomen , AL thigh for longer acting insulins, buttocks for children slowest Absorption. Good advice on NICE (change site, needle length, insulin type eg human analogue, technique, pressure on bleeding), rarely cutaneous amlyoidosis 

Change doses 10-15% and sick day dosing monitoring, carb counting and diet education
