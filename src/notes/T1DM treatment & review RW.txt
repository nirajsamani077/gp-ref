T1DM treatment & review RW

Testing & monitoring 
• Check C-peptide (endogenous insulin)+anti-GAD
• Refer to diabetes specialist & education/support 
• HbA1C target = 48, measure every 6mo at least 
• CGM gold standard for self monitoring
   - Fasting morning BM 5-7
   - Premeal BM 4-7
   - Measures glucose in interstitial fluid so may lag behind plasma, check finger prick BM if uncertain & otherwise check BM at least QDS premeal or higher if unwell 
   - “Flash” isCGM is intermittent rather than real time rtCGM 
• Annual review with specialist 

Insulin regimen 
• Basal-bolus with insulin analogues gold standard 
   - Long acting insulin detemir OD (Levemir)
   - Rapid acting insulin pre-meal (Novorapid)
• BD regimen with ordinary premixed insulin only if can’t use basal-bolus not possible 
• Insulin pump if HbA1C >69 despite optimum regimens or recurrent hypos 
• Counsel about hypo risks
   - Discuss symptoms & checking BM finger prick 
   - Fast acting glucose (gel) + long acting (toast)
   - IM glucagon & train family - if not working 999 
   - Hypo awareness -if impaired high risk of severe
   - If frequent refer BG awareness training prog 
• Sick day rules 
   - Dose increase 10-20% in event of illness 
   - Monitor BM ketones closely, risk of DKA 
   - If vomiting/no intake = no bolus, cont basal 
• Vary injection sites to avoid lipodystrophy
• If BMI >25 (or 23 BAME) offer metformin 

NB Insulins in T2DM 
• If starting rather than using insulin analogue preferred use intermediate acting NPH ordinary insulin (Insulatard, Humulin I) first line or premixed ordinary insulins (Humulin M3 Bd) - less risks of hypos and weight gain, alongside metformin etc
• Second line = long acting (Lantus/Toujeo/Levemir) insulin analogue OD (ultra long acting Tresiba only specialist), or premixed insulin analogues (Novomix/HumalogMix)

Cardiovascular risk & complications 
• Atorva 20mg primary prev if 
   - >40, had T1DM >10y, nephropathy or other RFs 
   - 80mg if had an event /confirmed CVD 
   - Aim >40% reduction non HDL 
• BP- 140/90, ACR >70 130/80, age >80  = 150/90 
   - ACE first line 
   - Dont avoid Bb or TZD - be aware of risks risks 
• Eyes - diabetic retinopathy screening annually 
• Renal - ACR >3 offer ACE, tighter BP if >70, not SGLT2 as risks of DKA 
• Neuropathy - foot checks, neuropathic meds, gastroparesis common = use small particle mashed diet, prokinetics (metoclopramide) & erythromycin only short term risks EPES 
• Consider ED, MH, eating disorders & other AI .
