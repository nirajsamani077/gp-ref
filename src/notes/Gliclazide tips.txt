I only really prescribe it during early stages of new diagnosis in patients with very high HbA1c’s to try and get it down more rapidly.

It becomes less effective as pancreatic function declines in those who have been diabetic for longer - these folks will need insulin

They need to have hypo and driving advice. They need to check BGs before driving (five to drive etc). Different rules for those with group 2 licence

I tend to leave them on a starting dose for at least a month before reviewing and a week prior to the review appointment will ask them to do some pre breakfast BGs (fasting)and amend the dose if needed but ideally once their sugars improve get them onto other orals and try to wean down the gliclazide. Obviously if they have hypos then speak with them sooner.

The only times I ask them to do BGs is before driving, if they think they’re having a hypo or for a week before any planned dose changes. Most will be ok with this.

Usual advice is to take with meal times ie twice daily. You can start at a lower dose and titrate. I usually ask for BM diary to base the decision if it’s helping or not , hba1 may take a while. There is a driving leaflet I usually give as the rules can be a bit tricky to remembe
