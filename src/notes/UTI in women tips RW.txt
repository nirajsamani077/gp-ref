UTI in women tips RW

Common, affects 1/3

Asymptomatic bacteriuria often in elderly, sexually active, diabetic, catheterised but not UTI without symptoms 

Can dip only if simple sounding UTI <65 (or even not based on sx alone if highly suggestive) but usually any complicating factor/>1 symptoms or >65 should be sending all for MC+S

Mixed growth /epithelial cells usually indicate contaminated sample - repeat if symptomatic 

Sterile Pyuria - consider PV infection, Gynae/ovarian path or non culturable organism eg TB

For simple cystitis (not a full lower UTI) can try 48hr without abx with simple analgesia and fluids (no good evidence for otc cranberry juice, D-mannose, alkalinising agents)

For lower UTI 3d
• Nitro MR 100mg BD (consider renal Fx eGFR <45) or TMP 200mg BD (not if recently treated as resistance risk) 
• Amox 500 TDS, Cefalexin 500mg BD or TDS or Pivmecillinam 400mg stat then 200mg BD 2nd line 

For upper UTI 7-10d
• Cef as above or coamox 500/125 TDS / TMP 7d if sensitivities 

For catheter related UTI 
• All will have biofilm colonised bacteriuria (Uroshield is USS device attached to catheter but has limited evidence in reducing risk)
• Ensure fever /upset not another infective focus (usually only 1/3 times it is a catheter UTI)
• Always clean MSU, always consider change if in situ >7d, low theshold for upper UTI Rx/admission 

UTI in pregnancy 
• Critical not to miss, risk of stillbirth miscarriage 
• Booking will have urine MSU & if any symptoms dip and send MSU
• Any bacteriuria even asymptomatic should be treated with 7d ABx & follow up MSU for TOC
  - Nitro 100mg BD unless 3rd TM (risk NN haemolysis) 
  - 2nd line = Amox or Cefalex 500mg BD 7d
  - Upper = admit, Cef up to TDS or coamox 7-10d
• Mixed growth a bit tricky but if contaminated with epithelials best approach is to repeat 
• Pyuria without bacteriuria (sterile Pyuria) think about cause but don’t need to Rx in same way 
• Any GBS = alert & intrapartum IV Abx .
