Dysphagia RW

All need 2WW UGI (?oesophageal Ca or HNSCC)
If swallowing unsafe = same day admission
SALT & dietician very important - thickened fluids 

Specifics on history 
• Feeling of location doesn’t correlate to anatomy
• If solids harder to swallow suggests stricture
• If liquids harder suggests achalasia or VC palsy (secondary to lung/breast) = ++ risk of aspiration 
• Globus = lump in throat when swallowing saliva but usually settles with E&D (not dysphagia)
• RF = smoking, ETOH, Paan (betel nut)/HPV 

Examination 
• Check oropharynx & ENT, cervical LN (Virchows)
• Hoarse voice/weak cough - VC palsy
• CN9-12 palsy - say ahh, uvula palate dev, tongue 
• Abdo/Epigastric mass 

Non-malignant causes of dysphagia 
• Oral = Poor dentition, dentures, TMJ dysfunction 
• Pharyngeal (airway protection)
   - Neuro (post stroke, MND, MS, PD) bulbar palsy 
   - Pharyngeal pouch/infection/DXT
• Oesophageal 
   - Oesophagitis/peptic stricture/thyroid compress
   - Achalasia/dysmotility 
   - Systemic sclerosis 

Eosinophilic oesophagitis
• Allergen mediated inflammation (IgE mediated) with UGI symptoms (endoscopic Bx Dx), most common trigger milk or wheat, often FHx or atopic 
• Rx - orodispersible budesonide 1st line (off licence PPI or ICS fluticasone), elemental diet/avoid trigger, may need balloon dilation 

Oesophageal spasm 
• Chest pain, dysphagia, reflux - sometimes related to intake, often undiagnosed, due to spasms of oesophageal smooth muscle (nutcracker = sudden high pressure contraction, normal motility, achalasia = dysmotility, impaired LOS relaxation) 
• Dx - OGD, barium swallow, manometry
• High dose PPI & lifestyle, if failed gastro may consider GTN/CCB (off license) or dilation
