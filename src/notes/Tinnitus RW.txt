Tinnitus RW

Common, esp with age, most horrible but harmless 
Objective can be heard, pulsatile consider vascular  
Consider MH affects (mini TQ test), QOL/sleep 
Worry about UL/asymmetric without obvious cause 

Check 
• Otoscopy - wax infection 
• Rinnes and Webers - SNHL or CHL with gross hearing (whisper test 3 ns at arms length occluding other ear) 
• CN7 

Tinnitus + vertigo = think stroke (sudden onset), acoustic neuroma (can be sudden onset SNHL), Menieres or vestibular migraine (usually no HL)

Audiology and MRI IAM, tympanometry if ? Drum issue related to ETD or CHL 

Any symmetrical non pulsatile tinnitus with no neurology or other Sx generally don’t need scan 
• Most resolve by itself (can take years) wax and wane learning to live with it “tinnitus support” British Tinnitus Association and CBT 

Refer routine all with persistent, objective, UL with HL /vertigo not sudden no neuro, or significant MH
• If HL refer hearing aid if affecting communication
