Domestic violence (DV) & abuse RW

Controlling coercive threatening or physical abuse to partner or family member (can be physical, sexual, psychological, financial, emotional)

May be scared, lacking confidence, be dependent or worried about children, or just not recognise it as abuse or be too ashamed

Signs maybe recurrent vague presentations, MH issues, partner attending & dominant 
• Speak privately (alone) & sensitively 
• Do you feel safe at home? Has somebody hurt you? Have you felt pressurised or controlled?

Scenarios where DV/DA disclosed & management 
• Immediate safety risk - call police, SG procedure 
• Any children - discuss risks, child SG procedure 
• Calculate DASH risk tool (https://safelives.org.uk/sites/default/files/resources/Dash%20risk%20checklist%20quick%20start%20guidance%20FINAL.pdf) 
 • If DASH 14 points or <14 points with professional concerns (sexual, pregnant, stalking, isolated, escalating) = referral to MARAC (multi agency risk assessment conferences) - get consent & check with SG lead to make MARAC referral (with full details of concerns), get advice from SG lead 
 • Signpost to domestic violence services - Women’s Aid, Refuge helpline, National Stalking helpline who can help with practicalities (changing locks, emergency safety plan, emergency accom, legalities (restraining orders) etc 
 • IRIS (Identification and Referral to Improve Safety) provides training and support for DV/DA 

Honour based violence a form of abuse for family honour, forced marriage, cultural killings - charities eg Karma Nirvana available for this .
