Melanoma RW

High risk of mets (lung, renal , liver, brain, bone) with median survival with mets 6-9mo so early recognition is critical! 

Main RFs - Sunburns, fair redhead (in non-white it’s rare but can be palm/sole/lentigo), IC, multiple atypical naevi/moles (atypical mole syndrome >100 common naevi or >2 atypical naevi with indistinct border - esp with FHx = refer derm)

2WW criteria: ABCD2 (EFG) or 7 point checklist 

ABCD2
• Assymetrical 
• Border irregular 
• Colour inconsistent 
• Diameter >6mm or Dimensions changing 

EFG for nodular melanoma & SCC (if not an obvious papulonodular BCC, also pyogenic granuloma rapid vascular lesion which needs Bx)
• Elevated 
• Firm 
• Growth, persistent 

7 point checklist = Major (2) & Minor (1) criteria 
•Major = change in size, irreg shape, irreg colour 
•Minor = diameter >7mm, inflamm, ooze, sensation 
Score >3 is suspicious, refer if any major category 

Other points 
• Dermatoscopy can help but only if trained 
• Amelanocytic = 10%, pink lesions, be suscpicious if fitting criteria 

When 2WW to derm 
• Excision biopsy with margin & prognosis depends on Breslow thickness (how deep it has infiltrated) & presence of microscopic ulceration 
• Curative excision and may remove further margin if not clear but if mets then targeted immunotherapy (against BRAF) may be used
