Valve disease RW

Refer any murmur, along with FHx/symptoms/ECG for an echo & cardiologist assessment 

Aortic stenosis (AS) 
• Common, usually older 
• ESM 2nd ICS RSE louder end expiration, carotids 
• Usually degenerative calcification or bicuspid AV in younger (normally tricuspid), rare = rheum fever in developing countries 
• Important signs - quiet S2, slow rising pulse 
• Any symptoms = angina, SOB, syncope/dizziness = poor prognosis would need surgery (AVR, TAVI if high surgical risk), if A/S depends on echo valve gradient, surveillance etc 
• Statins improve mortality (coexistent IHD), avoid ACE/nitrates in severe AS (preload reduction along with increased afterload = cardiogenic shock), use aspirin LT post TAVI  

Aortic regurgitation (AR) 
• Rare, usually younger 
• EDM 2nd ICS RSE down to LSE 3rd leant forward (high pitched, hard to hear, “sound in diastole”)
• Extra signs - Quinke De Musset, collapsing pulse 
• Acute: IE, MI, aortic root. Chronic: Bicuspid AV,RF
• LV volume overload - causes SOB CCF 
• Surgical management - AVR, TAVI if high risk 

Mitral regurgitation (MR)
• Common, with AF 
• PSM 5th ICS mid clav line to axilla, end expiration 
• Primary: Degenerative, IE, RF, CT disease. Secondary to LV dysfunction with normal valve (functional MR)
• LV volume overload - causes SOB CCF
• Surgical - MVR or repair/TEER with a clip 

Mitral stenosis (MS)
• MDM rumbling apex, snapping S1, malar flush 
• Rheumatic (RF) which usually causes left sided valve disease or degenerative 
• Surgical MVR or valvulotomy 
• Diuretucs, Bb, LA nitrates for symptoms 

Rheumatic fever - rare “carditis” & “arthritis” in developing countries with L sided valve lesions (MS most common) - usually post strep infection (scarlet fever, swab for GAS). Refer cardiology, long term pen V to prevent recurrence 
• Other signs - chorea, erythema marginatum, nodules (Modified Jones criteria GAS + criteria) 

Endocarditis ABx prophylaxis for mechanical valve should not be routinely offered for dental procedures (no evidence it prevents IE) but maintain good oral hygeine
