Hep B & serology RW

HBSAG = current infection (HBEAG = infectivity)
Anti-HBS = immunity (precious infection/vaccine)
Anti-HBC = previous exposure (IgM = recent) 

Scenarios 
• Acute Hep B = HBSAG & anti-HBC IgM 
• Chronic Hep B = HBSAG & anti-HBC (not IgM)
• Resolved & immune = anti-HBS & anti-HBC 
• Vaccinated successfully = anti-HBS >10
• Susceptible, need to vaccinate = anti-HBS <10

Major pubic health issue - 95% will clear but 5% chronic & 25% will get HCC so check if 
• High risk exposure - Country, MSM, IVDU, STI, prisoners, homeless, care home etc 
• At high risk - Pregnant, immunosuppressed
• Active infection - abnormal LFTs, signs of CLD

If test negative & anti-HBS <10 = susceptible so offer vaccination 
If positive refer all to liver team to treat antivirals 
In past cleared infection some meds eg high dose pred can reactivate  .
