Elderly falls assessment RW

Common (1/3 over 65, 1/2 over 80) & important (90% of NOF & Colles #) - marker of frailty & interlinked with BP, balance, gait, sarcopenia 

Falls assessment if 
• >1 fall in last year or sought medical attention 
• Mobility or balance problems 

3 main categories of elderly fall 
• Red flag fall (needing same day ED assessment)
• Hot fall - mechanical ppt by minor illness in context of frailty 
• Spontaneous fall - no ppt,significant frailty cause

Usual Hx before during after, drugs (DOAC, BP meds anticholinergics), mobility aids & check HR, postural BPs, gait, neuro/vision, OA knee/hip
• LS BP lie 5min, BP, then standing 1min then 3min standing BP, drop of SBP >20 or DBP >10 = drop, if positive titrate down BP meds, slow transitions & increase fluid intake 2L/d, & if resistant consider A&G geriatric medicine to consider fludrocortisone 
• Timed Up and Go (TUG) screening test - Sit down on chair, stand up and walk to other side (3m approx) then back to chair, record time. If >12s suggests higher risk of falling 
• Consider 6-CIT brief validated test

Ix - Baseline bloods & ECG/tape (esp if “drop attack”, LOC, any cardiac Sx/Hx), FRAX assessment & offer DEXA based upon this 

Management 
• PT OT home hazard assessment - eg footwear, rugs, walking aids (remember stick used on opposite side to bad knee/hip as arm swing on bad side helps support load, up straight stick should reach wrist creases)
• Specific falls prevention programme /group based exercise & strength training 
• Deal with polypharmacy & deprescribe 
• Visual and audiology tests via optician 
• Treat any UC 
• Refer to falls clinic
