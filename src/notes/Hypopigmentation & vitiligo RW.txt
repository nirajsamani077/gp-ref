Hypopigmentation & vitiligo RW

Causes 
• Vitiligo - any age usually young, autoimmune melanocyte destruction causing well dermarcated symmetrical white patches (Rx = potent topical steroid OD can repigment (or 0.1% protopic BD 6mo as alternative), high factor sunscreen SPF50 & camouflage make up from Changing Faces for free consultation & FP10 items - refer derm if >10% BSA, spreading rapidly or refractory for PTX
• Post-inflammatory hypopigmentation - pale patches around any inflammatory lesion, more common in darker skin (Rx = usually self resolves but may take several months)
• Pityriasis versicolour - young adults flat scaly hypopigmented patches (Rx = Ketoconazole /Nizoral shampoo OD 5d or selenium sulphide shampoo OD 7d, colour change may take months to improve)
• Pityriasis alba - mild ezcema with scale on sun exposed areas usually noticed on sunny holiday & after return (Rx = resolves spontaneously, emollients & 1% HC if inflammation)
• Halo naevus - benign melanocytic naevus attacked by immune system causing halo depigmentation, eventually naevus goes leaving patch (Rx = refer if Dx uncertainty as some malignant melanoma can develop halos)
• Rare = lichen planus, T cell lymphoma, leprosy
