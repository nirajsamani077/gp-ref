Precocious puberty RW

Suspect in 
• Girls <8 with thelarche /growth, more common but often less pathological 
• Boys <9 with testicular development, less common often pathological 

Features of puberty 
• Adrenarche - Adrenal axis activation, androgenic sx pubic/facial/axil hair (pubarche) lower voice etc
• Thelarche - breast growth, any <8 is early = refer 
• Menarche - periods , any <8 is early = refer 
• Genital change -testicular volume, penile growth, cliteromegaly (can be sign of virilism)
• Growth spurt - it crossing centile 3-6mo = refer 

Check 
• Parents age of puberty, mid parental height 
• Any neuro sx = red flags, headache, vision, DI
• McCune Albright - genetic mosaciasim, autonomous ovarian cysts secreting hormones causing hyperthyroid, Cushingoid, UL cafe au lait spots/facial asymmetry = rare 

Use Tanner staging (1-5) and RCPCH growth charts (have puberty lines for age limits, any rapid growth crossing 1 centile = refer)

Causes of precocious puberty 
• GDPP - Early GNRH secretion (dependent) = idiopathic/genetic factors, CNS tumour/infection, rare genetic (NF1)
• GIPP - Increased sex steroid hormone (independent of GNRH) = androgen producing virilising tumour (ovarian adrenal testicular), CAH of McCune Albright 

Management = refer all suspected, many have watch and wait, if idiopathic GDPP may consider GNRH analogues. Remember early menarche increases breast ca risk
