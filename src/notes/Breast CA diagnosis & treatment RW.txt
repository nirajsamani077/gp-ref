Breast CA diagnosis & treatment RW

2WW breast if lump >30 or nipple >50 
• Any unexplained breast /skin/axillary lump >30 
• Any UL nipple discharge/changes >50 
Non urgent if <50, persistent pain, nipple ezcema

RFs - background population = 1/8 so v common 
• Age, Obesity, ETOH probably largest RFs overall 
• HRT - continuous combined highest risk, micronised progs better, much lower than other lifestyle factors, doubles background relative risk which is still low, persists 10y after stopping, longer use >5y has higher risk
• Genetics - BRCA 1/2 mutations small proportion

Diagnosis triple test - exam, imaging (mammogram, USS) & biopsy (tissue Dx, with sentinel axillary LN biopsy)

Types 
• Invasive ductal carcinoma (80%) & DCIS “pre-invasive” usually asymptomatic on mammogram - Rx depends on ER/PR/HER2 status 
• Invasive lobular cancer (10%) from glandular milk producing cells 
• Triple negative (neg ER/PR/HER2) rare aggressive related to BRCA1 
• Inflammatory breast Ca which mimics mastitis so safety net & f/u if needed following mastitis 

Stages 
• 1 = localised to breast , 2 = breast + IL axillary LN
• 3 = locally advanced >5cm, 4 = metastatic 

Treatment via MDT, breast, oncologists, CNS 
• Surgery (mastectomy, WLE, axillary node clearance (lymphedema risk), reconstructive surgery with implants or self tissue 
• Chemo - PREDICT tool to decide pre or post op, several s/e including BM suppression cardiotoxicity
• DXT - usually after WLE, can cause skin changes soreness nausea 
• Hormonal - in ER positive premenopause tamoxifen postmenopause anastrozole for at least 5yrs. Some have GNRH injections for ovarian suppression.
• Biological - HER2 positive (15%) get herceptin (transtuzumab) IV 3 monthly for 1y, cardiotoxic (reversible ventricular dysfunction) 
• Bone protection (IV zoledronate also useful for bone mets/MSCC risk), 

CRUK & McMillan websites for support 

Post-treatment issues 
• Lymphedema - fluid accumulation in limb due to damaged lymphatic drainage, occurs in 20% usually in first couple years after Rx. Refer lymphedema nurses for multilayer bandaging/pneumatic compression, specific exercises to help flow & manual drainage by massage 
• Effects of hormonal treatments 
   - Tamoxifen in premenopausal women blocks ER but has ER agonist effects elsewhere (risk endometrial Ca & VTE), SE = menopause like flushes sweats hair thinning vaginal dryness. Metabolite interacts with fluoxetine/duloxetine & bupropion so should avoid any coprescribing!
   - Anastrozole/letrozole in post menopausal women (blocks conversion of androgens to oestrogen in peripheries but doesn’t increase overall oestrogen) - main risks are of oestrogen deficiency ie osteoporosis (rather than ER agonist)
   - Shouldn’t give any HRT for menopausal symptoms routinely unless specialist (breast, Gynae & oncologist)  - mainstay if non hormonal with SSRI (sertraline & citalopram preferred over above if on tamoxifen), avoid vaginal oestrogen too (lubricants mainstay) unless specialist 
   - All should have FRAX, DEXA, QRISK & counselling over psychological/sexual/fatigue 
   - May switch from anastrozole to tamoxifen in some women who can’t tolerate 
   - Risk balance between treating 5 or 10y with anti-oestrogen hormonal treatment (reducing risk of recurrence vs increasing risk of endometrial Ca, VTE, osteoporosis, CVD)
