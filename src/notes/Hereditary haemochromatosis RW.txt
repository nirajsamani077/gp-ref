Hereditary haemochromatosis RW

Autosomal recessive mutation (chromosome 6) affecting HFE gene which regulates GI iron absorption leading to iron overload 

Homozygous = 1:300, carrier = 1:10
M affected earlier as F have menstrual loss 

Presentation 
• Incidental raised ferritin or FHx
• Chronic liver disease 
• Arthritis (pseudogout)
• Bronze diabetes (pancreas) and endocrinopathy (pituitary invasion causing hypogonadism)
• CCF from cardiac 

Exam 
• Slate grey appearance
• CLD Signs - jaundice, hepatomegaly, ascites 
• Scars - liver transplant (rooftop/J shaped), biopsy scars or venesection scars in ACF
• Softer signs - joint replacement, SC insulin injection sites 

Ix: 
Screening - ferritin/transferrin sat unless family Hx = HFE gene (C282Y and H63D mutations)
Ferritin >500, TSAT M >50% F >55%, low TIBC
Other- HBA1C, AFP, USS liver, CXR, ECG, echo 
Liver Bx for diagnosis, staging 

Rx: 
Venesection - 1 unit/week til iron deficient (aim TSAT & ferritin <50) then 3-4x/year - desferroxamine = 2nd line

If cirhosis 200x increase risk of HCC (normal life expectancy if not and treated) - so HCC surveillance with AFPs/USS and ETOH avoidance
