Allergic food/drug reactions RW

Report under yellow card & avoid if serious harm 

If non serious harm 
• Immunological eg allergic, eruption/morbilliform rash then consider avoiding but typically morbilliform reactions occur along with viral infections so can be retried later if shared agreement 
• Non-immunological - known side effect eg myalgias with statins, thrush with oral steroids etc, consider restarting depending on situation 

Re Peanut allergy in children 
• Introduce peanut at weaning 6mo or at 4mo in infants with ezcema as prevention (unless high risk wirh strong FHx consider referral in latter) - early sustained exposure can be preventative 
• Oral immunotherapy can desensitise up to 50% but high risk of anaphylaxis during treatment 

Food allergy consider 
• IgE Vs non IgE mediated (skin GI resp other) - atopic ezcema, reflux, constipation & poor growth are non IgE mediated & delayed, IgE mediated rapid and typical 
• Refer paeds allergy clinic for skin prick test (SPT) or IgE RASTs for the foods if sx sound IgE mediated 
• If non IgE mediated consider 6 week elimination then reintroduce, dietician, if CMPA hypoallergenic formula or mother excluding cows milk in breastfed
