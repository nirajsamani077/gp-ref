Acute testicular pain RW

Either torsion or epididymo-orchitis
• Refer immediately ED if signs of torsion or systemically unwell
• Otherwise send urine MSU & first pass Urine NAAT for chlamydia and gonorrhoea 

If low risk for STI (older, urology issues etc)
• Ofloxacin 200mg BD 14d or Levofloxacin 500mg OD 10d (counsel risk of quinolone tendinopathy)
• If C/I quinolones = coamoxiclav
• Consider USS if uncertainty or sx remain after 2d

If high risk of STI (younger, sexual partners, no barrier, precious Sti, only Leucs on dip) 
• Refer to GUM /Umbrella clinic for testing and ensure urine NAAT checked, if ?gonorrhoea will be IM cef 1g STAT + doxy 100mg BD 10-14d (+ ofloxacin if ?chlamydia)
• Gonorrhoea more likely if urethral Dc and high risk sexual activity, abstinence, contact tracing 

Distinguishing between torsion and EO infection 
• Torsion usually sudden onset severe pain swelling nausea vomiting may have hx of trauma or intermittent whereas EO is less acute pain swelling fever and other sx related to infection 
• Examination 
   - Torsion - tender swollen testicle high riding horizontal angle (shortened cord), “Prehns” sign negative (lifting testicle relieves pain), no cremasteric reflex (stroke /pinch inner thigh causes testicle to lift)
   - EO - tender swollen epididymis back of testicles, redness, discharge, “Prehns” positive, cremaster present, reactive hydrocele
