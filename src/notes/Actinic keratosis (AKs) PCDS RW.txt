Actinic keratosis (AKs) PCDS RW

Rough macules sun exposed, can get scaly - due to sun damage 
• 25% resolve by itself with no treatment 
• Rarely can transform to SCC (higher risk if >1cm, growing, more/field of lesions or IC)

If papule nodule or ulcer >3 weeks with features of growing bleeding keratotic ulcerating - 2WW, not an AK and more likely an SCC 

Grade 1 - scale palpable > visible. 
Grade 2 - scale thick & easily seen. 
Grade 3 - very thick scale 
Field - multiple AKs (eg forehead)

Treatment 
• Sometimes leave alone (25% resolve)
• Sun factor 50 & vitamin D for all 
• Grade 1/2= Efudix cream (5-FU) - once at night for 4 weeks, wash off after 8hrs, expect +++ erythema crusting discomfort (more than Solaraze but more effective), can co-apply eumovate in mornings if needed 
• Thicker grade 2 = consider Actikerall solution (5-FU & salicylic acid) for longer on skin & 12wks
• Grade 3 = ?2WW, cryotherapy, curretage,cautery 
• Field grade 1s/thin = Solaraze gel (3% diclofenac) BD 8-12 weeks, review improvement after further month as can continue for further 1 month 
• Field and thick = Efudix or Aldara (imiquimod) 3 nights a week for 4 weeks, which are the strongest treatments, esp Aldara for confluent lesions
