MSK examination tips RW

Look feel move 
Functional movements 
Joint above and below 

2 minute GALS 
• Gait 
• Arms - Arms behind head (shoulder and elbow flexion), arms outstretched, make first, opposition & squeeze MCP (inflamm)
• Legs lying down - Bend both knees up (crepitus ROM), hip ER and IR (often lost OA), patellar tap and quick ligs (Lachmans), look at feet obvious stuff and MTP squeeze (inflamm, Morton’s)
• Spine standing - posture KLS, touch toes with 3 fingers on back (should move apart), neck movement range 

Special tests 
• Disc prolapse Back - SLR and slump (sit up, slump, extend knee and DF ankle = pain
• Neck - Spurlings test for cervical radiculopathy passive laterally flex neck to affected side then press down on top of head = pain 
• Hip - Trendelenberg waddling gait (proximal gluteal weakness) and test (stand on one leg, normally hips level but if pelvis drops one side weakness on other side)
• Knee - Lachmans (lying flat, move tibia forward of femur with left hand securing thigh right hand on TT moving back and forward, if no fixed end point = ?ACL tear), squat and waddle test (can’t do it too painful in meniscal injury)
• Foot ankle - stand one leg and tiptoes (no arch from side even when doing this if pes planus)
• Shoulder (all impingement)- painful arc, can drop test (Neers) and Hawkins (your arm through armpit to other shoulder), scarf for ACJ
• Elbow - Tennis elbow worse with chair lifting with 3 finger grip 
• Hand /wrist - Finkelsteins (wrist sideways over edge of table with thumb side and UD causes pain, move passively UD causes pain, move thumb passively flexing into palm causes pain - pain on the “selfie” position for DQTS), axial thumb pressure down grind test for OA thumb base, Pulling thumb for scaphoid, Tinels and Phalens don’t add much for CTS 

Paed GALS - “copy me” game 
• Gait - walk, tip toe, heels 
• Arms - hands out, fists, oppose, prayer, hands behind head, squeeze MCPs
• Legs - lying down slide foot up to touch bottom then lift rotate hip 
• Spine - check spine curve, ears to shoulder, look at ceiling, touch toes
