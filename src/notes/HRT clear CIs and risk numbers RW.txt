HRT clear C/Is and risk numbers RW

• Current or past breast CA (or hormone sensitive) but FHx depends on circumstances (eg age genetics <40)
• VTE or very strong FHx , Thrombophilia 
• Recent MI/stroke/ severe IHD / poor BP (160/95)
• Active undiagnosed bleeding or endometrial 
• Active liver disease, counsel if gallstones 

Migraine not C/I but use transdermal rather than oral to reduce stroke risk 
Any doubts refer specialist menopause clinic 

Phrase 
“HRT is associated with small risks, but for the majority of symptomatic menopausal and perimenopausal women, the benefits appear to outweigh the risks”

Risk numbers & convos 
• VTE - transdermal has almost no increased risk (bypasses liver FPM), background risk anyway is 1-2 out of 1000 which is very low but oral HRT puts this up to 3-4 out of a 1000 which is double but still low - much higher is smoking obese etc , if at high risk FHx for example refer haematology 
• Breast Ca - combined more than single, micronised progs better, much lower than other lifestyle factors, MHRA risk table useful , baseline risk around 10 for every 1000, increases to 20 in every 1000 on combined HRT, so doubles but still very low, risks seems to persist after stopping for 10 years and longer use >5y has higher risk where numbers up with age anyway go up from 50 to 100 for every 1000, but for context breast CA is common 1 in 7 lifetime risk in women mostly due to age and genetics, only 2% of all cases due to hormones (8% each for etoh/smoking, obesity and even 5% for not breastfeeding)
• Very slight increase risk of ovarian CA, reduced risk of colorectal Ca, cervical Ca/vaginal Ca risk not higher or lower - role of hormones is debatable, generally small factor, poorly understood
