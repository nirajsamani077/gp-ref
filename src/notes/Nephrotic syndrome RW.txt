Nephrotic syndrome RW

Most common paed renal disease (usually <10yo), often trigger like infection/allergic reaction then 
• Heavy proteinuria/ hypoalbuminaemia with ++ oedema (periorbital, hands feet, ascites) & frothy urine and rarely HTN (severe)
• Increase risk of infection & thrombosis 

If suspecting immediate paeds referral after Urine dipstick & BP check - for urine PCR, bloods (albumin, UE, ANA, ANCA, antistrep, C3-4), USS & genetic screening & renal Bx (most will have minimal change GN meaning kidneys normal on Bx)

Types 
• Congenital (<3mo)
• Idiopathic (most common) - steroid sensitive (most common) vs resistant 
• Can also be defined by remission (dip negative >3d), relapse (dip 3+ >3d or oedema), frequent relapse or steroid-dependent 

Treatment  
• Acute = pred 2mg/kg/d 4-6w then taper (1.5mg/kg same time) & prophylactic anticoag and Rx infections 
• Monitor at home with urine dip for protein, definitions as above, should be done twice weekly at least and daily if unwell or starting to get >1+
• Relapse = pred as aboce until dip negative 3d then taper and re dip urine daily, should also monitor BP and weight twice weekly 
• Avoid live vaccines (intranasal flu, MMR, BCG) if on immunosuppression, can have 5 yearly pneumococcal (Abs reduce rapidly in renal disease) & flu IM
