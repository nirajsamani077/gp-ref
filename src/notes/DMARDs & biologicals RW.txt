DMARDs & biologicals RW

Conventional, biologicals and targeted newer ones (the citinibs - Janus kinase (JAK) inhibitors!) eg baricitinib which are oral

Always started secondary care and then shared care agreement for continuing and monitoring 

Usually effect and adverse effects within first 3mo (GI upset mouth ulcers tend to settle with time)

Only stop in severe infection needing hospital (don’t stop routinely for surgery) 

All can cause BM suppression, hepatitis, pneumonitis 

• MTX - weekly SC or PO, folic acid 5mg on other 6 days (never same day as interferes with MTX absorption), can cause cirhosis, don’t prescribe TMP (interferes folate metabolism)
• Leflunomide - HTN, peripheral neuropathy, very long half life (so stop really really long time before getting pregnant!)
• Sulfasalazine - Neuropsych, oligospermoozia
• HCQ - Neuropsych, retinopathy (annual ophthalmology screening after 5yrs) worsens psoriasis 

Monitoring (apart from HCQ) initially FBC U&E LFT monthly for first 3mo then 3 monthly (MTX, SFS) or 2 monthly (LFN)

If ALT  >2x ULN - d/w rheum if unwell admit, consider stopping & other causes
If eGFR <30 typically stop and discuss 

Biologics if failed to respond to 2 DMARDs 
• Anti-TNFs (infliximab IV, adalimumab, etanercept SCs) - reactivate TB, monitor FBC U&E LFT, CXR rule out TB and risk of ca with all 
• Anti-IL6 (tocilizumab IV/SC) - monitor lipids, false CRP in infection 
• Rituximab - IV, rare cause progressive encephalopathy (PML)
• Decisions by specialist to withhold with infections etc, no live vaccines
