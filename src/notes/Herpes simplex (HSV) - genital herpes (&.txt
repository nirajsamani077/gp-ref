Herpes simplex (HSV) - genital herpes (& cold sores) RW

Cold sores HSV1 or HSV2 - topical/oral aciclovir
Genital herpes HSV-2 - oral aciclovir
Risk of gingivostomatitis - IV aciclovir

Initial infection (direct contact, would be sexual for genital!) often A/S, lies dormant in sensory ganglia, then reactivates when unwell or IC (HIV increases viral shedding ++) & is always painful! 
• Blisters/vesicles oral anogenital, can even cause cervicitis proctitis & lymphadenitis, dysuria stings!
• Constitutional (fever myalgia) usually primary 
• Comp-bacterial superinfection,aseptic meningitis  

Diagnosis - Viral HSV swab base of lesion (NAAT), no blood test in primary care (HSV-specific abs)

Cold sores (herpes labialis) management 
• Mild self limiting lesions lips cheeks nose usually resolve by itself within 2w, 1st HSV exposure
• Prodrome of neuro type sx burning tingling & then vesicles few hours later that can rupture crust and heal - 90% seroconversion without sx but 10% can cause primary gingivostomatitis miserable then migrates to sensory ganglia 
• Self care = avoid touching lesions sharing kissing oral sex hand hygeine ++ 
• In most antivirals not needed but consider oral aciclovir within 5d or if new lesions still forming = 400mg TDS 5d (or 200mg 5x/d) if severe lesions gingivostomatitis or primary infection - if IC seek specialist advice 
• Topical aciclovir 5% ointment (Zovirax) may be less effective than oral = 5 times a day 5d (may extend to 10d) so not often used 
• Recurrent = consider prophylaxis/suppressive treatment as below if >6 episodes a year or comps 
• Comps = dehydration (oral intake), ezcema herpeticum severe (any child with ezcema & herpes infection should have aciclovir!), herpetic keratoconjunctivitis (eye rubbing after touching lesion), erythema multiforme & pneumonia/meningitis 
• DDx of primary herpetic gingivostomatitis in children = HFMD, chickenpox, erythema multiforme & ALL/leukaemia in an ill child!

Genital herpes management  
• For pain- saline bath, analgesia, lidocaine 5% ointment or gel prior to passing urine 
• Aciclovir (or val) - start within 5d of episode 
    - Oral aciclovir 400mg TDS 5d (or 200mg 5x/d), if recurrent can use episodic shorter course higher dose treatment early (eg aciclovir 800mg TDS 2d)
• Abstain from sex during acute episodes of genital herpes - but partner notification important as asymptomatic shedding is common esp within first year of infection & big reason for transmission, condoms & suppressive aciclovir reduce the risk of transmission but don’t completely prevent (90%)
• Recurrent = Suppressive treatment - if >6 episodes a year or a HSV discordant relationship (one has it, one doesn’t) can use aciclovir 400mg BD/QDS (increase to 400mg TDS if breakthrough lesions), usually for 1 year then stop & if >2 recurrences restart long term 

In pregnancy HSV risk highest in 3rd TM, esp <6w from delivery lax due to risk of neonatal meningitis lesions & disseminated infection 
- Discuss all with obstetrics, should be under consultant led care & have elective CS (if infection earlier than 3rd TM could have NVD with suppressive Rx as long as no recurrences 
- Treat with aciclovir, safe & well tolerated
