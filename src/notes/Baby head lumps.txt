Baby head lumps 

Caput succedaneum 
- fluid collection due to pressure of scalp on cervix 
- present at birth 
- crosses suture lines, usually at vertex
- resolves spontaneously 

Chignon
- venthouse suction cup swelling, disappears after few hours usually 

Cephalohaematoma
- bleeding between skull & periosteum, often from instrumental delivery 
- appears few days after birth 
- doesn’t cross suture lines (subperiosteal), usually parietal 
- persists for up to 3 months as takes time to absorb, may cause jaundice/anaemia/hypotension

Subgaleal haematoma 
- bleeding above periosteum between scalp aponeurosis, appearing few days after birth, bruising, crosses suture lines & can cause systemic upset
