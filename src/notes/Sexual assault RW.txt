Sexual assault RW

1 in 4 women have been sexually assaulted or raped! Usually it’s by someone they know. But only 1 in 6 of rapes are reported & only 1 in 100 of these result in any charge 

GP is a safe space to disclose, deal with sensitively, document verbatim (may be used in courts & asked to write statement) and ask about specific details esp medical 
• Specific sexual act, nature of injuries, contraception, pregnancy test, anything known about assailant (eg BBV), any pain/bleeding 

If presenting within 1w of sexual assault encourage
• Reporting to police - can address any immediate safety risk & they can liase with SARC (but A&E if any immediate injuries & can also refer to SARC) - respect decision if they don’t want to report 
• SARC = Sexual Assault Referral Centre where can refer for forensic examination, DNA, immediate medical aftercare STI testing & support (otherwise would be Umbrella GUM/STI clinic who can provide testing & treating for emergency contraception, STI, HIV PEP & Hep B Ig if risks). Can be done without police involvement and DNA lasts up to 7d for vaginal sex 
• SG - if child or vulnerable adult 

If presenting >1w after still worth involving police, SARC and SG in same way but if historic and pt refusing referrals then can examine, screen for STI/preg test, psychological support (RAPE crisis charity, trauma focused CBT etc )
