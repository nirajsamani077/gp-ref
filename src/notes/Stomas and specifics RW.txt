Stomas and specifics RW

Bowel opening brought out to skin to bypass rest of GI tract (or provide it some rest) with 2 types 
• Ileostomy - R sided, large pout as SB fluid faeces contents irritable, panproctocolectomy or reversible for bowel rest after colectomy, anterior resection or ileoanal pouch, has drainage pouch emptied 4-5x/day 
• Colostomy - L sided smaller pout as less irritating formed stool, usually AP resection but can be reversible following Hartman’s, closed appliance changed 1-3x/day 

Practical points of managing stoma (stoma nurse)
• Bag empty regularly in day, but change bag 1-3x a week (usually colostomy more often), leaks can cause skin irritation & distress 
• Skin reactions common (use barrier /hypoallergenic products)
• High fibre or bulky foods can constipate & even obstruct bowel (not broken down in same way) so opt for low residue diet (low fibre high protein), sometimes gassy /flatulent stoma diet-related - liase with dietician
• Psychological impact significant - can still have sex, swim, sports - counselling is helpful 

Comps 
• Urgent surgical 
   - Dusky/dehisced stoma
   - Parastomal hernia with obstruction
   - High output stoma >2L/day (usually <1L/day max) which needs same day UE/calcium/Mg/phos and rehydration/loperamide (marshmallows or jelly babies can thicken stoma output!)
   - Stomal retraction/stenosis (can be IBD/CA)
• Routine stoma nurse 
   - Parastomal hernia without obstruction (bulging around stoma site on coughing, only if pink & not painful try apply gauze with 50% dextrose for 5mins whilst lying down, osmotic sugar effect reduces oedema & if so size less & can reduce with gentle pressure- most you’re going to refer anyway 
   - Peristomal skin irritation /overgranulation (pink friable tissue may need silver nitrate).
