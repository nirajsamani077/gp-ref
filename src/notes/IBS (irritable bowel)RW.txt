IBS (irritable bowel)RW

6mo of ABC - abdo pain/bloating/CIBH =consider  
Consider, R/O red flags then make a positive Dx 

Red flags pointing away from IBS
- >50, WL, PR bleeding, FHx bowel/ovarian CA

Check in all (to R/O red flag or other causes)
• Bloods - FBC, TFT, Coeliac, CA125 in women
• Stool - FIT (if criteria), faecal calprotectin (if <45 diarrhoea to R/O IBD), H Pylori stool if dyspepsia 

Other non-red flag DDx in IBS-D to consider 
• Bile acid malabsorption (prev cholecystectomy)
• Microscopic colitis 

Positive criteria (ROME criteria for functional GI disorders) = pain relieved by defecation or altered stool frequency/consistency + 2 of 
• Tenesmus like straining, urgency, incomp evac
• Bloating, worse by eating, better by pooing
• PR mucus, TATT, backache, bladder sx, alternate

Diet advice 
• Regular meals, don’t eat too fast
• Plenty of water, avoid excess caffeine/ETOH/fizzy (8 cups water & <3 cups coffee or tea)
• Limit high fibre foods/fruit/processed foods (insoluble fibre eg bran corn hard to digest, soluble fibre eg oats nuts seeds slightly easier)
• Linseeds 1tbsp with oats for trapped wind
• Low FODMAP diet
   - Limiting short-chain fermentable carbs (in small bowel) which reduces gut hypersensitivity, limited evidence but trials have shown some benefit 

Meds
• Pain (IBS-P)
   - Antispasmodics (mebeverine, buscopan)
• Constipation (IBS-C) - 
   - Laxatves (avoid lactulose, use bulk forming eg isphagula husk/fybogel 1st line, 2nd line = osmotic movicol or stimulant senna/bisacodyl
   - Refer to consider linaclotide 290mg OD if >12mo or resistant to >2 laxatives, works by softening stool & increasing transit time 
• Diarrhoea (IBS-D) 
   - Loperamide (Imodium) 2mg after each loose stool (usually 8mg max, 5d max) 

If above fails
• TCA (5-10mg amitryptilline) low dose, titrate up 
• SSRI 2nd line 
• CBT/hypnotherapy (IBS-specific) if >12mo (refractory IBS)

Other functional GI disorder points 
• Dysregulation of “gut-brain” connections which is very complex & not well understood 
• Ensure R/O organic pathology but common ones 
   - Functional heartburn/dyspepsia, globus 
   - IBS,functional constipation/diarrhoea/abdo pain
   - Opiate induced constipation or GI hyperalgesia 
   - Abdominal migraine in children 
• Diagnosis by specialist - biopsychosocial approach, looking after brain helps to look after gut (eg sleep stress emotions), may use SSRI/ amitryptilline & CBT (use framework between thoughts emotions and impacts on behaviours/physical symptoms) .
