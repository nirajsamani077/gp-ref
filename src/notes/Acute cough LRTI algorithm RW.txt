Acute cough/ LRTI algorithm RW 

Causes = Infective Vs Non infective 
• Infective 
   - URTI (cold, flu) viral most common
   - LRTI - Viral bronchitis/pneumonitis, pneumonia
   - Kids - VIW, bronchiolitis, croup, FBS 
   - Resp adults - IE COPD/asthma/bronchiectasis
• Non infective 
   - Common simple - PND, reflux, ACE/ARB
   - Serious - Lung Ca, ILD, HF, PE

If systemically unwell or high risk (comorbid, IC, premi, elderly, diabetic) - immediate/delayed ABx 

Otherwise can be 3 categories 
• Possible CAP - focal signs, use CRB65, ABx 5d+
• Acute LRTI cough - self care, usually no ABx
• Acute URTI cough - self care, no ABx 

Self care 
• Honey, salt water gargles 
• Weak evidence for OTC cough medicines (not codeine linctus but guaifenesin)
• No evidence for ABx - only shorten the cough by 1/2 a day on average and don’t improve anything else, cough usually lasts 3-4w anyway (in CAP recovery time up to 6mo, most cough SOB by 6w)

CRB65 - 1pt for each of Confusion (AMT <8), RR >30, BP S<90 D<60, >65
• 0 = Usually don’t admit
• 1 = Consider admitting 
• 2+ = Usually admits, CXR sputum plasma pneumococcal urine legionella & 7-10d ABx 

ABx choice 
• CAP = Amox 500mg TDS 5d 
• Acute cough = Doxy 200mg stat then 100mg 4d
• Alts = Clary 500mg BD or Erythro 500mg QDS 

In future could use POC CRP to help stratify 

Acute cough in children (usually <3w)
• Usually an URTI in well child with coryza 
• LRTI/VIW asthma/bronchiolitis if iWOB or chest signs - only ABx if pneumonia suspected (not for uncomplicated LRTI without above)
• Barking cough /stridor consider croup (viral) or bacterial trachietis/epiglottitis
• Sudden = consider FBS & refer A&E for bronch
• Atopy/seasonal = consider allergic rhinitis/asthma 
• FTT/clubbed = investigate further cause CF/CLD.
