Stroke /TIA tips RW

FAST - Face Arm Speech Time to call 999 (R/O hypoglycaemia - check BM)

85% of strokes ischaemic (rest haemorrhagic)
• Call 999 rapid response as needs immediate CT if within 4.5h of onset could thrombolyse/thrombectomy (clot retrieval) if R/O bleed, no aspirin if persistent i.e. stroke as could be bleed 
• Work up bloods (HbA1C/lipids), MRI brain, ECG (AF)/echo, carotid USS/MR (if neuro stable/not severely disabled with symptomatic stenosis >50% - candidate for carotid endarterectomy)
• Post stroke Rx - Aspirin 300mg 2w then 75mg clopidogrel or DOAC if AF cause, atorva 80mg (wait 2d as risk haemorrhagic transformation), BP and BM control tight, stroke unit PT/OT/SALT
• Screen malnutrition & depression post stroke 

TIA - Sx <24h (usually <1h) self-resolve, high risk for near-future stroke 
• For all give 300mg aspirin & specialist RV within 24h (likely admit) regardless of ABCD2 score (no longer used as can’t tell high vs low risk TIA)
• Focal weakness/sensory/speech, homonymous hemianopia/amaurosis, gait disturbance, vertigo 
• Specialist will decide if MRI needed & Rx eg clopidogrel, atorva 80mg etc 

Stroke types (Oxford classification)
• TACS/PACS = 3/3 or 2/3 of 
    - CL motor/sens defect (ACA arm/face, MCA leg)
    - CL homonymous hemianopia 
    - Higher cortical dysfunction (dysphasia, VS) 
• LACS = Lacunar stroke pure motor/sensory/SM 
• POCS = Cerebellar, vertigo/gait, CN palsy, dissection neck pain/movement, “normal” HINTS
