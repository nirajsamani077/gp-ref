Depression & BAD RW

If suspecting ask 2-question screening test 
• Do you feel down or low in mood? 
• Have you lost pleasure/interest in things?
Then PHQ9 and enquire biological sx (sleep, appetite, weight, TATT) & suicidal/SH thoughts + 
• Life at home, work, relationships, children, finances, stressful life events 
• ETOH/smoking/drugs 
• Anxiety symptoms & panic attacks (common)
• Periods of elevated mood ?BAD

PHQ <16 = less severe, >16 = more severe and consider whether antidepressants first line along with usual first like GP relationship, guided self help, identifying and managing stressors, CBT & counselling (therapy)

CBT framework for understanding & treating depression - connection between negative thoughts & emotions which impact our behaviours and physical symptoms and can cycle/spiral out of control - control what we can control ie the behaviours and the physical body (sleep, support network, walks exercise, healthy diet, avoiding alcohol, doing activities we enjoy) as this can connect back the other way & reverse the cycle!!!

DDx depression - stress reaction/adjustment disorder/PTSD, BAD (10%!), eating disorder, dementia or physical ill health (thyroid)

Treatment (low intensity PHQ <16) - 8-16 sessions with homework between, no quick fix, via IAPT (locally BHM >25, FTB <25)
• Guided self help - CBT concepts, coping skills
• Behavioural activation - better for social withdrawal to focus on increasing activity to combat mood, less avoidance, breaking negative cycle & using “microsteps” with simple activities 
• Group exercise - useful as activities as above 
• Mindfullness /meditation - specific programmes designed for depression, good for different POV & being in control. Mindfullness good at taking step back/breath and looking at bigger picture, self awareness & measured purposeful response rather than automatic reactions 
• Counselling - therapist providing emotional listening and working through issues - better for specific issues (eg work relationship social)
• Psychodynamic therapy - good for stress in relationships and working through conflicts 
• CBT (computerised, group based or individual) - framework above continual therapist 
• If failed then consider risks /benefits of SSRI along side this (high intensity reatment for PHQ>16) - see section 

Follow up all new cases after 2w then periodically (1w if young or high risk) and then 4-6w where action should be taken & then periodically after depending on plan - safety net crisis information 
• Safety plan - find things that calm you eg breathing, taking a bath & remember your reasons for living (loved ones etc). Move to a safe space & try to interrupt thoughts but if crisis…
• Crisis plan - if feeling unsafe speak to someone, loved one or friend, crisis team number and information for Samaritans contact number, if imminent risk then call 999. Link to Mind website & getselfhelp.co.uk 

If not improving with antidepressant with escalating doses or switching, or high risk DSH/suicide/psychosis or significant distress refer via SPA to CMHT for further management 
• May add 2nd antidepressant, switch to voritoxetine (if failed x2), TCA, MAOI or antipsychotic 

Relapse very common - continue treatment for at least 6-12months after remission to reduce risk including ongoing psychological therapy 

NB 
• Bipolar affective disorder (BAD) - cycling of low & elevated mood (affecting function, hyperarousal, pressured speech, insomnia, goal directed activities, risky behaviours, lack of insight & sometimes psychosis) - refer all urgently, specialist Rx with lithium & antipsychotics (mood stabilisers) - may have ESCA for lithium or antipsychotic (often quetiapine/risperidone/aripiprazole or combo fluoxetine + olanzapine) with monitoring
 - Lithium = Lithium levels (aim 0.6-1) 3 monthly 12h post dose for most with 6 monthly BMI, UE, TFT & LFT, calcium + check levels 1w after any dose change - safety net toxicity D&V tremor & avoid NSAIDS ACE TZD (nephrotoxic) 
 - Antipsychotics = Annual BMI/waist to height, FBC UE LFT PRL HBA1C/lipids & CVD risk assessment (QRISK), ECG at baseline (QTC) & specific for clozapine (serial FBCs risk of agranulocytosis) - SE weight gain, metabolic, hyperprolactinaemia, anticholinergic & EPES, only reduce or stop on advice of psychiatrist only 
 - Valproate sometimes used 

• Seasonal affective disorder (SAD) - common depression during winter only, hypersomnia overeating etc - treat same but lightboxes (purchasable ~£100) mimic sunlight and could help 

Signpost - MIND, Northumberland self help leaflets
