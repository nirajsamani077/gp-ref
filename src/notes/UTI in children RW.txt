UTI in children RW

Any <3mo child with ?UTI refer to paeds 

Otherwise may not have typical sx (dysuria, frequency, urgency, haematuria, abdo/loin pain, smelly cloudy urine, new bedwetting, fever, dehydration) - dip urine all & send for MC&S if <3y or RFs >3y & start ABx based on dip of clean catch 
• Upper UTI = fever >38 + bacteriuria +/- pain 
• Lower UTI = bacteriuria, no fever/loin pain 

Abx for children >3mo = 3d for lower, 7d for upper (if not referring to hospital = low threshold)
• Lower UTI 
   - TMP 4mg/kg (max 200mg) BD if low resistance
   - Nitro 750mcg/kg (50mg QDS) or 100mg MR BD
   - 2nd line = Cefalexin or Amox
• Upper UTI 
   - Cefalexin 12.5mg/kg BD (125 BD to 500 TDS) 
   - Coamox (only if culture) 0.25-0.5ml/kg TDS of 125/31 suspension or 5ml TDS of 125/62 if 6-11yo

Further Ix /imaging after UTI (to identify obstruction, vesicoureteric reflux VUR or renal scarring) - USS all <6mo
• If <6mo & responds well to ABx after 48h - USS within 6wks, DMSA (identifies renal scars) or MCUG (identifies VUR) depending on result 
• If <6mo with atypical (ill, obstruction, AKI, non-E Coli) or recurrent UTI (3 cystitis, 1 pyelo 1 lower or 2 pyelo) then USS during admission + DMSA +/- MCUG in 4-6mo
• If >6mo only USS during infection & DMSA 4-6mo after infection if atypical or recurrent .
