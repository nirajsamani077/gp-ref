Raynaud’s phenomenon RW

Primary (usually young women BL symptoms) or secondary to CT disease
• Rheum - systemic sclerosis/scleroderma,SLE, RA
• Other - leukaemia, cryoglobulinaemia, COCP, cervical rib /thoracic outlet obstruction 

Red —> white —> blue colour in cold 
Some can do capillaroscopy with dermatoscope of nailfolds which can differentiate CT disease 

Refer rheum if suspect underlying CT disease along with rheum bloods (ANA, ENAs, DSDNA, CCP, C3/4)

Avoid cold & keep whole body warm, gloves 
Stop trigger - smoking, caffeine, drugs, Bb/triptan 

Nifedipine 10-30mg TDS only licensed drug 
Specialist may give IV prostacyclin or other options  including surgical (digital sympathectomy if severe ulcers etc)
