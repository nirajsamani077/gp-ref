T1DM diagnosis & DKA in childhood RW

If considering check BM, ketones (urine if no blood ketones) & if elevated admit to paeds same day (if unwell via ambulance) - can have normal BM (>11 = high), but raised blood ketones (>1 usually) 

Insulin deficiency /osmotic symptoms = polydipsia, polyuria, weight loss, TATT, recurrent infections
• Have high index of suspicion in any child with generalised sx (vomiting, abdo pains, lethargy)
• Specialist will make specific Dx - usually will be T1DM (will check C-peptide, anti-GAD) or rarely MODY (strong FHx T2DM acanthosis obese BAME)/monogenic which can develop <1yo 

Upon review check 
• HbA1C every 6mo, target <48 
• BM monitoring (premeal, when unwell) 
   - Fasting morning BM 5-7
   - Premeal BM 4-7
   - Ketone testing unwell or high 
• Diet & carb counting to dose insulin according to carbohydrate-to-insulin ratio 
• Teaching of injection & rotating sites 
• Counsel about hypo risks
   - Discuss symptoms & checking BM finger prick 
   - Fast acting glucose (gel) + long acting (toast)
   - IM glucagon & train family - if not working 999 
   - Hypo awareness -if impaired high risk of severe
   - If frequent refer BG awareness training prog 
• Sick day rules 
   - Dose increase 10-20% in event of illness 
   - Monitor BM ketones closely, risk of DKA 
   - If vomiting/no intake = no bolus, cont basal 
 • Annual flu, pneumococcal, regular dental check 
 • Rest as per T1DM treatment section (eyes kidneys BP statin etc)

Signpost JDRF charity - support for newly diagnosed T1DM 

NB - HHS is adult T2DM life-threatening comp with severe hyperglycaemia (>30), high osmolality & dehydration with drowsiness = admit urgently for IVI +++ +/- insulin infusion (20% mortality)

Causes of “euglycaemic DKA” - SGLT2, ETOH, liver disease, fasting & pregnancy  .
