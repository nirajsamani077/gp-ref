Injectable diabetes drugs (GLP)

Glucagon-like peptide (GLP-1) normally released from small intestine in response to oral glucose load to stimulate release of insulin to lower blood sugar which is known as the incretin effect (not the case for IV glucose), in T2DM GLP-1 reduced so incretin mimetic will increase GLP-1 so lower glucose 

All the -tides eg semglutide = wegovy/ozempic, liraglutide = saxenda (OD), exenatide = byetta (BD)

All SC injections often one weekly 
Causes weight loss ++, consider if BMI >35 or less if insulin unacceptable, good addition as triple therapy, aim for HbA1C drop >11 and 3% weight loss after 6 months 

Main risk = pancreatitis, GI side effects too 
Avoid if severe renal, liver, cardiac or GI impairment 
Usually eGFR <30 

Saxenda,  now approved by NICE for obesity treatment if BMI >30 with weight related comorbidity or >35 with non diabetic hyperglycaemia or high CVS risk via tier 3 weight management service
