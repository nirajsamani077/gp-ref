Macular lesions 

Flat pigmented skin lesion (<1cm, >1cm = patch) 

Red - drug eruption, viral exanthem, chloasma 
Dark red/purple - purpura 
Brown - junctional naevus (flat mole), freckles (solar lentigines), cafe au lait (>6 = NF)
Light /depigmented- pityriasis versicolor/alba, vitiligo 
Other - post inflammatory pigmentation, malignant (peutz jehgers on lips/MM with SB polyps, hutchinson giant variegated freckle), Berloque dermatitis (due to perfume on side of neck)

5 differentiators
• Itch/irritation? - drug eruption 
• Variable pig - pityriasis versicolor 
• Red - drug or viral 
• Small (<0.5,cm) - usually chloasma bigger 
• Muzzle area - cholasma

Drug eruption can be from 2 weeks after first dose 
Scrapings or under Woods light for pityriasis versicolor if doubt
