PDE5 inhibitors

PDE5 inhibitors cause vasodilation to allow blood to engorge corpus cavernousum (also in PHTN)
- Sildenafil (Viagra) 50mg short acting, take 1h before, can be obtained OTC, only generic sildenafil is prescribable without restriction on NHS
- Tadalafil (Cialis) 5mg longer acting & can be regular OD if anticipating regular use, only prescribable under certain circumstances on NHS (like viagra, levitra, spedra)

Contraindications 
- hypotension
- nitrates (do not use within 24h), nicorandil
- 6 months post MI/stroke (caution if Ix for IHD) 

Side effects 
- blue pill causes blue vision, risk of AION
- flushing, nasal congestion, headache
- don’t initiate erection but maintain (so need to be sexually aroused

Tips for starting 
- 6-8 doses, review in 6-8 weeks for response 
- Increase to max dose (sildenafil can go up to 100mg or down to 25mg) or try other
- Vacuum erection device as alternative in old men or unable to use sildenafil etc 
- Refer to urology for further (alprostadil for spinal issues, penile prosthesis) 

Other general advice 
- lose weight, stop smoking, cut down ETOH
- reduce cycling if >3h per week
