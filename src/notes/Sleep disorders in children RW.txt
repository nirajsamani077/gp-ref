Sleep disorders in children RW

Common childhood issue, 25% but up to 80% in neurodeveloomental disorders 
• Usually 10-17h <6mo, 10-12h <6y then 8-10h
• Most children should take 30min after lights out 

Causes of poor sleep 
• Behavioural - Most common 
• Psycholocal - Anxiety at bedtime with preoccupation about not going to sleep
• Physical - Related to chronic health condition 

Main 3 symptoms 
• Falling asleep, whilst asleep, & daytime 
• Parasomnias (eg night terrors, sleep walking) with concerning nightmares/traumatic experience 
• Restless legs - check ferritin, Rx if <50
• Don’t miss OSA, narcolepsy or OSA apneas

Management 
• Usual sleep hygiene - regular schedule, dark etc 
• Star charts and praise/reward good behaviours 
• Paeds may consider melatonin but off licence - can be helpful especially in ADHD/ASD (Circadin 2-3mg before bed) 
• Avoid co-sleeping, risk of SIDS
