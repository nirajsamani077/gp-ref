FTT / faltering growth RW

Babies: 
In first 3w of life WL <10% of BW common, usually stops by 3d and starts to regain by 3w
Any with >10% WL 
• Examine ?unwell 
• Observe feeding and feeding support, top ups /EBM after breastfeed 
• Refer if persisting 

FTT after 3w
• Usually multifactorial, feeding big part, 10% physical illness, 5% neglect so consider 
• Check weight/height and plot on UK-WHO growth chart, if <9th centile drop >1 centile, if 9-90th centile drop >2 centile, if >90th drop >3 centile, any <2nd centile or 2 centile spaces under MPH
• >2y calculate BMI (<2nd = small, <0.4th = refer)
• Any Weight loss worrying - refer 

Assessment
• Full Hx birth development social, food diary
• Urine dip, Coeliac screen 
• If concern about any specific UC refer 
• Monitor weight /height weekly <6mo, 2-4wkly >6

If no specific concerns watch wait 4-8w with feeding written advice and support from HV (Caroline Walker trust, Reg eating patterns, can be messy, avoid blaming child), if not improving consider dietician or referral 

If referring they will usually do further tests 
• FBC, ferritin, U&E, TFT, Coeliac, vit D 
• Sweat test, genetic/chromosomal tests 
• Urine MSU, CXR
