Capacity assessment RW

Situation/decision and time specific 
• 2 stage test = understand & retain information then weigh up info & communicate 

If has capacity can refuse treatment for this decision even if it will result in death (unless compromising others or public safety)

If lacks capacity 
• Any LPOA or advanced directive to refuse treatment (ADRT) - check gov website for forms, LPOA done with Office of the Public Guardian, ADRT doesn’t need solicitor, if neither a close NOK can apply to be court appointed deputy via Court of Protection 
• If not then make decision in best interests involving patient, NOK in process to establish previous wishes & beliefs (can also refer to advanced statements as part of this as not legally binding)
• IMCA if no NOK or relatives 
• DOLs Deprivation of Liberty Safeguards = if health or social worker feels someone needs to be deprived of liberty to care for them (in care home or hospital)

Children & capacity 
• Child <18 should be involved in treatment decisions but assess Gilick competence (no lower age limit for this even <16)
• Any child <18 is presumed competent to give consent (if Gilick competent) but CAN’T REFUSE treatment or withdraw consent in UK - in this situation seek advice & involve indemnity (eg 16yo girl refusing referral to hospital for ?ectopic) - if 
• If refusal as above or not Gilick competent then those with parental responsibility will need to make decision (automatic right if birth mother, father if married to mother at time or birth or named on birth certificate, if in care may be shared with local authority, if child safety can be restricted by court of protection) .
