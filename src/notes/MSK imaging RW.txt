MSK imaging RW

Most simple things don’t need imaging as wouldn’t change management - should try treatment RICE, physio, painkillers, brace ,injection if necessary 

If red flags image & refer urgently 

Explain often clinical diagnosis, doesn’t change how we manage, often doesn’t match physical sx (esp XR), most of time gets better 

Situations to consider imaging 
• Suspected osteoporotic vertebral insufficiency #
• Late presentation of trauma (usually A&E still)
• Knee pain post trauma (usually MRI via OTS after RICE period), positive knee or ankle Ottowa
• History of recurrent shoulder instability 
• Ganglion or ST mass for USS
• Stress # foot although XR usually poor sensitivity so if normal and still concerned refer for MRI
• Uncertain inguinal hernia usually surgical A&G

If CES sx >2w or bilateral sciatica without CES symptoms consider instead of A&E to urgent OTS to be seen within 2w with safety netting
