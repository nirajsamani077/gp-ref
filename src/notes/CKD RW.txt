CKD RW

For diagnosis 
• eGFR <60 and/or UACR >3 x2 results 3mo apart
• Structural kidney problems regardless of above 

Kidneys less efficient at getting rid of toxins in urine, causes issues with blood vessels fluid build up and when severe needs things like dialysis/Tx

Is it AKI
• If new eGFR <60 repeat again in 2w or sooner depending on clinical picture 

UACR 
• Ideally early morning urine
• If >3 raised - repeat early morning if between 3-70 to ensure accurate, if >70 don’t repeat 
• A1 is <3, A2 >3, A3 >30 on grading 

When CKD is formally diagnosed 
• BP, usual target clinic <140/90 unless ACR >70
• Urine dip r/o haematuria (doesn’t need micro)
• Other baseline bloods inc lipids HbA1C
• Renal USS only if eGFR <30, accelerated drop 25% (usually 15) per year or change category, any haematuria any obstruction or FHx PCKD - usually alongside renal referral 
• Calculate 5 year risk of RRT score (Kidney failure Risk equation /QRISK for kidneys , if >5% = refer) - Excel spreadsheet 

Management = all 5 A’s 
• Advice to pt- weight loss, smoking, annual RV at least (if eGFR <30 or ACR >30 6 monthly or more), annual influenza and pneumococcal, avoid nephrotoxics 

• ACE/ARB - if persistent UACR > 70 without diabetes or >3 with diabetes offer ACE ARB and titrate to max tolerated dose, and offer dapaglifozin (only one licensed in CKD as long as eGFR >15) esp if UACR >30 (but can offer >3) all for Renoprotection. Tight BP control with CKD due to CVD risk but only lower target (<130/80 clinic) if UACR >70 (only instance where this is target). Newer drug finerone (Kerendia) been approved after dapa 

• Atorva - offer to all 20mg, regardless of lipids, can’t use QRISK in CKD, if eGFR <30 and suboptimal drop <40% non-HDL discuss with renal rather than increasing dose 

• Anaemia - CKD as cause of normocytic or microcytic ACD, rarely the cause if eGFR >30 but often the cause if <30. If 30-60 usually investigate haematinics etc 

• Advice from renal - refer if 
  - 5 year KFRE score >5% (need phosphate and bicarb on next blds) 
  - ACR >70
  - ACR >30 + haematuria 
  - Progressive /accelerated drop (3 readings)
  - Rare cause suspected (RAS, PCKD)
  - Poorly controlled HTN 4 agents 
  - Suspected obstruction with hydronephrosis (urology)

Starting/upping ACE/ARB - don’t start if K>5 or AKI
• U&E after 2 weeks - if >25% drop eGFR or K >6 stop and check for other factors eg NSAID, dehydration but could be RAS

Other drugs
• Avoid NSAIDs , unhappy triad 
• DOACs need monitoring & calculate Cockcroft gault creatinine clearance - always check BNF as eGFR can over-estimate renal function (esp if old or extremes of muscle mass) and DOAC are renally excreted (like vanc, dig) so bleeding risk if done wrong 
• Nitro, tetracyclines, opiates all significant caution 
• Bisphosphonates only if eGFR >30

NB nephrotic syndrome - rare in adults 3/100k
• Usually GN, many secondary causes (DM, SLE, amyloid, myeloma, drugs, infections)
• Urine PCR >300 (rather than ACR), also severe hyperlipidaemia (TC >10)
• Oedema +++, HTN, refer all renal urgent
