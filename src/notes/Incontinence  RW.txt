Incontinence  RW

Common but unrecognised/taboo, 10% of women, probably higher in elderly, most mixed 

Top causes
• Stress (with coughing/bending) with our without prolapse (post pregnancy/pelvic surgery)
• Urge (when I have to go I have to go) = OAB usually idiopathic but can be secondary to dementia/ CVA/MS/ spinal path (acute think CES)
• Overflow (more continuous) due to chronic outflow obstruction E.g. BPH, TURP (temporary) or vesicovaginal fistula (rare)
• UTI/stone 
• Causes of polyuria/immobility in elderly 

History 
• Distinguish stress/urge/overflow patterns, along with UTI symptoms, dysuria, haematuria, nocturia and male LUTS 
• Background for cause (pregnancy, surgery, dementia, neuro conditions, diuretics, prostate surgery, chronic cough/IAP) 

Examination /investigations 
• Palpable bladder would indicate obstructive
• Urine dip /MSU 
• PV spec check prolapse check pelvic floor squeeze for tone during bimanual 
• Consider bloods (U&E, PSA) and USS KUB if obstruction suspected 
• Keep bladder diary 3d 
• Refer for urodynamics/uroflowmetry

Management (urge)- more medical than surgical
• Lifestyle- reduce caffeine ETOH spread water in day and pelvic floor exercises 
• Pads - Bladder Bowel UK website and number 
• Bladder retraining via continence nurses (timed voiding, ignoring urges, holding on) - https://www.swbh.nhs.uk/wp-content/uploads/2012/06/Bladder-retraining-ML3615.pdf
• Antimuscarinics /mirabegron (beta 3 agonist which relaxes bladder)- solifenacin cheapest, avoid oxybutinin in elderly women (significant anticholinergic falls risk)
• Ensure topical oestrogen if PM atrophy 
• Desmopressin at bed with no water before and during effect time 8h if ++ nocturia (not >65 with cardiac hx), risk hyponatraemia check UE first 
• Surgical & urodynamics - botulinum bladder wall injection, further cystoplasty sacral nerve stuff 

Stress incontinence - more surgical than medical 
• Supervised pelvic floor exercises 3 months 3 times a day (8 contractions, Kegel) - use electrical biofeedback if can’t do this . 
• Duloxetine if declines surgery 
• Surgical - colposuspension, sling, bulking, transvaginal tape all help strengthen pelvic floor (mesh’s controversial - can give women info)

Re pads containment - adjunct 
Re catheter = last resort if significant distress with skin effects of urine bedbound retention infections 

Pelvic floor 
• Dome shaped group of muscles from pubic bone at front, bottom of spine at back, and side of pelvic bone (ischial tuberosities)
• Supports pelvis, lower abdomen, keeps things in (continence prolapse) and sexual function 
• Pregnancy, surgeries, obesity/DM, age are RFs 
• 3 months supervised PFMT 3 times a day 8 exercises - use Kegel or Squeezy app but basically try to lift and tighten muscles (squeeze) of vagina and backpassage (contraction) 1-2s then break then repeat, increase length
