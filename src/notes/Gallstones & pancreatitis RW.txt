Gallstones & pancreatitis RW

Gallstones common up to 25%, most don’t cause problems & don’t need treating (asymptomatic gallstones on USS)

RFs 
• Fs - Female, Fat, Forties, Fertile (preg/oestrogen)
• Crohns, Cirhosis, Haemolysis 

Spectrum 
• Asymptomatic gallstones (incidental)
   - Gallbladder stones with normal CBD = observe 
   - CBD stones high risk of comps = refer 
• Biliary colic - RUQ pain after fatty food
• Cholecystitis - Above + fever/systemic upset 
• Pancreatitis - Epigastric pain to back, vomiting, systemic upset, comps (infected necrosis, pseudocyst, ascites, pleural effusion) = admit 
• CBD stone - Obstructive jaundice 
• Cholangitis - Ascending CBD infection usually with stone, fever/jaundice/RUQ pain triad 

If symptomatic gallstones 
• If simple biliary colic & systemically well can Ix via primary care & refer (but any other sx = SAU) 
• LFT & USS - if abnormal LFT or dilated CBD need MRCP/ERCP 
• Should be offered lap chole (within <1w of acute episode, although if “hot” GB may wait to cool)

Pancreatitis 
• Usually ETOH or gallstones (rare drugs eg TZD/steroids/valproate/GLP1 gliptins/AZA, trauma, post ERCP, hyperTgs, hypercalcaemia, AI (IgG4)/Ca)
• All acute need admission - IVI, bowel rest, ITU
• Chronic pancreatitis 
   - Recurrent acute attacks, chronic abdo pain
   - Malabsorption/exocrine dysfunction - unable to absorb fat soluble vitamins ADEK, steatorrhoea (as fat not absorbed, similar to obstructive jaundice, check faecal elastase), replaced with enzymes (Creon/Pancrex) with meals, 2 yearly DEXA 
   - Endocrine dysfunction - Secondary Type 3c DM (usually needs insulin), 6 monthly HbA1C 
   - Risk of pancreatic CA (new abdo pain WL DM >60 = 2WW direct access CT abdo)
