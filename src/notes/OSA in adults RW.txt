OSA in adults RW

Snoring & excess daytime sleepiness (different from TATT) with intermittent upper airway obstruction causing mini apneas (pause in breathing >10s) where transient o2 drop, autonomic firing and disturbed sleep 
• OSAHS - OSA hypopnea syndrome 
• COPD-OSAHS overlap - worsens hypoxia and hypercapnia if coexists 
• Obesity hypoventilation syndrome - OSA risk and poor inspiration causing hypercapnia 

Ask about 
• Night - snoring, choking/pauses in breathing 
• Morning - unrefreshed, headaches 
• Day - excessive sleepiness, naps, irritability/fog 

Epworth sleepiness scale for all to assess daytime sleepiness (not used for assessment of severity or for referral) + STOP-Bang questionnaire 
BMI, ETOH, sats, job 

Refer all for sleep studies (diagnostic) & urgent if 
• COPD or obesity, esp if sats <94%, risk of CO2
• Driver or task focused safety job 
• CVD

Lifestyle - WL, ETOH, smoking, sleep hygeine, explain collapse of upper airway avoid late night etoh reduces tone, neck circumference etc, try sleeping on side (can use bed position modifiers like tennis ball technique attacked to back)
Driving - if ESS >10 inform DVLA and stop driving - https://sleep-apnoea-trust.org/driving-and-sleep-apnoea/detailed-guidance-to-uk-drivers-with-sleep-apnoea/

Secondary care treatment 
• CPAP at night to stent open upper airway - can cause dryness 
• If declined - Mandibular advancement device, surgery/tonsillectomy 
• If COPD or obesity may need NIV or LTOT

Other causes of excess daytime sleepiness (EDS)
• Stress, baby, meds (SSRI, Bb), nocturia etc 
• True insomnia - hard to get or stay asleep 
• Rare = periodic movements (worse with caffeine or neuroleptics, like restless legs can use dopamine against pramipexole or gabapentinoid if really needed), narcolepsy (1 in 2000) .
