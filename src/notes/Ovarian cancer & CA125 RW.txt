Ovarian cancer & CA125 RW 

IBS is rare >50 = in women consider CA-125 for these types of Sx (as well as ruling out colorectal CA with symptomatic FIT & pancreatic CA with direct access CT abdo if also weight loss)

Another late diagnosed due to non-specific Sx often put down to benign conditions (eg IBS, UTI, GORD, menopause!)

2WW criteria 
• Any obvious abdo/pelvic mass or ascites = 2WW
• >50 with persistent or frequent vague symptoms (bloating, early satiety, abdo/pelvic pain, LUTS) or more obvious red flags (weight loss, CIBH) or just new TATT = CA125 & if >35 refer for urgent /direct access USS abdomen & TV USS pelvis 
 - If suspicious scan result = 2WW 
 - If negative = keep looking (FIT, CT abdo, urine) as raised CA125 is risk for non-ovarian CA 

Some say sequential CA125 then USS but small risk of delay, UK NICE still recommends sequential - if strong clinical suspicion do both in parallel as CA125 can have false negatives 

RF - Age & post-menopausal, oestrogen stuff (obesity, nulliparity, HRT), FHx breast/ovarian, BRCA & Lynch 

Class = low grade or high grade 
• Low grade (30%) - slow growing but respond worse to chemo 
• High grade (70%) - aggressive & mets early, relation to distal fallopian tube /peritoneal, BRCA associated, more sensitive to chemo/hormonal 

Treatment 
• Curative or palliative (debulking) surgery & neoadjuvant chemo (platinum based) if very large or usually for relapse 

Population studies have shown screening not beneficial 

Target ovarian cancer/CRUK/MacMillan for support
