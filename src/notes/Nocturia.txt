Nocturia 

Occasional is normal, if causing distress assess 

Top causes (excluding UTI)
• Age related (reduced bladder capacity) or excess fluid/ETOH close to bed (increased night volume) 
• Any cause of swollen ankles (fluid in legs redistributes at night) 
• LUTS due to BPH (men), OAB (women/men)
• Any cause of frequency/polyuria 
• OSA (causes waking)

History 
• Symptoms in daytime? 
• LUTS, storage symptoms (urge, incontinence)
• Leg swelling 
• Fluid /ETOH intake towards night 

Examine/investigate 
• Abdo/PR, pitting oedema, urine dip/MSU, bloods (U&E, HbA1C), urine frequency volume chart
