Visual loss / blurred vision algorithm RW

Hx - what mean by (complete loss, loss of sharpness, loss of colour), sudden or gradual, UL/BL, trauma, central or peripheral vision, improves with blinking (? dry eye) 
• Painful/red eye (AACG, AU, corneal, scleritis, OC)
• Sudden painless (TIA amarosis fugax, CRAO, CRVO, retinal detachment (RD), vitreous haemorrhage (VH), wet AMD) 
• Swelling/discharge (conjunctivitis, periorbital cell)
• Temporal headache/jaw claud/scalp tender (GCA)
• Floaters/flashes/curtain down - TIA or RD 
• Other eye sx (dc gritty watery photophobia) 

BG - Contact lens, eye issues /surgery, trauma/work (eg mechanic), FHx glaucoma or retinitis pigmentosa, old age (?GCA) young age (?MS)

Ex 
• External - swollen lids (periorbital), proptosis (orbital), conjunctiva (uniform redness = conjunctivitis, sectoral (episcleritis/scleritis if central), circumcorneal (AACG or AV)
• VA - Snellen, use pinhole if no glasses to correct 
• Pupils - abnormal size/shape suggests anterior uveitis (AV) if painful red dilated, 3rd nerve palsy (dilated, down and out eye, ptosis - fixed pupil = compressive, accommodating pupil = ischaemic) or Horners (constricted miosis, ptosis, anhydrosis)
• RAPD - screen for optic nerve pathology 
• EOM - painful movements with loss of colour, RAPD suggests optic neuritis, ?3rd nerve/LR 6th nerve palsy
 - Monocular Diplopia - double vision persists with one eye closed suggests UL eye issue (cataract) 
 - Binocular Diplopia - double vision improves with one eye closed suggests CN palsy (malignment) or IC lesion so take more seriously 
• Fundi - red reflex, papilledema/ haemorrhages 
• Fluorescin staining if available - yellow with blue light ?corneal abrasion /FB/ ulcer 

DDx and Rx 
• Sudden onset (all same day eye cas/stroke Ref) 
  - Painless = TIA (AF), CRAO/VO, RD, VH, wet AMD
  - Painful = AACG, AV, cornea/trauma, Orb cellulitis 
  - Maybe = GCA, Optic neuritis
  - Bilateral painless = ?Stroke or ICP (papilledema) 
• Gradual onset (usually opticians + routine Ref)
  - Refractive error (corrects with pinhole)
  - POAG, diabetic retinopathy, dry AMD, cataracts 
  - Systemic (AI rheum or drugs amiodarone HCQ)
