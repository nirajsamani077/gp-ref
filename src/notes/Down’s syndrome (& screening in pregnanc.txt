Down’s syndrome (& screening in pregnancy) RW 

Screening T21 in pregnancy -risk higher with maternal age (>20 = 1/1500, >40 = 1/100). 
1/1000 at 30 then divide by 3 for every 5yrs. 

Use combined test (bloods markers HCG + PAPPA between 10-14w) & NT on dating scan 11-14w along with age to calculate risk - if >1/150 refer for amnio/CVS (newer non-invasive tech will be testing fetal DNA in maternal blood for trisomies - will be started soon)

Annual Downs review points 

• Medical stuff 
   - Metabolic - annual BMI, 2 yearly TFT given higher hypothyroidism risk, consider HbA1C if RFs
   - Dementia - much higher risk but usually still >40 & may present vaguely (sleep change, apathy, incontinence) , 6-CIT may not be useful 
   - MH - higher risk of depression, ASD & should be managed by specialist & present differently 
   - CVD - QRISK every 5y after age of 40, higher risk of valve (may have had surgery before & mitral or aortic regurg most common), HTN, IHD etc 
   - Resp /ENT - OSA & OM common, do Otoscopy 
   - GI - GORD & Coeliac common, SALT issues 
   - Cancer - higher risk esp leukaemias although baseline mild macrocytosis/polycythaemia, lower risk of solid tumours, enquire about screening 
   - Bone - osteoporosis & atlantoaxial instability causing cervical myelopathy 
   - Neuro - risk of seizures 
   
• Routine stuff 
   - Vision & hearing - 2 yearly checks 
   - Dental - 6 monthly check 
   - Imms - usual schedule, if CHD annual flu & one off pneumococcal 
   - Driving - enquire 
   - Contraception - enquire, adherence, higher risk of infertility esp men who most often are (lack VD)

• Consider developmental regression, coercion etc .
