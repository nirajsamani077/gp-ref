Atrial fibrillation (AF) algorithm RW

Irregular pulse or cardiac Sx (SOB, CP, dizziness, palpitations, stroke/TIA) - opportunistic checking 
• RFs - ETOH, thyroid, obesity HTN, IHD, valve 
• Most of time don’t know why, often progresses paroxysmal (PAF) —> persistent —> permanent AF

Top chambers of heart which fill the heart beat irregularly due to abnormal electrical signals, lots of reasons heart disease age infections etc, causes heart to beat irregularly and fast uncoordinated with sx, major risk is blood not flowing properly when heart beating irregularly which causes clot which goes up to brain = stroke 

ECG to confirm Dx (if suspect but doesn’t show get tape or Reveal device/loop recorder via A&G) = no P waves, irreg irreg QRS, fib waves 
• If unwell/HD unstable/very fast = admit 
• If not check bloods (FBC, UE, LFT, TFT for baseline/electrolytes and prior to starting DOAC) &Ex (overload - ?HF, murmur - valvular, ?BP -CVD) 
• Refer for echo if high risk above (HR, valve, HTN) or planning cardioversion (for LA size which predicts success/ LAA clot which can shoot off to brain if done) 

Management = AC-DC 

• AC Anti-Coagulate to prevent stroke (5x inc risk)
- CHADSVASC for stroke risk - CHF, HTN, Age >65 =1, >74 =2, DM, Stroke/TIA =2, Vasc disease, Age, Sex F.  If 0 or 1 as F don’t need anticoag, if 1 as M consider anticoag, if >=2 offer anticoag 
- ORBIT-AF for bleeding risk - Elderly >75, CKD, antiplatelets, Hb/HCT low (2), Hx of bleeding (2). 0-2 = low risk 2%/y bleed, >=4 = high risk 8% 
- Applies to any AF (including PAF) apart from valvular or just been cardioverted (LMWH/warfarin) 
- Don’t just use scores, patient led decision, falls risk but rarely causes major bleeds and falls alone or age alone shouldn’t be reason to withhold 
- DOACs are first line & should start promptly as every day without is a risk of stroke 
  • In most benefits of taking outweigh risks 
  • Needs CrCl (Cockcroft Gault) checking prior 
  • No large head to head trial for DOACs 
  • Apixaban BD often first line as oldest, has most safety data vs OD dosing (rivaroxiban/edoxaban) where a missed dose may increase risk of stroke 
  • Recent drive to use Edoxaban due to pharmaceutical factors - manufacturers can provide additional incentives for using/monitoring eg tools, smartphones, therapy reviews - od dosing no reversal agent, not necessarily better 
  • If on warfarin and eligible (not low CrCl, APS, some valvular) - offer switch to DOAC
  • Needs to be lifelong as risk is there even if resolved or PAF 

• DC Decide Control (rate or rhythm) - not if PAF 
- Most of time = rate control but rhythm criteria are 
   • Witnessed new onset (ie not incidental) <48h
   • Obvious reversible cause (pneumonia)
   • AF causing new HF 
   • Young with structurally normal heart (likely to have more normal atria without clot that can be reversed easier) 
   
- RATE CONTROL (aim HR <100 no symptoms)
   • Bisoprolol or rate limiting CCB (diltiazem), never both, avoid CCB if HF
   • Add digoxin as dual therapy usually, better for mainly housebound patients who don’t exercise or co-existing HF as only reduces HR at rest (only dig monotherapy if can’t use Bb or CCB)
   • Refer for rhythm control/ablation if failed 
  
- RHYTM CONTROL (aim for SR)
   • Refer most for DC electrical cardiovesion (or chemical with amiodarone, esp if within 48h witnessed new onset, usually in hospital) 
   • Many need long term chemical with amiodarone or dronedarone esp if also LVSD 
   • If not suitable or failed DC - consider LAA appendage ablation with 3mo amiodarone after or PPM if coexistent LVSD 

For Paroxysmal AF (PAF) - usually refer 
• Common, easily missed
• Refer for 24-48-72h tape or ambulatory ECG /Holter monitor / ILR if ?PAF with initial normal ECG
• AC as above , esp if frequent episodes = high risk 
• DC is specialist decision as won’t need immediate rate control, so refer to consider if 
 -  “Pill in the pocket” with Bb/flecainide to abort attacks esp if known ppt (caffeine ETOH), structurally normal heart, resting BP/HR normal 
 - Long term rhythm control usually chemical with Bb, sotalol, amiodarone, dronedarone or LAAA
