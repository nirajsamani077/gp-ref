Frailty RW

Frailty is common, colinks with dementia, falls, multimorbidity, sarcopenia & is defined as a minor stressor causing a MAJOR impact - is age related but not an inevitable part of aging (preventable)
• Should be proactively identifying & using screening tool called eFI (Electronic frailty index) which uses 36 parameters on patient record to categorise no/mild/mod/severe frailty (not perfect)
• Rockwood Frailty Index has 9 categories ranging from no frailty to severe terminally ill
• Use NHS RightCare approach - 3 Ps
   - Prevent = if no frailty, health promotion 
   - Protect = if mild frailty, refer comprehensive geriatric assessment (CGA) & MDT Ax to reduce risk of admission & progression 
   - Prioritise = if mod severe, often CH annual personalised care plan, review, SMR & advanced care planning, deprescribing & EOL planning  

Frailty chronic “phenotype”
• Unintentional weight loss (never normal so Ix!)
• Exhausated/fatigue 
• Sedentary 
• Slow walking or weak grip 

Acute “geriatric giants” which uncover frailty 
• Falls/off legs., delirium, polypharm, incontinence 

Specific interventions for frailty & sarcopenia 
• Muscle strength training - 30mins 4x/w, group 
• Protein supplement - natural or 2x 200ml fortisip each containing 25g protein and 400cal .
