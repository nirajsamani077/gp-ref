Smoking cessation RW

1/6 adults smoke regularly - rates falling but biggest intervention to prevent cancer/CVD & single most effective thing to improve your health 

When thinking about stopping 
• Brief intervention using FRAMES (Feedback that smoking bad, Responsibility yours, Advise specifically stopping & alternative eg NRT/drug/vaping, Menu specific ways of adapting behaviour, Empathy I know it’s hard but Self-optimism you’ll be doing a great thing and it can be done!)
• Group support via NHS stop smoking services - find a service/refer smoking cessation nurse & use SmokeFree app 
• E Cigarettes as a step down from smoking tobacco (don’t contain harmful tobacco but do contain addictive nicotine) a better option, but shouldn’t encourage starting them otherwise, unsure of long term data!
• NRT is the best way of replacing the addictive effect of nicotine, without the tobacco, in a way to step down over time - varenicline/bupropion also have good evidence 

Consider many factors to starting /continuing smoking eg household, cultural, job, low SES, drinking, stress, MH so link these to understand pt 

NRT
• Start 1-2w prior to stopping & give amount to last until 1-2w after stopping (so max 1mo in all, should come back if needing more)
• Can use patch + combine one other (spray, gum) for shorter term 
• Typical regimen = Nicotine patch 20mg every day & 2-4mg nicotine gum or lozenges PRN (max 10/d) - check doses on EMC rather than BNF

Varenicline (champix) & bupropion if adult 
• Varenicline = partial nicotine agonist, 12w course, caution in depression/DSH- not available in UK!
• Bupropion - norad/dopamine reuptake inhibitor + nicotine antagonist, risk of seizures so epilepsy CI
• In pregnancy - 1st line CBT, NRT if failed, screen CO level at booking (>7ppm)
