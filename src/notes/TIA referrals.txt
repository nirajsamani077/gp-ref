TIA referrals 

ABCD2 no longer recommended by NICE

Give aspirin 300mg unless bleeding disorder, anticoagulant or already on aspirin 

If >1 TIA (crescendo) - urgent admission 

If TIA symptoms <1 week ago - hospital review within 24hrs

If >1 week - hospiral review within 1 week 

Investigations in secondary care 
- no CTH routinely unless Dx doubt 
- MRI head usually recommended 
- ECG
- Carotid Doppler unless not candidate for CEA (only consider if carotid territory, not severely disabled, stenosis >70%) 

Clopidogrel is 1st line (aspirin + dipyridamole if cannot tolerate) 

Tips 
refer to rapid access clinic. Give a week of aspirin 300mg + stop driving but they are contacted within 24hr for initial clinic appt but Lasting neurology - A&E On a DOAC - A&E (needs CT head first prior to aspirin to r/o bleed) Crescendo TIA’s - A&E
If TIA occurred over 7 days ago, can be referred and assessed within 7 days and therefore referral to TIA clinic is appropriate.
