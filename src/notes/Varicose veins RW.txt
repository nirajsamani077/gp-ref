Varicose veins RW

Common - 1/3 of population, 3% get venous ulcer 

Reflux (backflow) of deeper to superficial LL veins often due to chronic high pressure in deeper veins (worse when standing, or abdopelvic mass/pregnancy), associated with chronic changes like valve incompetence. 

Most common medial ankle/leg saphenous distribution - often itch heaviness cosmetic 

Complications of chronic venous insufficiency
• Venous ezcema
• Haemosiderin staining (dark)/atrophe blanche (light star shaped scars)
• Lipodermatosclerosis (hard, upside down champagne bottle due to tightened skin) 
• Superficial thrombophlebitis (hard painful lumps) 
• Venous ulcers (shallow medial gaiter area)
• Bleeding - direct pressure, elevate, ice packs, can be heavy bleed due to pressure so 999 

Check pulses & ABPI (as cannot use graduated compression stockings unless >0.9) & examine abdomen ?compressive mass 

Management 
• Lifestyle - WL, keep legs elevated, avoid prolonged standing, calf exercises to help flow
• Graduated compression stockings (grade 1 initially if pulses present, otherwise grade 2+ if ABPI checked), no stockings if referring (unless in pregnancy)
• Refer vascular if symptomatic, skin changes /thrombophlebitis & especially any venous ulcer
  - Duplex venous USS to confirm anatomy 
  - May consider endothelial ablation/laser, foam scleropathy (blocks vein) or surgical stripping/ligation 

Graduated compression stockings  
• Should always have ABPI prior (should be >0.8)
• Not used it referring to vascular (apart from preg)
• For symptomatic relief, Px max tolerated grade 
   - 1 (light 14-17mmHg) 
   - 2 (medium 18-24mmHg)
   - 3 (high 25-35mmHg)
 • Whole leg or below knee, made to measure by practice nurse or pharmacist, 2 pairs at a time (cost £120) one for wear one for wash, replenish 6 monthly
