Perioral dermatitis and impetigo PCDS RW

Periorificial dermatitis 
• Papulopustular, red nasolabial fold, aggravated by topical steroids so stop & usually 6w lymecycline 408mg OD or erythro 500mg BD (rather than topical abx)
• DDx impetigo, rosacea (telangectasia), seb derm & rarely lupus rash (butterfly sparing NL folds)
• = risks of topical steroids on face (as well as glaucoma cataracts skin thinning acne) so use lowest strength (1% HC) for shortest time (7d) & if not consider alts (protopic BD for facial ezcema or vit D analogues for facial psoriasis) 

Impetigo 
• More common in kids 
• Staph/strep superficial bacterial contagious infection , vesicles which rupture and exudate forms gold crust, lesions resolve without scarring within 2-3w 
• Non-bullous more common but bullous where vesicles so large become bullae (>1cm) which can rupture (golden crust), can become widespread and affect MM = PO Abx and consider admisssion 
• Only swab if worse after oral /topical Abx or recurrent (skin & nasal swab for MRSA) 
• DDx - herpes, perioral derm, bacterial resistance 

Rx 
• Mild non-bullous  - 1% hydrogen peroxide cream (Crystacide) TDS 7d or Fusidic acid/mupirocin if ?mrsa (topical Abx) esp if around eyes or failed HP
• Bullous/ IC/widespread - 7d PO flucloxacillin or clary/erythro & consider admission 
• Recurrent - nasal swab for Mrsa, clearance with nasal mupirocin BD 5d and whole body chlorhexidine wash, consider prolonged mrsa abx 
• Hand hygeine, avoid sharing, off school till all lesions crusted or 48h after starting abx
