Oesophageal CA (and gastric CA) RW

Very rare, aggressive, late presenting (5y S = 15%)
• SCC now declining upper 1/3 (smoking/etoh) 
• Adenocarcinoma rising lower 1/3 (Barrett’s >7mm metaplasia, H Pylori) same as gastric 

UGI 2WW - Dysphagia, upper abdo mass, >55 weight loss + pain/reflux 
Consider esp if progressive dysphagia (solids, then liquids), hoarseness/cough late signs 

Ix - FBC (if doesn’t meet 2WW criteria as non-urgent OGD criteria anything else/abnormal FBC), OGD + Bx then staging CTNTAP/PET to decide if curative or palliative Rx 

Rx - MDT decide based on stage & operative risks 
• Mucosal (crossover with high grade dysplastic Barrett’s) - endoscopic ablation 
• Locally advanced - oesophagectomy + chemo 
• Advanced- palliative stenting/brachytherapy DXT
• Future may be targeted immunotherapy (HER2)
• Often need PEG/jej feeding, nutritionist, post op risks (eg malabsorption/dumping syndrome) 

Support via CNS, McMillan nurses, CRUK website
Cancer care map for local services 

Gastric CA  
• More common in Asia/South America/Europe - main RF chronic H Pylori, smoking, ETOH, Lynch 
• Same 2WW as above - main ones abdo pain weight loss, Virchows node is rare 
• Treatment based on stage - primary surgery resection with neoadjuvant chemo if higher stage, but 50% have incurable/mets at diagnosis so palliative stent/bypass/DXT etc 
• Secondary care will do surveillance OGD CT but monitor for haematinic & calcium deficiency esp B12 post gastrectomy .
