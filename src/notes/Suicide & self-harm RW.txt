Suicide & self-harm RW 

“Are there times where you’ve thought about or have hurt or harmed your self - things like cutting or overdosing on tablets - or even ending your own life” - explore anything said fully /empathetically and link the initial question to something said or use “sometimes when people are very down etc”

DSH common (esp young female) & associated with significantly higher risk of completed suicide - higher risk for cutting than OD 
• In many cases the reason is to release overwhelming emotions rather than suicidal intent - ask about internet/social media influence/bullying 
• Risk assess each situation/case & don’t make assumptions regarding motivation 
• Refer all to CMHT if risk of repetition as early intervention can prevent suicide 
• Refer crisis team if high level of distress, violent method or current active suicidal thoughts/plans 
• Offer close follow-up within days & safety net fully with detailed safety plan & involvement of NOK/family support where able if consented 

Suicide - completed rare 10/100000 with highest risk middle aged isolated men, thoughts common 5% and attempts less common 0.5%, leading cause of death in young people 
• Asking about it doesn’t increase risk of it!
• RF - previous attempts, MH, drug, FHx, current adverse life events (relationship, finances, legal)
• PF - family, children, responsibility, religion 
• Risk assessment - vague fleeting thoughts of escape all the way to high intent high lethality advanced specific plans - avoid negative questioning (“you’ve not had?”) and explore any plans fully 
• Anybody with active suicidal thoughts or plan should be referred to crisis team or 999, kept in place of safety if imminent risk to self - if vague thoughts without plan risk assess and have low threshold to refer 
• Consider impact on family & on yourself 

Safety plan - find things that calm you eg breathing, taking a bath & remember your reasons for living (loved ones etc). Move to a safe space & try to interrupt thoughts but if crisis…

Crisis plan - if feeling unsafe speak to someone, loved one or friend, crisis team number and information for Samaritans contact number, if imminent risk then call 999. Link to Mind website & getselfhelp.co.uk .
