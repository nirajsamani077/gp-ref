Cerebral palsy RW

Generalised for neurological motor problems caused by brain damage in womb or during/just after birth 

High risk situations should be F/U by neonates til 2yo = Premi <28w, neonatal sepsis, HIE, maternal infection during pregnancy 

However if child has delayed motor milestones or abnormal examination (assymetry, low or high tone, feeding problems eg drooling) refer urgently to community paeds (to paeds neuro if focal neuro, no specific cerebral palsy RFs or FHx neurological) 

Delayed motor milestones 
• Can’t sit supported >8mo, unsupported >12mo, can’t walk & talk >18mo 
• Head control >6mo, rolling >9mo,crawling >12mo
• Hand/side preference <12mo 

MDT will support family/child & manage risks 
• MSK - pain, subluxation, spasticity, low BMD
   - Rx: splints, baclofen/botulinum, DEXA, PT
• Neuro - epilepsy, hearing/vision/speech, LD
   - Rx: annual RV, prophylactic keppra, schools 
• MH - mood, ASD/ADHD
   - Rx: should be under specialists as higher risks 
• GI - feeding/swallowing problems, constipation 
   - Rx: annual BMI, dietician, SALT
• Other - incontinence, resp failure 
   - Rx: refer specialists, chest PT, bladder training 

Many adults live with cerebral palsy with similar issues as above - constant annual review similar to LD or Downs with carer & consider social factors 
• Most common is spastic cerebral palsy where most can walk with or without aid but often deteriorate over time with other comps as above 
• Variable specialist services in UK - need to find work around solutions & continuity of care
