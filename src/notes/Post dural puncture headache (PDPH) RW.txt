Post dural puncture headache (PDPH) RW 

Following spinal/epidural anaesthetic (post-natal) can have slow CSF leak through defect in meninges - causing intracranial hypotension (low pressure headache) - occurs in 1% of blocks 

Usually within 3-5d of puncture - bilateral raised ICP-type headache (worse on bending/lying down/morning/coughing/straining) associated with mild neck stiffness/vomiting/photophobia 

Comps = SDH & CVST due to low slow state (both rare but significant) 

Refer all suspected into A&E for anaesthetic assessment & CT/MRI to R/O other causes eg actual bleed or CVST
• Most Rx supportive (bed rest, fluids, analgesia)
• Autologous blood patch (own blood collected then injected into epidural space to patch up the defect) for moderate-severe cases (70% success).
