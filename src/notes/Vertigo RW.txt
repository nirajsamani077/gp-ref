Vertigo RW

Is it vertigo  
• Lightheadedness - postural BP, arrhythmia etc 

Acute vestibular syndromes 
• Single sudden new episode doesn’t settle elderly neurology = POCS stroke ADMIT 
• Single prolonged episode with viral Hx = vestibular neuronitis (with SNHL) or labyrinthitis
• Consider ototoxics (gentamicin)

Episodic most common = BPPV Menieres vestibular migraine 
• BPPV - sudden onset with head movement resolved quickly normal between attacks, positive Dix Hallpike and Use Epley and Brandt Daroff ex. 
• Menieres - rapid onset lasts 20min-2h with tinnitus fluctuating SNHL aural fullness normal between attacks , refer ENT for diagnosis 
• Vestibular migraine - rapid onset lasts days may have hx of migraine, headache nausea photo phonophobia, Rx as migraine (stemitil!)

Gradual progressive + SNHL UL = acoustic neuroma/CPA tumour 2WW (rare 1 in 100k/year)

Long-standing episodic “unsteady” without spinning worse with visual distractors better with non visual distractors = Persistent postural perceptual dizziness (PPP), vestibular rehab and sedatives (stemitil) but don’t use long term 

Always check postural BP and HR, examine neuro for cerebellar signs (past pointing, nystagmus, DDK, tone)

Dix hallpike & Epley for BPPV 
• Sit up on couch, turn head 45deg, lie down rapidly head supported below flat, hold position ask vertigo check rotational nystagmus, slowly sit up recover and same on other side 
• Epley for treatment (dislodges crystal in SC canal), wait for nystagmus to clear, turn head back neutral, roll body all the way to other side and turn head 90 so fully on other side now, wait then slowly up keeping control of head & chin to chest 
= successful 3/4 of time but recurs often, but 2nd Epley and home Epley improves almost all…if doesn’t work Brandt - Daroff exercises (very similar to start of DH exercises up and down fast in reps) 

Head impulse test ?central Vs peripheral 
• Hands on cheeks, fix on nose, quickly turn head to side whilst fixing on nose then slowly return to central a few times. 
• If positive = peripheral, as eye flicks away then corrective saccade back to nose on turning head away due to abnormal vestibulo-ocular reflux which would suggest vestibular path , so if negative is CENTRAL. 
• Also as part of HINTS = 
 1)Nystagmus - if unidirectional suggests peripheral, if bidirectional or vertical suggests central 
2) Test of skew - cover each eye sequentially while looking at nose, if uncovered eye moves up or diagnonally suggests central cause  

Menieres 
• Excess fluid in SC canals (BPPV crystals lodged in canals), ENT diagnose, no good treatment but vestibular rehab, hearing aids and psychological support. Betahistine licensed but doesn’t really work. Surgery can help with HL
