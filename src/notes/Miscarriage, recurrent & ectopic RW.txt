Miscarriage, recurrent & ectopic RW

Bleeding & pain in early pregnancy with positive urine pregnancy test 

Miscarriage - pregnancy loss <24w
• Sadly very common, 15% end in miscarriage & has significant physical/emotional consequences
• Types = threatened (bleeding, os closed), inevitable (bleeding, os open), incomplete (bleeding & POC, os open), complete (full POC, os closed), missed (fetal demise but no bleeding & closed os)
• Spotting/light bleeding in early pregnancy common & not associated with miscarriage, if confirmed viable IUP on scan reassuring!
• If positive pregnancy and light bleeding with no pain <6w from LMP (6/40)-expectant management 
 - Often too early, advise repeat pregnancy test in 7d (if neg will mean miscarriage), if worsening sx return 
• Otherwise refer to GAU /EPAU for management, especially if pain or heavy bleeding & if severe pain or concerns for ruptured ectopic = A&E 999
 - Will need USS & if previous miscarriage & viable IUP confirmed give vaginal micronised progesterone 400mg BD until 16/40
 - Different forms of managing confirmed miscarriage (inevitable, missed etc)
   • Expectant - not if risk haemorrhage/infection
   • Medical - oral mifepristone (ripens cx), then vaginal/oral misoprostol (contractions) after 48h
   • Surgical - evacuation by vacuum aspiration 
• Aftercare 
   - Repeat preg test after 3w, if still positive ?retained POC 
   - Signpost Tommys support, emotional support 
   - If 2+ miscarriages or mid-TM refer recurrent miscarriage clinic & give Sands information 
   - Can retry when emotionally ready, no increased risk on next cycle, explore lifestyle & folic acid etc 

Recurrent miscarriage (>=2, or mid trimester)
• Refer recurrent miscarriage clinic, do not need to be consecutive (some definitions >=3)
• Provides support & investigates UC 
   - Antiphospholipid screen, TFT/TPO Abs in all 
   - Thrombophilia screen in mid-trimester 
   - Pelvic USS
   - Cytogenetic analysis of POC (chromosomal) or parental karyotyping if no further miscarriage 
• Prognosis is generally excellent - 75% will have good future pregnancy outcome without progesterone even (better with)

Ectopic pregnancy 
• High index of suspicion of any WCBA abdo pain nausea vomiting or dizziness - do preg test!!!!!!!
• RF = PID, prior tubal surgery, smoking, IVF
• Hx = shoulder tip pain, dizziness 
• Ex = HR (may brady vagal), BP, tender abdo, cervical excitation 
• If unstable = 999 A&E and inform Gynae on-call 
• If stable = refer GAU/EPAU for progesterone, HCG (48h trend and 1500 to see IUP) & USS
• Management - usually surgical (laparoscopic salpingectomy) if other tube healthy otherwise salpingotomy, otherwise MTX (if stable, low HCG & no evidence of heterotopic pregnancy - both ectopic & IUP together, need F/U ++ bloods etc) or expectant if stable 
• Should be no difference in fertility unless already known subfertility - if MTX wait >3mo to conceive 

Other non pregnancy related causes of pain 
• Ovarian accident (cyst, haemorrhage, torsion)
• Fibroid red degeneration 
• OHSS (usually if IVF - needs admission)
• Appendicitis, pancreatitis, cholecystitis, UTI!.
