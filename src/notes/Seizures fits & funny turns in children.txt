Seizures fits & funny turns in children RW

Any LOC in child <12 needs same day/urgent paeds assessment 

DDx 
• Epilepsy, including infantile spasms 
• Febrile seizures/convulsions 
• Reflex anoxic seizures/ breath holding attack - Painful/emotional stimulus causes sudden loss of tone, then seizure, may lose continence, usually grow out of them, need to R/O cardiac abnormality
• BRUE in babies <1 - Apnea, change in tone/consciousness <1min spontaneously resolved (not a seizure), admit all for Ix 
• Non-neuro (eg cardiac or vagal syncope)
• Sleep myoclonus & tics - sudden involuntary movements 

Infantile spasms (West syndrome) 
• Usually first year of life, severe epilepsy with poor prognosis, occurs in 1/2000
• Subtle “Salaam” attacks of repetitive movements (head nodding, arms jerking, pulling knees up, more common after waking up - video them 
• If suspecting refer same day to paeds for neurologist assessment, EEG (hypsarrhythmia)
• Rx = vigabatrin, steroids 

Febrile seizures /convulsions   
• Most common childhood seizure, affects 5% most common between 6m-6y (peak 18mo)
• Based on Hx of fever >38 and GTC usually self resolving - 1/3 recur usually within a year but it’s not necessarily the rise in temp that causes (combo of genetic & environmental factors) - so no evidence that para would or could prevent 
• Check urine, listen chest, consider meningitis 
• Refer immediately to paeds if 
   - First episode (all) 
   - Clinical signs of sepsis or meningitis (unwell)
   - Complex seizure (focal, >10min, multiple)
   - Focal neuro or lack of recovery >1h (post ictal)
   - No obvious explanation for fever itself 
• If recurs = BLS, in safe place ensure airway surroundings clear, when stops put in recovery position, if lasts >5min give buccal midazolam/rectal diazepam & ambulance 
• Very low risk of future epilepsy - most do not, risks varies 2-8%, higher if complex or underlying neurodevelopmental concerns
