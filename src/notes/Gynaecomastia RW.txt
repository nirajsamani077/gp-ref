Gynaecomastia RW

True XS glandular tissue which can thicken/fibrose = Increased oestrogen:androgen ratio but common to see pseudogynaecomastia (fatty tissue) if raised BMI
• Glandular tissue = >2cm firm subareolar swelling 

Physiological
• Puberty 60% between 12-14yo settles <18mo (Ix)
• Older men >65 (due to testicular involution)

Pathological 
• Obesity by itself 
• Hypogonadism 
• Testicular tumour or ectopic oestrogen secreting 
• Hyperprolactinaemia/hyperthyroid 
• Liver /renal failure 
• Drugs - spiro, cimetidine, dig, finasteride, goserelin, oestrogens, steroids, cannabis

Hx - rapid onset more likely pathological, check other endocrine Sx, galactorrhoea, ED/libido, weight, etoh/anabolic steroids, FHx breast Ca 

Ex - Breast as above (consider 2WW male breast ca), testicles for tumour (3% of gynaecomastia have a tumour, 9% of tumour have gynaecomastia), stigmata CLD hypogonadism etc 

Bloods - FAI (morning testosterone, SHBG), Ue, LFT, TFT, +-  tumour markers (AFP, BHCG) & if low FAI/testosterone refer endocrinology + 
•LH/FSH, prolactin, oestradiol 
•USS testes for volume or if any lump/raised marks 

If treatment (usually wait >18mo puberty) if fibrosis hasn’t occurred = Tamoxifen off licence 10-20mg/d 3mo+ (95 % respond), especially good if related to anti androgens) or surgical management if fibrosis has occurred (>1y idiopathic)
