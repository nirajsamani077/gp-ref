Vasectomy 
• Failure rate 1:2000 - 10x better than female steri (1:20), usually day case under LA by urology or GPWSI to snip vas deferens (tube from epididymis which produces sperm to prostate seminal vesicles which store it & comes out as ejaculation) 
• Irreversible (or very difficult to reverse & can only be done privately) - ensure family definitely complete & discussed fully with partner with Alt options (LARCs etc) - good for both together clinic 
• Not immediate - need 2x semen analysis prior to UPSI after 12w post op - need good contraception until this (some men have persistent non motile sperm, special clearance if <100,000 of these)
• Comps - haematoma, infection, sperm granuloma, chronic testicular pain (up to 30%)
• Post op abstain strenuous activity/sex for 7d & wear tight supportive underwear continuous for 2d

Female sterilisation 
• LARCs are highly effective, reversible & safer than tying tubes (lap surgery) & have other benefits eg for HMB  - High risk of regret as above!
• Risk of surgery (injury bladder bowel vessels )
• Must have had neg pregnancy test & no UPSI in last 3w to have op, & continue oral contraception for at least 7d after (&wait to remove IUS >7d after) .
