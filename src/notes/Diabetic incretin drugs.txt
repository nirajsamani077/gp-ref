Diabetic incretin drugs 

GLP-1 mimetics (liraglutide, exenatide) 
- in T2DM oral glucose load causes less insulin release than IV load, due to lower levels of GLP-1 (an incretin) which is released by small intenstine (incretin effect is normally where oral causes more insulin release to bring sugar down)
- GLP-1 mimetics increase GLP-1 therefore reduce sugar level from oral glucose load 
- SC injection, exenatide BD 1h before meals, liraglutide OD. Exenatide has risk of severe pancreatitis.
- causes weight loss ++ so consider if BMI >35 or less if insulin unacceptable, good addition as triple therapy 
- aim for HbA1C drop >11 and 3% weight loss after 6 months 

DPP4 inhibitors (sitagliptin, alogliptin)
- inhibits peripheral breakdown of GLP-1 to increase incretin effect 
- well tolerated, no hypo or weight gain 
- good option in TZD failed/can’t be given
