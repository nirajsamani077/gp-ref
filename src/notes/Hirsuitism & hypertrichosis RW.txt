Hirsuitism & hypertrichosis RW

Hirsuitism = androgen dependent hair growth 
• PCOS (common), obesity (insulin resistance) 
• Idiopathic (together PCOS & iat = 95% of cases)
• Endocrine (Cushings, CAH, androgen therapy)
• Androgen secreting adrenal/ovarian tumour
• Drugs (steroids, phenytoin)

Assess with Ferriman-Gallwey score (https://bhns.org.uk/ccs_files/web_data/Resources/Diseases%20(severity%20scoring)/Hirsuitism.pdf) 

Concerning for tumour = recent onset /rapid virilisation 

If Rotterdam features of PCOS (irregular periods, androgenic, Uss PCO) then diagnose, otherwise 
• Morning testosterone + SHBG (FAI) - anyway
   - FAI = Total testosterone x100/SHBG 
   - FAI <3% normal, >5% suggests PCOS
   - Total testosterone <5 more likely PCOS, >5 consider androgen secreting tumour & refer 
• TFT, prolactin, 24h urinary cortisol, pelvic USS 
• 17-hydroxyprogesterone follicular morning as a test for non classical CAH (partial 21-hydroxylase deficiency) - refer if >5 

Management (won’t get full improvement, <50%)
• Cosmetic hair removal (waxing, shaving, laser)
• Depilatory cream - topical eflornithine (Vaniqa), only used if cosmetic & systemic options failed, but blacklisted as expensive & not so effective 
• Dianette (COCP with antiandrogen cocyprindiol) but ++ VTE risk so not long term <3mo max, Yasmin good alternative 
• Secondary care treatments (all teratogenic) - spironolactone, finasteride, metformin 

Certain genetic phenotype (olive skin, dark hair) means hair is more apparent & thick, shaving increases coarseness of hair but not growth

Hypertrichosis = non androgen dependent & much rarer/severe with excess growth whole body 
• Congenital (languinosa, terminalis)
• Porphyria cutanea tarda (PCT)
• Anorexia 
• Drugs (minoxidil, ciclosporin)
