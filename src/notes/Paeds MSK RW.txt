Paeds MSK RW

Red flags 
• Acute limb or acute septic /traumatic joint 
• Night pain or deep bone pain 
• Systemic - B Sx, LN, FTT
• Delayed/regressed milestones
• Weakness/neuro

6 serious conditions 
• Malignancy - bone or ST sarcoma or haem malignancy (ALL) get urgent access XR/USS/FBC within 48h and d/w paeds on call 
• Infection - refer all immediately to paeds IP
• NAI - SG referrals likely admission for this, any incongruent story/delayed Px/bruising in immobile child or suspicious bruising 
• Inflammatory Arthritis - JIA, more subtle, post viral reactive mimics, common single joint stiffness lack of trauma >6w, multiple subtypes and systemic signs (rash, nails, uveitis, LN, HSM, renal), refer rheum urgent 
• NM disorders - DMD, myosotis (proximal weakness , falls, gait, delayed milestones, myoglobinuria dark, check CK LDH and refer)
• Ortho subacute - SUFE (puberty, obese, reduced painful IR hip), Perthes (4-8yo boys) = refer XR same day 

Normal variants (consider vit d deficiency)
• Bow legs - refer if >3y, UL or worsening 
• Knock knees - refer if <3y, UL or worsening 
• Flat feet - all will be til 3y, if painful or not flexible (ie resolving on tip toes refer)
• In-toeing - almost all resolve by 8yo (3 causes metatarsus adductus curved inner foot, internal tibial torsion, femoral anteversion), refer if >8yo or not flexible 
• Out-toeing - usually first steps resolved by 2yo, if new older consider SUFE Perthes 
• Tip toeing - common as learning to walk, can be sign of developmental delay/spinal problem 

Growing pains criteria 
• 3-12yo symmetrical LL non joint pains 
• Never in morning after waking up 
• No limp no limitation normal exam and milestone
If Dx (DOE) then explain and safety net, reassess
