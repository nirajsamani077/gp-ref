Ovarian cyst RW

Common, benign, usually asymptomatic
Functional (cycle related) resolve within 3 cycles but pathological tend to remain and grow

TVUSS and CA125 only in non simple cysts (>5cm, BL, loculated, solid, symptomatic, ascites) esp in post menopausal (premenopausal simple cyst <5cm doesn’t need monitoring) & refer 

Physiological cysts - follicular, corpus luteal 
Pathological - endometriomas (choc cyst), dermoid cyst (teratoma contain any tissue), cystadenomas serious & mucinous. Consider malignant potential 

Secondary care do RMI calculation & MRI
Sx usually pelvic pain, bowel/urinary sx, bloating!
Acute can be cyst accident (bleed, torsion!) 
Do not CA125 a simple <5cm cyst pre menopausal 
If complex cyst <40 = HCG AFP LDH markers due to risk of germ cell tumour 

Usually if postmenopausal with cyst >5cm refer
• If <5cm and normal CA125 rescan 6mo & 1y if unchanged no further F/U 
• Usually refer (2WW if sinister features/ascites)

In premenopausal 
• Simple <5cm = no FU, 5-7cm rescan 1y, >7cm ref
• Indeterminate /haemorrhagic /endometrioma = rescan 6w, consider markers, then refer if persists
