Cellulitis RW

Very commonly misdiagnosed as this, more often venous stasis dermatitis - often leg, DDx 
• DVT, gout, septic joint, venous change, superficial thrombophlebitis, lymphedema 

Ways of differentiating 
• Bilateral cellulitis is rarely ever a thing 
• Specific dermal changes for venous ezcema (haemosiderin staining brownish, lipodermatosclerosis whitish, scaling crusting itch)
• Leg raise test - if erythema disappears more likely venous, if remains more likely cellulitis 
• Only swab if failed to respond initially or tropical 
• Assess NVI - pulses very important 
• Erysipelas = localised superficial staph infection 

Management  
• Flucloxacillin 500mg QDS 5-7d (1g QDS higher dose off label if severe or Hx PVD - Clary 500mg BD in PA 5-7d) + mark area & review @ 48h +/- 7 days to consider extending 
• Specific situations 
   - Facial cellulitis - Co-Amox preferred (or clary + metro if PA)
   - Lymphedema - Amox for 14d may have better tissue penetration & cover against strep 
• If severe pain, systemically unwell, comorbid (esp PVD DM) = refer hospital for IV ABX 

Recurrent cellulitis 
• If >2 episodes cellulitis same site in last year refer  derm to consider prophylactic ABX (pen V or erythro 250mg BD) - review at 6mo & consider stopping (similar to UTI prophylaxis) 
• Compression stockings & skin health 

Necrotising fasciitis (nec fasc) 
• Rare serious rapid SC tissue infection usually caused by GAS - progressss to gas gangrene/necrosis, rapid sepsis & multi-organ failure 
• If considering - immediate time sensitive admission, they use LRINEC score based on bloods & need surgical debridement & IV ABx
