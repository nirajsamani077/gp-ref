Epistaxis /nosebleeds RW

Lean forwards (blood runs out) pressure to soft part of nose 10mins, ice packs sometimes, if doesn’t stop call for help 
• Vasoconstrictors (oxymetolazone), cautery with silver nitrate, packing 

Recurrent nosebleeds 
• Red flags = NPSCC (UL, otalgia, CHL, eye, LN, smoker, Asian or North African), telangectasia (HHT, check mucosa and fingers, ask FHx), child (<2 ?SG, juvenile nasopharyngeal angiofibroma in young boys)
• Blood forwards one nostril = anterior local reassuring, blood back both nostrils = posterior needs nasendoscopy so always refer ENT

Check episodes, triggers (blowing nose, nose picking, cocaine, anticoag), first aid, FHx clotting disorder 
• check FBC +/clotting if concerns
• check if any bleeding point with nasal speculum 

Management 
• Self care - First aid, nasal precautions 24h after (no blowing or picking nose, no ETOH/hot drinks, no strain and stay upright) - ENT UK PIL epistaxis
• Naseptin 2 weeks to reduce crust & vestibulitis as an antiseptic (not in peanut allergy) - alt = Mupirocin or simple vaseline 
• Refer ENT if concerned recurrent etc 

Nasal injury = check for septal haematoma (BL boggy swelling of septum) needs immediate ENT drainage (risks septal necrosis and permanent deformity) - any suspicion for broken nose refer to ED esp if concerns for compound fracture - usually nasal precautions abx etc, if simple nasal fracture no deformity usually no need for ENT but generally if considering surgery need to refer to emergency clinic (rv within 7d to consider rhinoplasty) if deformity
