HMB & fibroids RW

Very common 30%, can be abnormal heavy or intermenstrual /unpredictable 

Structural causes - CAH-FAP
• Endometrial CA/Hyperplasia (PMB, IMB, higher risk with more oestrogen (obesity PCOS nullip tamoxifen)
• Fibroids - myometrial growth can bleed and distort cavity, benign but few can transform (leiyomyosarcoma) or degenerate in pregnancy which is painful 
• Adenomyosis - myometrial endometriosis 
• Polyp - endometrial mucosal outgrowths 

Non-structural - PICA
• PID
• Iatrogenic due to CuID or implant etc 
• Coagulopathy (>10%, VWD,  other bleed + FHx)
• Anovulatory cycles (excess oestrogen due to no ovulation, can occur extremes PCOS obesity, erratic bleeding )

Hx always ask PCB IMB (PMB if relevant). Discharge. Pain. Cycles. Urine/Bowel. Smears. STI. Surgeries/Gynae Hx 

Always FBC (sometimes ferritin, TFT, clotting)
If no other sx don’t have to examine, trial non hormonal (TXA) or hormonal (COCP/Mirena) first. Otherwise examine, STI swab and then TVUSS (only need if pain or enlarged womb) otherwise direct access hysteroscopy + Bx where treatment can be done 

PCB often cervical, IMB often endometrial or hormonal balance, PMB concern endometrial Ca

Non hormonal - TXA 1g TDS few days prior to period then max 4d of period, only when bleeding as helps blood to clot, no risk of VTE if used like this (+ mefenamic for pain)

Hormonal
• 1st line = Mirena. 
• 2nd line = COCP. 
• 3rd line = Progestagens (cyclical oral progestagens eg MPA or NET TDS from d5-26) or LARCs
• Refer for endometrial ablation /hysterectomy (cannot retain fertility) 

If fibroid >3cm /distorting cavity or suspecting adenomyosis/polyp usually gynae procedure (polypectomy, myomectomy, UAE, ablation, hysterectomy) - myo/UAE can preserve fertility 
• Esmya (Ullipristal) is a Prog receptor modulator which can reduce fibroid size as hormone sens but main risk = liver toxicity (check monthly initially and monitor, stop if ALT >3x ULN), restricted now to short bursts in secondary care 
• Ryeqo (oral GNRH antagonist) which reduces ovarian hormones so stops oestrogen sensitive fibroids , with add back HRT , now licensed use in secondary care 

In young/teens
• Most commonly anovulatory cycles (immature HPA axis) which eventually improves after menarche 
• Consider other PIC as structural causes rare 
• Usually can try treating without exam if no other symptoms (as above) 

Fibroid types 
• Submucosal “pushing in”
• Subserosal “pushing out”
• Intramural 
• Pedunculated (with tight neck)
Usually cause problems when >3cm distorting cavity and pressure on other structures (difficult IUS in distorted cavity) so other LARCS or OCPs
