Diarrhoea & vomiting in children RW

Check contacts, travel & notify PHE if outbreak 
If high fever, meningism, NBR, blood in stool, bilious vomiting or significant abdo tenderness/mass if is not gastroenteritis!

Stool MC&S if admitting systemically unwell, IC, blood or mucous in stool or persistent >7d 

Assess level of dehydration 
• Lethargy/irritable, reduced nappies
• Sunken eyes, dry MM, reduced skin turgor 
• Mottling /cold extremes or delayed CRT, tachy, iWOB = severe signs suggesting shock 
• Rehydration with ORS (Diarolyte 50ml/kg over 4h, continue breast/milk feeds & water, if can’t tolerate oral then admit NG or IVI if severe/shocked but risks electrolyte imbalance) - don’t give if not dehydrated unless high risk (>3 vomits, >6 diarrhoea or under 1/comorbid)
• Some evidence for ondansetron syrup, none for probiotics 

Don’t forget HUS (caused by E Coli EHEC/STEC Shiga producing toxin)
• Tried of haemolytic anaemia, thrombocytopenia & AKI (glomeruli clogged up by haemolysed RBC)
• Gastroenteritis + dysentery + pallor
• Admit, IVI, above on bloods, 50% need dialysis!

Usual time frame for recovery = 3d vomiting, 5-7d diarrhoea - make sure hand washing & off school till 48h clear!
