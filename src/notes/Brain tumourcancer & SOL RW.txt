Brain tumour/cancer & SOL RW

Difficult to spot, only 2% diagnosed via 2WW! 

Types 
• Gliomas (glioblastoma/astrocytoma) 60% 
• Meningiomas 30% usually benign slow-growing 
• Mets - half of cases of SOL, lung/breast usually

No single symptom has good PPV but best are seizures, weakness & confusion (usually acute admission) - otherwise Best combo of 
• Headache + other neuro 
• Cognitive + other neuro
• Combo of headache + cognitive 
• Always think about mets if previous Hx of cancer 

Headache ICP symptoms (although may not have)
• Worse on lying down or morning 
• Worse with coughing/sneezing 
• Vomiting or visual Sx without another cause 
• Associated neuro, cognitive or Hx of malignancy 

Brain tumours in children 
• Red flags = similar to above but RCPCH /RCGP has a Headsmart tool which can help for specific features in preschool (behaviour, head circumference/tilt, vomiting, balance) or school age (delayed puberty, recurrent headache) - https://bettersafethantumour.com
• If concerned same day (signs of raised ICP or unwell) admit, otherwise refer direct to paeds 2WW urgent or discuss with paediatrics on call 

Ix - MRI is imaging of choice. Glioblastoma is most common malignant primary brain tumour & is very aggressive (5% survival at 5y!) 

Rx - MDT depends on type/spread/patients (surgical, chemoradiotherapy, palliative). Consider seizure risk (prophylactic keppra), neurorehab, psychological effects & fertility post treatment 

Any signs of raised ICP = new headache, visual disturbance (diplopia, blurred, loss of fields)/papilledema & pulsatile tinnitus is emergency 
• Check BP (?malignant HTN) 
• Full neuro
• Fundoscopy for papilledema/haemorrhages (if unsure and concerning story refer), start on 0 and change dial to focus, ask pt to focus on object behind you “pretend I’m not here”, follow the “v” of the vessel branches which point to the disc, papilledema swollen disc lack of clears margins  & loss of normal venous pulsations 
• Refer same day admission for scan +/- LP.
