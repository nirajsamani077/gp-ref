Lyme disease & tick bites RW

Borrelia burgdoferi, spirochaete, through tick bites which used to be rare but in UK but now increasing 

Presentation 
• Erythema migrans - large red non itchy bullseye rash usually within 1mo of bite & pathognomonic (80% of Lyme) - if see this can stop there & offer treatment (no Ix needed) 
• General malaise/fatigue/headache/fever/MH
• Late Lyme inflammatory manifestations 
   - Neuro = CN7 palsy, meningoencephalitis
   - Cardiac = Peri/myocarditis, heart block 
   - Rheum = Large joint arthritis 

Rx = Doxy for 21d (Amox 2nd line, Azithro 3rd), Amox only licensed in children. Only Abx for acute infection rather than long term comps (which is why generalised sx difficult crossover CFS/fibro). 

If no erythema migrans = further tests 
• ELISA - if positive immoblot to confirm 
   - If ELISA negative repeat if done within 1mo of onset after further 1mo, but if sx persist despite negative ELISA >3mo immunoblot & specialist .
