Skin lesions 101 PCDS

ABCD of melanoma = 2WW 
• Assymetrical 
• Border irregular 
• Colour inconsistent 
• Diameter >6mm or Dimensions changing 

EFG for nodular melanoma & SCC= generally 2WW
• Elevated 
• Firm 
• Growth, persistent 

If an EFG is NOT an obvious papulonodular BCC then 2WW (DDx = pyogenic granulomas which are rapid vascular lesions bleed easily, need vaseline & occlusion). EFG can be papulonodular BCC but usually slow growing, rolled edge, but refer urgent if high risk area or appearance 

Other 2WW lesions 
• Non healing ulcers >3w, mucosal/subungal 

SebKs
• Greasy stuck on black brown keratotic lesions easily traumatised can fall off and get inflamed - often leave alone but minor surgery to remove or other others (eg cryo) 

Dermatofibromas 
• Red brown benign macule or papule with peripherally more pigment aged, when pinching sides can get central dimple and is a firm lump, but Bx if doubt 

Angiomas 
• Vascular coloured soft papules and nodules, consider referral 

Other flat pink lesions 
• AKs and Bowen, superficial BCC
• Benign melanocytic naevi (moles), but forget hypomelanocytic melanoma!
