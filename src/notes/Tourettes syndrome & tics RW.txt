Tourettes syndrome & tics RW

1% of UK school children (onset always in childhood) & poorly understood 
• Transient tic disorder = <1y
• Persistent tic disorder = >1y motor or vocal tics 
• Tourette’s = >1y , >2 motor & and >1 vocal tics 

Tics = involuntary sudden rapid recurrent non rheum of motor movements or vocal sounds 
• Simple = 1 muscle group (head twitch,click/grunt)
• Complex = fully formed gestures 
   - Echolalia = repeating, Coprolalia = swearing 
 
DDx tics = Myoclonic jerk, dyskinesia, mannerisms associated with ASD (rocking, thumb sucking, head banging, nail biting, hair pulling, teeth grinding)

In Tourette’s - tics can vary (worse with stress, relieved when concentrating on something else) & often get mounting inner tension before tic which is relieved by doing the tic!
• MH issues, OCD, ASD commonly co-exist along spectum of neurodivergence
• Diagnoses by CAMHS /neurologist (if dyskinesia suspected eg chorea ataxia )

Management 
• Mainly behavioural (habit reversal training to modify response to building inner tension)- involves identify warning sign & replacing with different response or distraction
• CBT in co-existent MH
• Drugs - only offer atypical antipsychotic or clonidine under specialist if severe (last resort deep brain stimulation like for PD)

Signpost Tourette’s Action .
