Headache (non-migraine) algorithm RW

Red flags = refer immediately A&E, SDEC or urgent access CT or MRI if ?SOL
• Thunder-clap sudden onset, neuro/altered GCS, meningism with fever, head injury = Bleed, SAH, dissection, meningitis, CVST 
• Worse on bending, coughing/sneezing, morning or IC/precious CA = ?SOL 
• Symptoms suggesting GCA or AACG

If not red flags keep headache diary 8w and 4 main causes 
• Tension headache - BL tight band 30min+ no other sx but worse with routine activities, can be episodic or chronic 
• Migraine -UL/BL throbbing 4-72h with aura, phonophotophobia, NV, have to stop activity 
• Cluster headache - UL only around eye sudden severe 15min-3h with UP ANS red watery eye sweating runny/blocked nose, ptosis/miosis - needs neuroimaging as part of Dx 
• Medication overuse headache (MOH) - variable, frequent use of acute Rx like para NSAID opiate triptan >3mo for >1/2d of month 

Tension Rx - para or NSAID, no opiates, stress management, acupuncture may help (10 sessions over 8w) 

Cluster Rx - acute = SC/nasal triptan, 100% O2 (no tension Rx), prevention = verapamil under specialist (other autonomic trigeminal cephalgias may use indomethacin), always need neuroimaging , may use GammaCore vagus nerve stimulation if not responding 

MOH Rx - prevention but stop overused drugs usually abruptly (unless high doses of strong opiates) for 1mo, will get worse before getting better, review periodically 1mo-2mo using alternatives, most will improve within 2-4w
