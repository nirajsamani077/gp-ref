Splenomegaly 

Massive (>8cm) haem or infective = Myeloproliferative (CML, myelofibrosis), Malaria (+ other tropical infections eg visceral Leishmaniasis) i

Moderate (>4cm) = As above haem with lymphoproliferative/ITP also, Infiltration (amyloid, sarcoid, Gauchers, Feltys)

Tip (<4cm) = As above + portal HTN, haemolytic anaemia (eg hereditary spherocytosis) and other infections (EBV, IE, hepatitis) 

So think cause 
Haem - MP massive, LP, ITP, spherocytosis (HA)
Infective - Malaria massive, Leish, EBV, IE, hepatitis 
Infiltrative - amyloid, sarcoid, Gaucher, Felty (RA)
Portal HTN 

LUQ mass moves inferomedially with inspiration, dull to percussion, has notch, cannot get above or ballot (unlike kidney)

Causes of HSM = all of above essentially esp myelo/lymphoprolferative, portal HTN or infective cause 

Check for rheum/IE hands, CLD signs, LN, HM 

USS abdo & bloods 
?Haem - FBC/film, staging CT, BMAT, LN Bx
?infective - thick and thin films, viral serology 

Splenectomy - indication = trauma/rupture or massive size esp due to ITP/spherocytosis 
Work up = Encaps vacc 2w prior, pen V lifelong and medic alert bracelet
