Lung CA RW 

2WW /Urgent CXR with 2w criteria 
• >40 with haemoptysis or CXR suggestive = 2WW 
• >40 & 1 symptom if smoker/asbestos or 2 symptoms if not for Urgent CXR (within 2w) 
 - Resp/gen sx = Cough, SOB, CP, weight loss etc 
 - Resp/gen ex = Recurrent LRTI/signs, LN,clubbing 
 - Abnormal FBC (ie raised plts /thrombocytosis)

Interestingly haemoptysis only in 5% of diagnosed lung CA (best were cough & SOB) so consider as possible Dx of ILD COPD may mask 

Rarer HP effects 
• Hoarseness due to RLN palsy 
• Horners syndrome from apical tumour (miosis, partial ptosis, anhydrosis, shoulder pain)
• HPOA/clubbing of fingers 
• Paraneoplastic syndrome (SIADH low Na in SCC) 

Smoking ++++ risk (90% of lung Ca) but rising number of non smokers - link with gene mutations (EGFR, BRAF) & targets for immunotherapy 
• If treated & in remission stopping smoking reduces recurrence & success of treatment!

Ix
• CXR although has low pick up & misses 20% of cancers so if concerned get direct access CT thorax or 2WW
   - FBC if doesn’t meet top criteria as thrombocytosis >40 warrants referral urgent CXR
   - If less concern - spiro, PPI trial silent reflux, routine resp referral & consider repeat CXR
• Secondary care will arrange staging CT NTAP/PET, biopsy (bronchoscopic or VATS) for immunogenetics & tissue Dx which split into
  - Small cell (worst prog but most chemosensitive)
  - Non small cell (SCC, adenocarcinoma,large cell)

Treatment (MDT, depends stage, operative fitness)
• Localised = Lobectomy (open or VATS) or DXT
• Locally advanced (nodal involvement) = Surgery & neoadjuvant chemo /immunotherapy esp SCLC
• Metastatic 
   - SCLC = Palliative chemo/DXT/pleurodesis/stent
   - NSCLC = Immunotherapy in some, palliative 

Signpost McMillan cancer support & CRUK website, Cancer care map local services 

Low-dose CT thorax for smokers may be used to screen for early lung CA (already implemented in USA) - not yet completely approved in UK, issue surrounding correct coding of smokers & incidental coronary artery calcification (CTCA).
