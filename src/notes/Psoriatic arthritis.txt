Psoriatic arthritis 

10-20% of skin psoriasis, can occur before rash 
Seronegative

5 subtypes 
• symmetrical polyarthritis (like RA)
• asymmetrical oligoarthritis (like reactive)
• DIP 
• sacroilitis
• arthritis mutilans (severe telescoping fingers) 

Periarticular changes
- entethesis - inflamed tendon/ligament insertion site (E.g Achilles tendinitis)
- dactylitis (sausage digits)
- tenosynovitis (usually flexors of hand)

Nail changes - pitting, onycholysis, subungal hyperkeratosis

X-ray - pencil in cup, periositits, erosion

Management 
- mild = NSAID
- mod/severe = MTX, new biologicals (ustekinumab/secukinumab)
- better prognosis than RA
