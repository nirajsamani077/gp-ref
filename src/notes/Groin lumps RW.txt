Groin lumps RW

Most common causes = Inguinal hernia & LN (malignant or infection including STI)
Other causes = Undescended testicle & vascular 

Hx 
• Painful? - Infective LN often painful, malignant often painless 
• Reducible? - Strangulated/incarcerated hernia, cough impulse, manually reducible?
• B symptoms - fever, night sweats, WL
• STI RFs - HIV/syphilis/HSV risk, LGV (chlamydia), any ulcers on the skin, travel/sexual Hx 

Ex 
• Inguinal hernia = superomedial to pubic tubercle vs Femoral hernia inferolateral to pubic tubercle (more lateral and lower)
• Check cough impulse, standing then sitting, reducible? 
• Examine genitals any ulcers, both testes down?

Hernias 
• 95% Inguinal, 95% Male - Female femoral more likely to strangulate (refer any woman with hernia, refer man unless minimal sx or doesn’t want repair)
• If tender/irreducible to A&E

Vascular 
• Saphena varix (saphenous vein varicosity, located next to femoral IL to PT, disappears when lies down, soft compressible) - refer if unclear Dx
• Femoral aneurysm/pseudoaneursym (rare serious pulsatile associated with previous angio or IVDU- refer to A&E 

LN (malignant or infective: leg cellulitis or STI…)
• HSV - BL tender LN with genital herpes ulcers/blisters, swab and Rx aciclovir & refer GUM
• Syphillis - BL non tender LN with chancre (single painless papule), secondary = generalised LN, macpap mucocutaeneous rash, systemic upset - refer GUM & offer syphillis serology 
• HIV - Glandular fever type illness, persistent generalised LN, refer GUM & offer HIV serology 
• LGV - Rare MSM in UK (heterosexual in tropical counties) chlamydia UL painful LN/abscess with proctocolitis - refer GUM 
• Malignancy - Skin Ca, Lymphoma/CLL (check other LNs, HSM, B symptoms) & urgent FBC & 2WW haem
