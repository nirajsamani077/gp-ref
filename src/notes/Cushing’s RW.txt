Cushing’s RW

Consider if weight gain/refractory DM HTN, muscle weakness, easy bruising, osteoporosis 
• Endogenous (adrenal cortisol producing tumour, ACTH producing pituitary tumour = “Cushings disease”, ectopic ACTH tumour) = ask mass effect specific Qs (headache, vision) & refer endocrine (MRI, surgical excision) 
• Exogenous (steroids) = slowly reducing dose, may need steroid sparing agents under specialist 

Specific features on exam 
• Moon face/facial plethora 
• Easy bruising, thin skin 
• Purple striae
• Proximal myopathy 

Investigating ?endogenous Cushings = refer +
• 24h urinary cortisol (if eGFR normal) - need to do 2-3x and high false positive & negative rate 
• Overnight dexamethasone suppression test (usually done by specialist) - 1mg dex 2300 then cortisol 0900 - if <50 ruled out Cushings 
• MRI pituitary /adrenals under specialist 

Comps - resistant HTN (with Hypokalaemia)/DM, osteoporosis, infection risk 

NB - any adrenal “incidentaloma” picked up on scans should be referred to endocrine for MDT & check for functioning/hormonal sx, if lipid rich <4cm non functioning will likely d/c, otherwise surveillance
