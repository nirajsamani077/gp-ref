Acute severe asthma exacerbation management RW

Salbutamol via spacer up to 10 puffs (one at a time, 5 normal tidal breaths) - if not improving (can’t speak) - call 999 - a good tactic for all

In adults 
• Severe = PEFR <50% predicted/best, can’t speak in full sentences, HR >110, RR >25
• Life threatening = PEFR <33%, sats <92%, silent chest, HD unstable, low GCS 

Rx = 
• 10 puffs or salbutamol neb 2.5-5mg if hypoxic  & call 999 if no response, otherwise follow a wheeze plan (10 puffs every 4h initially stretch down dose or time to PRN) 
• Pred 40mg for 5d (can consider quadding up ICS dose for 7-14d but often difficult), if PEFR <75% (ie moderate exac) steroids usually needed 
• ABx only if bacterial features
• Asthma PAAP via Asthma UK
• Asthma nurse RV in 2w (or 2d if admitted) ?need step up of regular Rx 

Wheeze plan 
https://ruh.nhs.uk/patients/patient_information/EMD002_Adult_Asthma_Discharge_Management_Plan.pdf

PAAP

In children 
• Severe = Similar but sats <92%, if >5 = RR >30 HR >125, if 2-5 (usually VIW) RR >40 or HR >140
• Life threatening = as above 
Rx = 
• 10 puffs or neb as above, reassess 15mins, call 999, otherwise if not admitting follow wheeze plan
• Pred 20mg <5, 30mg >5 for 3-5d
• Abx only if bacterial , PAAP, and f/u as above
• Refer if >2 attacks in a year
