Neuroendocrine tumours (NET) RW 

Rare but increasing prevalence - consider if unexplained GI or resp symptoms usually in small bowel/pancreas/lung but categories 
• Non-functional - no hormones,mass effects/mets 
• Carcinoid (5-HT) - flushing, diarrhoea, wheeze, palpitations 
• Zollinger-Ellison (gastrin) - resistant GORD 
• Phaeo & Cushings also (adrenal)

No specific 2WW referral pathway for these malignancies so 2WW non specific sx explicitly saying to rule out neuroendocrine tumour or urgent A&G endocrine/gen med - usually referred onwards to tertiary centre if suspicious 

Rx 
• Resection, ablation, embolisation, radionuclide Tx 
• Octreotide (somatostatin analogue) monthly injections reduce hormone (esp 5-HT) secretion but can cause loss of pancreatic exocrine function .
