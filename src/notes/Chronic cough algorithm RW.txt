Chronic cough algorithm RW

Persistent cough >8w, impacts QOL sleep PU 

Red flags = Haemoptysis, weight loss, SOB, chest pain, dysphagia, hoarseness, smoking /asbestos 

2WW/urgent CXR within 2w criteria 
• >40 with haemoptysis or CXR suggestive = 2WW 
• >40 & 1 symptom if smoker/asbestos or 2 symptoms if not for Urgent CXR (within 2w) 
 - Resp/gen sx = Cough, SOB, CP, weight loss etc 
 - Resp/gen ex = Recurrent LRTI/signs, LN,clubbing 
 - Abnormal FBC (ie raised plts /thrombocytosis)

Most common causes = Asthma, Reflux, PND, ACE 
• Triggers, variation, FHx, heartburn, nasal Sx 

Major causes = Lung Ca, IPF, COPD, chronic infection (usually cough with LRTI better by 6w, max 3mo), bronchiectasis, TB or pertussis (whooping cough ?paroxysms unimmunised)

Idiopathic = cough hypersensitivity (neuropathic?)
 
Should all really have CXR, spirometry & bloods (raised plts suspicious for lung CA, eosinophils?)

Treat most likely underlying Dx in turn (nasal steroids, antihistamines, PPI, trial SABA or LABA/ICS trial)

In children chronic cough >4w, main Q is ?asthma 
• If isolated with no wheeze, sob or FHx = unlikely 
• Otherwise low dose ICS/SABA prn trial as per pre school wheeze + PEFR diary 
• If wet cough, clubbing, FTT or recurrent LRTI = refer paeds to consider protracted bacterial bronchitis vs bronchiectasis/CF (work up = CXR, sputum, sweat test, Igs etc) 
• If no asthma & no wet concerns = observe 8w .
