Heart failure (HFREF, HFPEF) RW

BEAT-HF campaign - Breathless, Exhausted, Ankle Swelling = Time for a simple blood test (BNP)
• SOBOE, orthopnea, PND, ankle swelling, fatigue 

Major causes = IHD & HTN
• Other causes = Valve, AF, CM, congenital, infection (HIV, Lyme, myocarditis) 
• Rare causes = HH, Freidrichs ataxia/muscular dystrophy, chemo (transtuzumab, doxorubicin)

Categories 
• HFREF = HF with reduced EF <40% (old = LVSD) most often caused by IHD/MI 
• HFPEF = Preserved EF >40% (old = diastolic failure), poor filling/relaxing of heart but contraction/emptying of blood intact, most often HTN cause with gradual remodelling, can progress to HFREF 
• Can have HFMREF = HF with mildly reduced EF 40-50% (mild systolic & diastolic dysfunction) 

Use NT-proBNP (more sensitive than BNP) 
• <400 = HF unlikely 
• 400-2000 = Echo within 6w with specialist MDT
• >2000 = Echo within 2w (poor prognosis) 
• False high BNP - COPD, PE, liver failure, CKD
• False low BNP - Afro-Caribbean, ACE/Bb/diuretic 

Check 
• Examination - BP, chest, overload, ?murmur 
• ECG & renal function/HbA1C/lipids + UACR 
• Consider CXR (CTR), spirometry ? DDx 

Management 
• MDT with important non-medical interventions = 
   - Cardiac rehab (exercise/education programme)
   - Smoking cessation 
   - Annual flu and one off pneumococcal jabs 
   - Malnutrition & depression screening (common)
   - Preventing admission (self weighing, FR <1.5L)
   - Advanced care planning (RESPECT form) 
• ABCDEF of drugs for HFREF 
ACE/ARB 
• For all, start low & go slow to max tolerated dose
   - Ramipril 10mg, Lisinopril/Enalapril 20-35/40mg 
   - Losartan 150mg, Candesartan 32mg 
• Check U&E 1-2w after starting/increasing dose
• If not tolerating /Afro Caribbean = hydralazine + nitrate (ISDN) under specialist 
• ARNI (entresto: sacubitril/valsartan) - ARB + Nephrolysin inhibitor new drug under specialist if still symptomatic on max dose ACE/ARB (instead, start after washout period). Specific criteria 
• Specialist may combine ACE & ARB (not for us!)

Beta-blocker (Bb) licensed for HF 
• For all, start low & go slow to max tolerated dose
   - Bisoprolol 10mg, Carvedilol 50mg 
• Check BP & HR after each increase in dose 
• Caution (not C/I) in COPD, PVD, ED, DM 

Check K & add Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist (spironoloctone/epleronone) if still Sx
• Spironolactone 25-50mg 
• Check K initially (<5) & 1-2w after increasing dose (esp in combo with ACE/ARB) - risk of hyperkalaemia +++
• Monitor 3 monthly U&E once in max dose and even closer if coexist CKD 

Dapagliflozin & Digooxin 
• Dapa 10mg OD licensed for HFREF with or without T2DM, start if still Sx (under specialist)
• Digoxin if severe/worsening HFREF despite other treatments (best if also slow AF sedentary)

Extra/Extended treatments (all under specialist) - specific criteria LVEF <35%, NYHA >2 (symptoms)
• Swap ACE /ARB max dose = ARNI (Entresto) 
• Swap Bb max dose = Ivabradine (if SR >75), better than Bb which also slows SA node to reduce myocardial demand bur Bb also reduces contractility which Ivabradine doesn’t 
• Cardiac resynchronisation therapy (CRT) or ICD if wide QRS on ECG 

Fluid overloaded?? - Furosemide for Sx only 
• Furosemide up to 80mg/d if fluid overload signs 
• No mortality benefit unlike other Rx above 
• Monitor U&E 1-2w post starting/increasing dose 
• Self weighing -gain of >1kg/d would need increasing of furosemide 

HFPEF /MREF treatment 
• Up to 50% of HF, similar mortality (50% 5y) & admission rate, but hard to diagnose (SOBOE, fluid overload ++, 1 in 5 have normal BNP as less LV wall stress), underlying factors inflamm obesity HTN AF
• On echo mild LVH, LA dilation, PHTN 
• No specific drug treatments or mortality benefit 
   - Rx underlying factors 
   - Furosemide for fluid overload /Sx - high doses
   - Dapa +/- ARNI recently approved (specialist)

Anaemia common in HF and worsens SOB/high output HF situation 
• Often IDA or ACD due to malnutrition, malabsorption and chronic inflammation 
• Correct often with ferrinject rather than EPO

DVLA only Notifiable if Group 1 NYHA 4 severe HF or group 2 (HGV) any NYHA (EF and symptom criteria to drive)
