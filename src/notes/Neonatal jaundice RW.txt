Neonatal jaundice RW

Early (<24h) is usually sepsis or haemolytic disease & needs immediate assessment septic screen FBC DAT 
• Rhesus/ABO incompatibility
• G6PDH, spherocytosis 

Mid (24h - 14d) is common & is usually physiological or breastmilk jaundice, may be other causes

Prolonged (>14d term or >21s premi) can still be breastmilk but more likely to be haemoysis, infection, biliary issue or congenital issue 
• Biliary atresia (check split BR, raised CBR needs surgical referral as conjugated - dark urine pale chalky stools other causes below to check but needs urgent referral)
• Galactosemia (urine reducing sugars)
• Congenital hypothyroidism (TFT)
• Congenitall infection (TORCH)

Usually common, transient and harmless - can continue breastfeeding, and encourage top up bottle & reg feeding 3 hourly 
If concerned check under natural light and use TC bilimeter if available & plot on bilirubin chart - discuss with paeds (often will be via CMW)

Rx = PTX single/double/quad lights with regular bili monitoring & if severe exchange transfusion - risk = kernicterus .
