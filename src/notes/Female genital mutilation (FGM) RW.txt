Female genital mutilation (FGM) RW

Ancient African/Middle East sometimes Asia traditional /cultural practice involving cutting or removal of parts of female genitalia “coming of age” = important SG issue, refusal to comply may lead to ostricisation, deeply entrenched sexual inequality = ILLEGAL & a form of child abuse/violence against women & is a dangerous practice with risks of infection pain etc 

Classes “cut or closed /infibulation”
1: cliteridectomy
2: + labia minora (excision) 
3: apposition of vaginal opening (infibulation)
4: all other (piercing, cauterising, pricking, tattoo)

“Known” cases where girl <18yo tells you she’s had FGM done or suspected FGM when examining should be reported to police (don’t examine only to look for FGM however)
• If immediate risk = 999, if not then 101 non emergency number 
• Otherwise if FGM suspected but not above, at risk of FGM or disclosed by 3rd party - refer local SG pathway - GMC duty to SG & explain legal position 

GP practices submit data on all FGM patients to HSCIC for enhanced dataset (legal requirement).
