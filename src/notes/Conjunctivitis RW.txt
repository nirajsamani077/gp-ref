Conjunctivitis RW

Usually bacterial rather than viral in children but hard to clinically distinguish (more likely if purulent Dc or coexisting OM) - but topical ABX (chloramphenicol) doesn’t really make much difference to either 
• Can return to nursery /school even without drops 
• Is available OTC so can obtain this way 
• Delayed prescribing can help unnecessary use 
• If using = 1% drops QDS if less severe, 2 hourly if more severe, use for 7d or at least 48h after better 

Viral common - hand hygeine avoid sharing towels & direct contact as contagious 

Neonatal conjunctivitis 
• Refer all due to risk to paeds ophthalmology - risk of chlamydia /STI - don’t swab or give Abx 
• Most often it’s actually nasolacrimal duct obstruction BL sticky eyes - without conjunctival redness/inflammation, should resolve within 1y of age (if persistent refer)
