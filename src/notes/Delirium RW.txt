Delirium RW

Common underdiagnosed, esp hypoactive compared with hyperactive = “Acute confusional state” New onset disturbance alertness/attention with disorientation which fluctuates & trigger 
• ICU BED HEAD - Infection, Constipation, Urinary retention, Bleed, Electrolye/sugar, Dehydration, Hypoxia/hypercapnia, Environment, Acute pain, Drug/ETOH (esp opiates BDZ Z-drug gabapentinoids anticholinergics psychotropics)

Validated assessment tool = 4AT
• Alertness (ask name & age)
   - Normal = 0, Mild sleepiness = 1, Abnormal = 4
• Attention (count months or 20-1 backward) 
   - >Half correct = 0, <Half = 1, Untestable = 2
• Acute/fluctuating - No = 0, Yes = 4
• AMT4 
  - No mistake = 0, 1 mistake = 1, >1 mistake = 2
Any score >0 suggests cognitive impairment & score >=4 suggests delirium 

Most delirium is admission unless refuses with capacity, clear cause to be treated & can be safely managed at home with close F/U

Managing distress/agitation - optimise environment (safe space, avoid overstimulation, familiar faces) & really no place for BDZ or antipsychotics unless at significant risk to self or others (usually admitted) - would be lowest possible dose (0.5mg) haloperidol IM or PO under specialist (can worsen PD/Lewy Body dementia so is more challenging, quietiapine or adjusting PD meds is best approach in this group) 

Follow up as delirium can be sign of undiagnosed cog impairment or dementia - debrief time and assessment a few weeks after episode should be arranged.
