Health promotion for healthy aging RW

3 trajectories for health - high level stable, low level stable (chronic disease), rapid decline (CVD, malignancy)

Basics in health promotion 
• Smoking - stopping has benefit at any age
• ETOH - screen AUDIT-C & support 
• Exercise - staying active very important, prevents falls sarcopenia, improves balance/social, consider seated exercise classes/Tai Chi & LIFE exercises or Wake up Work out videos on NHS website 
• Sarcopenia - Increases risk of frailty, falls but not inevitable, optimise strength/resistance & nutrition (unexplained weight loss not normal so Ix!)
• Sleep - Shift in circadian rhythm to sleep & wake earlier, arthritis nocturia & mood impact, avoid sleeping meds as increase risk of delirium/falls, focus on sleep hygeine /CBT-I and exposure to natural light in daytime 
• Vision/hearing/dental/podiatry - regular community checks, contributes to falls/nutrition .
