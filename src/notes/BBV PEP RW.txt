BBV PEP RW

Hep A - HNIG or HepA vaccine 

Hep B ~30%
- known responder = booster vaccine
- known non-responder or being vaccinated = booster/accelerated vaccine + HBIG (if HBsAg positive source only) 

Hep C ~ 3%
- monthly PCR, if seroconversion = IFN + ribavarin

HIV ~ 0.3%
- depends on whether exposure low risk (human bite) or high risk (needle , sex) & viral load of source 
- PEP start within 72h (ideally within 2h) for 4 weeks (usually tenofovir/emtricitabine + raltegravir), need BBV and syphillis screen 1st
- HIV testing at 12 weeks after finishing PEP
- Now pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) for people without HIV but undertaking high risk activity to prevent HIV transmission either regular or on demand - accessed via GUM 

Any PEP should be sorted by Umbrella/GUM/HIV specialist 

If a sharps injury / exposure 
• wash site soap water 
• Check index pt HIV status if positive unless Undetectable viral load 6mo on treatment will need PEP (do not need consent to test if in best interests but should get consent really)
