BCC PCDS RW

Slow growing, many have multiple most often head and neck but anywhere , sun exposure & IC 

Can be 
• Superficial BCC - most common, slowly expanding, fine raised whipcord edge, can get erosions and crust 
• Papulonodular BCC - papule or nodule with raised rolled shiny telangectaic edge which can eventually ulcerate and crust 
• Mixed BCC - mixture of the 2
• Infiltrative/ morphoeic - poorly defined on face, waxy patch can be depressed firm and extend deeper than anticipated so refer all suspected 

BCCs tend to bloody crust whereas AK/SCC spectrums tend to keratinise and scale

For superficial BCC options include 
• No treatment if asymptomatic and reduced life expectancy, sun screen 
• Topical Efudix of Aldara for 6 weeks to lesion and 4mm around it - immunomodulators, good for low risk not having surgery but lower cure rate, similar used in AK/genital warts, ensure sun screen ++ and can cause ++ skin reactions (1/4) and post treatment pigmentation change 
• Cryo or laser therapy 
• Surgical excision with 4mm margin 

For papulonodular BCC 
• Most need excision with 4mm margin, can be minor surgery (if confident low risk and confident trained clinician)
• High risk all need secondary care which includes young (<25), IC, Gorlins syndrome (multiple BCCs), head and neck, anatomical challenge, very large (>2cm), or poorly defined 

DDx for BCC - apart from AK/Bowens/SCC & melanoma 
• Keratocanthoma (benign but looks malignant)
• Amelanocytic melanoma (opposite to this)
• Dermatofibroma (benign firm nodule on limbs, may have central dimple when lateral pressure)
