Vaginal discharge (thrush, BV & STI) RW

Can be normal physiological discharge but change in discharge can suggest infection or pathology (thrush overdiagnosed, STI & BV underdiagnosed)
• Ask about bleeding, pain, dyspaerunia, itch 
• Already used OTC treatment for thrush may make non-thrush vulvovaginal dermatitis worse (rare bacterial desquamative inflammatory vaginitis  - refer GUM, needs 2% clindamycin cream) 

If symptoms suggestive of either of the 2 main non-STI’s = thrush or BV with no STI RFs then can treat empirically & safety net 
• Vaginal thrush (candidiasis) - often colonised so only treat if Sx, thick curdy white offensive, pH acidic (<4.5), pain itching soreness, satellite lesions - give oral flucnazole 150mg stat + clotrimazole pessary 500mg (repeat after 4d if severe), topical treatment not very effective & can irritate 
• Bacterial vaginosis (BV) - most common cause in WCBA, overgrowth of anaerobic bacteria & reduced protective lactobacilli sometimes related to excess douching, thin grey/white fishy offensive, pH alkali (>4.5), specific Amsel criteria if swabbing (don’t need to if uncomplicated typical) - give metronidazole 400mg BD 5-7d or 2g STAT(more expensive), vaginal metro/oral or vaginal clindamycin also available 

If history not typical of thrush or BV, or RFs for STI, examine external for dermatitis/herpes, spec to look for FB (tampon), obvious cervical lesion/cervicitis/ectropion & do HVS + STI endocervical charcoal swabs 
• If refusing ECS then offer self-taken LVS for NAAT (or first catch urine NAAT for men) which is actually better for most STIs 
• Can check pH using narrow range pH paper from Camlab to differentiate thrush (pH <4.5) from BV (pH >4.5) - not often done but could be useful!

The 4 common STIs causing vaginal discharge (all cause green frothy offensive d/c, need referral to GUM (Umbrella clinic) if suspecting for treatment, follow up, other STI screening, abstain until treated, contact tracing (last 6mo of partners) & partner notification
• Chlamydia - most common but often A/S (70%), treat doxy 100mg BD 7d (2nd line azithro 1g STAT then 500mg BD 2d), comps = PID (15% if untreated), risk of ectopic, infertility (tubal), reactive arthritis, perihepatitis (Fitz-Hugh-Curtis) 
• Gonorrhea - increasing, slightly more symptoms (50% A/S), treat Cef 1g IM STAT, can affect any mucosal sites (pharynx, conjunctiva, rectum) & comps similar to chlamydia but particular risk of arthritis & disseminated infection, if known resistance then cipro 500mg stat instead (counsel quinolone tendon rupture risk)
• Trichomonas (TV) - most common worldwide, 50% A/S, flagellated motile protozoan on wet mount (tested on NAAT from HVS/urine - get to lab quick, or endocervical charcoal from posterior fornix usually GUM), same treatment as BV (metro) 
• Mycoplasma genitalium - similar to chlamydia & rising with risk of PID, same tests (ECS gold standard), treat doxy 100mg BD 7d + azithro 1g STAT then 500mg 2d (ie strong chlamydia Rx!)

Don’t forget non-infective causes (FB, cervical ectropion/polyp & malignancy!) 

BV specifics (as most common in WBCA)
• Reduced protective lactobacilli, overgrowth of anaerobic - RF = douching, cunnilingus, IUD, STI (but it is not an STI)
• If typical sx (thin grey white fishy discharge) without STI RFs can treat empirically without swab (metro 400mg BD 5-7d or 2g STAT) & safety net 
• If complicated/recur confirm on HVS & specific wet mount microscopy showing clue cells & adding KOH causes fishy smell, vaginal pH <4.5 helpful too (Amsel criteria - speak with lab)
• Avoid douching/detergents/topical treatments 
• Recurrent BV = refer to GUM to confirm wet mounts but ABx prophylaxis (metro 400mg BD 3d @ start and end of period or metro 2g STAT once a month) 
• Pregnancy & BV risks recurrent miscarriage, metro is safe at 500mg dosing, treat even if A/S to ensure clearance (if BF use vaginal metro instead)
