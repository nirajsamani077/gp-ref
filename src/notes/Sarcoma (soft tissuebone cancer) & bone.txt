Sarcoma (soft tissue/bone cancer) & bone mets RW

Rare but critical to identify with
• Urgent direct access XR within 48h for any unexplained bone pain/swelling ?bone sarcoma 
• Urgent direct access USS within 48h for any unexplained growing soft tissue lump ?ST sarcoma 
• 2WW if suspicious imaging or ongoing concern to sarcoma specialists depending on location for MDT, Bx, staging CT, surgery/DXT 

Soft tissue sarcoma 
• Many types based on tissue affected (leiomyosarcoma = SM, rhabdomyosarcoma = skeletal muscle, liposarcoma = fat) 
• Easily missed as so rare - lipomas are 100x more common than liposarcoma
• Consider any lump suspicious if bigger than a golf ball, growing, deep to fascia (hides when muscle contracts), painful or recurrent = red flags 

Mets present with 1/2 of patients at diagnosis, very poor prognosis for lung mets (<10% 5y survival) 

Bone mets 
• 80% from breast lung prostate (sclerotic), consider also primary osteosarcoma as above or myeloma lytic lesions 
• Causes bone pain, usually well localised night pain but can vary (referred, radicular) & 1/2 of bone mets don’t cause any pain 
• Emergencies = pathological # & MSCC 
• Calcum & ALP may be raised but XR not great (need 50% cortical loss to see) so CT/MRI is best 
• If known cancer new bone pain depends on suspicion (high = severe night pain loss of function progressive >6w) = urgent AP lateral XR & bloods (ALP calcium) within 48h (speak to fracture clinic) & avoid weight bearing + d/w ortho or primary specialist involved 
• Treatment - walking aids to reduce weight bearing, sustained release morphine for cancer pain, DXT & IV zol would benefit almost all so refer for these, surgical fixation as last resort to prevent pathological #.
