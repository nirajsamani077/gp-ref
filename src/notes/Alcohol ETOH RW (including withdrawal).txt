Alcohol /ETOH RW (including withdrawal)

1/4 of UK adult population drinks alcohol at harmful or hazardous levels 
• UK CMO advises <14 units for male & female - avoiding binges by spreading equally over >3d/week & absolutely none in pregnancy!

1 unit = (to calculate units = ml x %ABV/1000)
• 1 shot of spirit (40% ABV)
• 1/2 a small glass of wine (12% ABV)
• Half pint of beer (4% ABV, 1/3 if 6% ABV)

Specific whole drink units 
• 1 pint or small glass of wine = 2-3 units 
• 1 large glass of wine 3-4 units 

Risks include many cancers, liver/pancreatic disease, CVD & MH/accidents caused by ETOH - previously thought low amounts cardioprotective but latest evidence is any benefit outweighed by risks eg weight gain 

Screening for harmful alcohol consumption 
• AUDIT-C = 3 initial screening questions 
   - How often do you drink in a year? 
   - How many drinks do you have each time?
   - How often do you have >6 drinks in one time?
• If AUDIT-C score >=5 complete full AUDIT 
   - AUDIT <7 = low risk, reassure 
   - AUDIT 8-15 = hazardous, brief intervention 
   - AUDIT 16-19 = harmful, ++ brief intervention 
   - AUDIT >20 = dependency, refer & withdrawal 

Specific ICD criteria for alcohol dependence 
• Compulsion, can’t control, can’t withdraw without significant effects 
• Prioritises over other things, persists despite being told about or experiencing harmful effects 

Other implications 
• If harmful or dependent at risk of thiamine deficiency (poor diet, malabsorption, higher body demands) & risk of Wernikes /Korsakoffs dementia so supplement with thiamine PO 100-200mg/d (vit B costrong no longer used as lack of evidence)
• If dependent then should notify DVLA & have to give up license, if harmful don’t need to but should say legal responsibility to not drink & drive, delayed effects, and if can’t control drinking should inform DVLA 
• If hazardous drinking + (AUDIT >7) or M >50 unit, F >35 unit - do LFT with GGT & FBC - even if normal LFT do USS liver anyway and ELF test (separate gold top) with referral for fibroscan if >9.8 (high risk of advanced fibrosis)  

Brief advice approach - your questionnaire shows you are drinking alcohol at a potentially harmful level, how do you feel about this? The use FRAMES
• Feedback - Amount definitely risky, link problem  
• Responsibility - Not easy but their responsibility 
• Advice - Cut down <14u/w, spread over >3d/w
• Menu - Practical advice, lower/non ETOH alts 
• Empathy - Understand it’s not easy but…
• Self-efficacy - Benefits will make life a lot easier!
Extended brief advice = motivational interviewing 

Support & treatment 
• Signpost self referral to CGL (Change Grow Live)
   - If needing medically assisted withdrawal or support (dependent AUDIT >20 or >14 units/DAY)
   - If wanting medications (first line = acamprosate which blocks cravings & naltrexone which blocks pleasure- both amber on formulary & significant risks kidney liver opiates etc so only specialist)
   - If needing more professional support (including alcohol focused psychological therapy/CBT
• Group support via AA, Drink Aware, SMART recovery 

Alcohol withdrawal - in hazardous or dependent 
• 6-12h - symptoms (tremor, sweaty, palpitations)
• 36h - seizures 
• 48-72h - delirium tremens /DT (coarse tremor, confusion, psychosis) which can be fatal 
• Wernicke’s- severe thiamine deficiency causing ophthalmoplegia, nystagmus, ataxia, confusion - if not treated leads to Korsakoffs dementia (amnesia & confabulation) 

Any seizures or DT = acute admission for BDZ reducing regimen & IV thiamine (prevents Wernickes) - consider underlying cause for stopping & other substances/BBV risk 
• If not admitting advise to never stop drinking suddenly & only gradually reduce no more than 1/4 every fortnight 

Abstinence can be checked by DVLA using carbohydrate deficient transferrin test - more specific than GGT but can be affected by other things - not one for GP .
