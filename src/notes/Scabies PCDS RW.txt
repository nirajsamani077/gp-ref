Scabies PCDS RW

Skin infestation by mite Sarcopetes scabei which burrow into skin, lay eggs, hatch into larvae causing delayed hypersensitivity response - easily spread by direct contact 

Generalised itchy maculopapular rash usually on trunk and limbs with burrows (linear lesions) where mites migrate, often in skin creases webs folds 

Treat 5% permethrin cream (Lyclear) x2 treatment 1w apart 
• Apply whole body to cool dry skin (not after bath)
• Keep on overnight for 12h before washing off  
• Usually 30-60g of cream needed per treatment 
• Treat household contacts x1 treatment same time
• Launder all clothes/bedding high heat 50deg +
• 2nd line = malathion (keep on 24h)
• Papules/nodules & itch persist up to 6w, use sedating antihistamine or 10% crotamiton cream (Eurax), only use topical steroid if definitely eradicated 

Crusted scabies less itchy and rare, in IC patients, scaly crusty rash (Rx = ivermectin)
