Pertussis (whooping cough) RW

Can get it despite immunisation or prev infection, although vaccine given + in pregnancy as neonates very vulnerable 

“Cough cough cough vomit” paroxysms
• Coryza + paroxysmal coughing bouts worse at night/feeding with inspiratory whoop (if vaccinated may not get this) & may end with vomiting
• During episodes can become centrally cyanosed/apneic/anoxic syncope/seizures & persistent coughing may cause SC haemorrhage
• 21d incubation period (resp droplet spread)
• Known as “The 100 day cough” as usually lasts around 3mo despite treatment 

Diagnosis = clinical but if suspecting notify PHE & • NPA/throat swab for PCR if within 21d 
• Pertussis serology to confirm if between 2-8w

Treatment - admit if infant <6mo otherwise 
• Oral macrolide (clary 7d or azithro 3d) only if within first 21d of cough (more to limit spread)
• Household contact may need prophylaxis (same as treatment dose and/or vaccination)
• Off school/nursery 48h after starting abx or for 21d after start of illness if no abx 

In pregnancy - pertussis vaccination (Boostrix) single dose offered around 28w (best protection before 32w) to protect neonate for first 2mo before formal vaccination schedule starts, give in every pregnancy .
