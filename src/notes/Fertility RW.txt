Fertility RW

If F<40 reg UPSI - 80% conceive in 1y (90% in 2y) 
• Higher if sex every 2-3d, reducing etoh 2 units, BMI <25 (losing weight) and stopping smoking 
• Lifestyle plays a big part, avoid timing intercourse, diet weight exercise smoking etoh, avoid heavy caffeine or tight underwear for men
• Ensure folic acid pregnacare 

Investigate and refer any couple >1y UPSIx, but any F>36 or underlying condition (PCOS/endo) refer sooner 

Women 
• D1 FSH/LH, D21 progesterone (7d before period) to check if ovulating or POF (<30 suggests not ovulating, check prolactin also) 
• Rubella serology (if neg vaccinate then only preg 1mo after), as poor preg outcome with Rubella 
• Other blds eg TFT only if clinical sx 
• STI (chlamydia) screen and Smear UTD
Upon referral will have HSG for tubal patency
If having IVF will need BBV serology 

Men 
• Semen analysis, use WHO criteria, repeat about 3mo or sooner if abnormal - don’t ejaculate 1w before then full sample + to lab within 1h
• If features of hypogonadism or cause (testicular issues eg varicocele, pituitary/hypothalamic signs, genetic eg Kleinfelter) - check USS testes and bloods for FSH/LH, testosterone (early morning fasted) + SHBG, PRL, chromosomal ?
• Hypogonadism = low libido, ED, loss muscle mass high fat mass, less body hair. Gynaecomastia, low testicular volume (<4cm) consider Kleinfelters 
• Re varicocele most men 2/3 do not have issue with fertility, can affect blood flow, refer urology if concerns for ?embolisation if poor sperm quality but evidence is that it doesn’t improve pregnancy 
• May need urology or endocrine ref if found 

Refer with these results of whilst awaiting 

4 main causes 
1. Ovulatory failure - PCOS, POF, PRL hypothalamic 
2. Tubal damage - PID
3. Uterine issues - Endometriosis, Ashermans 
4. Male factor - Oligospermia, hypogonad, surgical 
5. Many are unexplained 

Treatment depends on cause 
1. Ov - may try pulsed GNRH analogue or clomiphene to induce ovulation, IVF
2. Tub - tubal surgery or IVF
3. Ut - laparoscopy or IVF
4. MF - gonadotropins, IVF with ICSI/IUI/donor
5. Unexplained - IVF after 2y trying 

Eligible women <40 should have 3 full cycles IVF +/- ICSI (1 if >40) under NHS - generally single embryo transfer to prevent multiple preg 

ICSI = injecting sperm into egg then IVF whether mans or donors 
IUI = injecting sperm into uterus either mans/donor’s
