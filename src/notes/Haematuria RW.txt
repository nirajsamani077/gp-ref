Haematuria RW

2WW urology 
>45 visible haematuria without UTI (eg after Rx)
>60 non-visible haematuria without UTI + either 
• Dysuria 
• Raised WCC (check FBC)

Non-urgent urology - >60 persistent/recurrent UTI
Any visible haematuria needs urgent Ix 
Any haematuria best to recheck after treatment 

Main causes
• UTI or stone 
• Bladder /kidney /prostate or Gynae CA
• Renal - CA, GN, IgA nephropathy, PCKD

For non-visible haematuria - on dip >1+ significant 
If doesn’t meet urology 2WW guideline 
• Examine, BP, UACR, U&E & consider renal USS
• If any significantly abnormal refer to renal 
• If symptomatic and above normal refer urology 
• It asymptomatic and above normal AHR

Recurrent LUTS that’s not 2WW or haematuria 
• Women with frequency/urgency esp PM check CA125 + TV USS pelvis if raised 
• Men with classic obstructive LUTS or ED check PSA and DRE 
• Recurrent UTI = culture proven UTI >2 in 6 months or >3 in a year (if within 2w not a recurrence, a relapse)
• Low threshold to look beyond UTI if persistent
