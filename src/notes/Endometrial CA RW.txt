Endometrial CA RW

Rising incidence due to aging, obesity, tamoxifen 
• RFs unopposed oestrogen as above main factors (obesity ++ oestrogenic, tamoxifen ER antagonist in breast but ER agonist in endometrium - however isn’t used post menopausal in highest risk group as anastrozole, but hard to distinguish this) 
• Other related - DM, early menarche, late menopause, anovulatory cycles (PCOS) 
• Lynch syndrome (HPNCC) with colorectal Ca FHx 

2WW if 
• PMB (>1y post LMP, usually >55, not on HRT)
• On HRT with bleeding persisting >6w after stop (although common in 1st 6mo starting - work-up)

Direct access urgent TV USS pelvis 
• PM new discharge, visible haematuria or thrombocytosis (raised plts)

Direct access urgent OP hysteroscopy 
• Premenopausal IMB/irregular, or endometrial RFs
• Only arrange USS premenopausal if pain or ?endometriosis/fibroid on exam although probably useful to check ovaries 

TV USS endometrial thickness (ET)
• Premenopausal & ET isn’t helpful (varies)
• PM not on HRT = ET >4mm if symptomatic should be referred either urgent direct outpatient hysteroscopy or 2WW depending on endometrial RFs & symptoms (if PMB will obviously be 2WW)
• PM on HRT = if sequential ET >7mm, if continuous ET >5mm refer direct access outpatient hysteroscopy (if not meeting threshold either stop trial 6w or modify progestagen/add POP/Mirena as trial) 

Treatment depends on stage - usually surgical TAH BSO in early & LN dissection/omentectomy/brachytherapy/chemo for later stages with specialist FU at least 5y
• All with endometrial CA should be genetic tested for Lynch syndrome (often also get colorectal CA)
• Cannot have any form of HRT as it’s a hormone sensitive cancer so risk of recurrence 

Obviously oestrogen only HRT with a womb is unopposed oestrogen = much higher risk but continuous combined HRT actually reduces risk of endometrial CA by 1/3 - however if had treatment .
