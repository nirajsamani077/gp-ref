Alopecia PCDS RW

Distinguish scarring Vs non scarring

Non-scarring - no inflammation/atrophy 
• Alopecia areata (patchy) - well circumscribed totally bald smooth patch, normal or slightly red skin, exclamation mark hairs (short broken) on margins. No good treatment (sometimes betnovate scalp shampoo 3/12 if strong pt wishes with >50% loss) but 50% regrow by 1yr, 90% eventually. Camoflage. Alopecia UK website for support (other DDx patchy = trichotillosis, traction alopecia) 
• Telogen effluvium (diffuse) - profound diffuse hair loss due to stress, fever, iron def anaemia, thyroid, meds (check FBC ferritin TFT), typically regrows 
• Male pattern baldness - frontal triangular recession then vertex, consider Regaine (minoxidil foam 5%), finasteride 1mg for up to 1y or hair transplant (but none available NHS, only private)
• Female pattern baldness - diffuse centroparietal thinning (frontal spared), consider Regaine for women. If acne/hirsuitism etc check FAI & prolactin, consider PCOS or endocrinopathy (androgen excess) 

Scarring - inflammation, atrophy (shiny), hair tufts
• Tinea - scaly red, send skin scrapings and plucked d hairs for mycology, Rx = oral terbinafine Lamisil 250mg OD 4w to reduce risk of scarring alopecia (check LFT before) and household Ketoconazole (Nizoral) twice a week for 2w (only scarring one which is reversible!)
• Discoid lupus /scleroderma 
• Lichen planopilaris (scarring usually on margins) 
• Folliculitis decalvans (pustular, erosive)
Rx for all = Apart from tinea (scrapings) start dermovate scalp application and refer semi-urgent (within 6w) to consider intralesional steroids/retinoids/immunosuppression

Assessment for both 
• Check for regrowth 
• Ask triggers (stress, meds, diet) & impact QOL
• FHx of AI conditions 
• Bloods - FBC ferritin TFT at least in all & skin scrapings if ?scarring tinea 

Management specifics alopecia areata (common)
• Hair will usually regrow within a year 
• Treatments can trigger/assist regrowth but don’t cure and most will have repeat episodes 
• Sunscreen & signpost camouflage (wigs) which are available privately see Alopecia UK or under secondary care 
• Presence of regrowth & extent determine
   - Any regrowth or <50% loss = watchful waiting 
   - No regrowth & >50% loss = consider betnovate/dermovate scalp application 3mo (protopic as steroid sparer if facial) & referral 
• Referral to derm if uncertain, distress, no response, child, pregnant/BF, wanting wig for 
  - Intralesional steroids (triamcinolone)
  - Dithranol (induces low grade dermatitis) 
  - DMARDs/biologicals
