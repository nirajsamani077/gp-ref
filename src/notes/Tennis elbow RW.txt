Tennis elbow RW

Lateral epicondylitis but a tendinopathy - not a tendinitis - overuse leads microtears in common extensor origin at lateral epicondyle (tendons of the muscles that bend back the wrist and hand). - usually worse when doing these activities or stretching the tendons (opposite)

More manual workers then tennis players now 

DDx - Radial tunnel syndrome, OA lateral elbow 

Clinical Dx

Takes months to resolve, mainstay exercises
Stop doing aggravating movements
Eccentric loading exercise (wrist over edge of chair arms, with forearm and elbow supported weighted item flex extend slowly or UD/RD) - to lengthen and strengthen the tendon 
https://www.csp.org.uk/conditions/elbow-pain/video-exercises-elbow-pain
NSAIDs topical or oral 
Tennis elbow brace eg Masalo Cuff
Steroid injections no longer recommended (can help with physio recovery) 

Usually formal physio if failed self exercises 6w
Usually refer OTS for surgical excision after 6mo
Most recover regardless of what we do within months (max a year)
