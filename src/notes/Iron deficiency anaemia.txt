Iron deficiency anaemia

Ferritin <30 confirms but acute phase reactant (could still be deficient even ferritin <100) + microcytic anaemia 

Ix and Rx underlying cause E.g blood loss from menorrhagia/GI bleeding (esp malignancy)/urinary , Coeliac/malabsorption & diet (veg)
- don’t need to Ix further if clear cause in young woman with heavy periods and no GI sx or FHx of colorectal CA 

Increase iron in diet - meat (vegs often deficient), dark green veggies, bread fortified with iron, raisins/apricots

Oral iron supps @ 65mg daily dose of iron = 
• ferrous sulphate 200mg OD (up to TDS)
• ferrous fumarate 210mg OD (up to TDS)
• ferrous gluconate 900-1800mg/day 
On an empty stomach, at least 4h after thyroxine or calcium in milk (reduces absorption) 
Continue for until 3 months after deficiency resolved
Common dose dose related GI upset (especially constipation, black stools normal) , if not tolerated
• Reduce to alternate day dosing
• Try taking with or just after food instead 
• Laxative if constipated 
• Switch to alternative oral prep or ferric maltol (30mg BD for 3 months) 
• Consider referral for IV iron

Monitoring 
Recheck Hb in 1 month - should have increased by 20g/L in that time and if so recheck again after 3 months again and continue for 3 months from when iron and Hb in normal range before stopping (monitor FBC 3 monthly in first year, 6 monthly in second/third year) + consider ongoing prophylactic  dose oral iron if recurs/poor diet/malabsorption/menorrhagia 

But if not (inadequate response)
• Check if compliance and if GI issues address by above measures 
• Consider further Ix for underlying causes eg OGD/colonoscopy/Coeliac/H pylori 

In pre-menopausal women with menorrhagia or pregnant women can do trial of oral iron 1 month even if doubt/uncertain ferritin results (but in men or post menopausal women do not use diagnostic trial & exclude GI bleeding causes or Coeliac)
